## 🎓 Your BTS Degree in Germany

BTS (_Brevet de Technicien Supérieur_) is a 2-year post-secondary diploma. In Germany, it is generally recognized as equivalent to **Fachhochschulreife** (technical university entrance qualification) or in some evaluations to a **Berufsausbildung** (vocational qualification). It is **not** automatically equivalent to a full university bachelor's degree (Hochschulabschluss).

For Ausbildung purposes, this actually doesn't matter much — Ausbildung doesn't require a degree. What matters is:

- You have the BTS (shows academic capability)
- You have 1 year professional experience (rare for Ausbildung applicants)
- Your German is progressing (they will see the trajectory)

You can mention your BTS and note it is a recognized higher technical diploma. You do **not** need to get it officially evaluated (anabin/KMK) for Ausbildung — that matters more for university admission.

## 🛂 Visa — The Fast Track Option

You are likely eligible for the **Beschleunigtes Fachkräfteverfahren** (accelerated skilled worker procedure). This is the "express visa" you mentioned.

Here is exactly how it works:

1. **The employer initiates it**, not you. The company must agree to use this procedure. So once you have a confirmed Ausbildungsvertrag (training contract), you ask the company to apply through this process.
2. The company contacts the **Ausländerbehörde** (foreigners' office) in their city and pays a fee (~411€).
3. The Ausländerbehörde then coordinates everything — credential recognition, Federal Employment Agency (BA) approval — within **4 weeks** guaranteed by law.
4. You then get an appointment at the German embassy in Tunisia quickly, often within days instead of months.

**What you do on your side:**

- Prepare all documents in advance: passport, BTS diploma (with certified German or English translation), work certificate, motivation letter, proof of German language level (even an enrollment certificate from your language school counts), biometric photos, health insurance proof, proof of financing for first months.
- Contact the German embassy in Tunis proactively: [tunis.diplo.de](https://tunis.diplo.de/) — check their Visa section for Ausbildung/Beschäftigung.
- Ask your future employer explicitly: _"Können Sie das beschleunigte Fachkräfteverfahren für mich einleiten?"_
