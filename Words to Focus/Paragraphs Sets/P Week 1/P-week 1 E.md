# German in Everyday Life – Ten Connected Texts

---

Anna's daily routine begins every morning at six o'clock. She wakes up, showers quickly, and has breakfast in the kitchen. Anna is a teacher and works at a school in Berlin. Today she has an important meeting with her boss, so she is in a great hurry. Punctuality is very important to her — she never misses an appointment. Her schedule for the day is well organized: first the lessons, then a short break, then the conversation with her supervisor. She drinks her coffee quickly and leaves the apartment at half past six. Before she goes, she reminds her son Felix to do his homework. Anna feels fit and strong today — no stress, no delays.

---

Felix is twelve years old and goes to school. Every day he studies German, English, and Mathematics. He finds Mathematics difficult, but his teacher always explains the problems clearly. Today the class is reviewing a test on numbers — from zero to one hundred. Felix practiced a lot last night and feels well prepared. He asks his friend Tom: "Are you on time today?" Tom replies: "Yes, I'm not late today!" The exam starts at nine o'clock and lasts one hour. Felix passes the test successfully and receives a good grade. After class, the students discuss the problems with one another.

---

Anna's husband, Thomas, works as a software engineer in a large office. Every Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday he takes the train to work. The train station is next to the school where Anna teaches. Sometimes they travel home together by tram. Thomas earns well, but he often works overtime — which sometimes makes him tired and stressed. For three months he has been planning to discuss a pay raise. His supervisor is an experienced man who monitors the work of his colleagues. Thomas is very reliable and motivated — he completes all tasks on time. During his break, he often calls Anna and asks: "How are you?" She replies: "I'm great, thanks!"

---

On the weekend, the family goes shopping at the supermarket. Anna writes a list of all the ingredients for dinner. They buy fresh vegetables, meat for the main course, and dessert for Felix. The deals at the supermarket today are very good — many products are on sale. Thomas pays with his credit card at the checkout. He asks the cashier: "Is this item still available?" The cashier answers kindly: "Yes, but it's almost sold out." Anna always saves money when she shops — she compares prices and picks the most affordable option. She receives a receipt and puts it in her bag. The shopping trip takes about an hour.

---

At home, Anna cooks lunch. She peels the vegetables, chops them, and fries the meat in the pan. Felix helps her in the kitchen — he seasons the side dish and tastes the sauce. "Does it taste good?" Anna asks. "Delicious!" Felix answers, laughing. Thomas sets the table in the dining room and serves the water. The family eats together — it is their daily habit. After the meal, Thomas recommends a new recipe he found on the internet. "Next week we can bake this," he says. Anna prefers simple dishes, but she enjoys trying new things. Everyone relaxes after dinner on the sofa in the living room.

---

The family's apartment is spacious and cozy. In the living room, a large sofa stands next to the television, and the cat lies on the carpet. The bedroom is bright and modernly furnished — with a large bed and a wardrobe. Anna airs out the rooms every morning and cleans the bathroom every Saturday. They pay a moderate rent, because the landlord is very friendly. The utility costs — electricity, gas, and water — come once a month. In the basement they have their washing machine and many old things. The attic is dark, but Felix finds it very interesting. Thomas sometimes repairs small things in the apartment — he is very practical and creative.

---

In summer, the family plans a trip to Austria. Thomas books the tickets online — round trip by train. The vacation lasts two weeks, from the first to the fourteenth of August. They pack their suitcases and luggage a whole week in advance. Felix asks: "When do we leave?" Thomas answers: "On Monday at eight o'clock." They stay in a cozy hotel in Vienna. There they visit many historic buildings and explore the city on foot. Anna books an excursion into nature — into the greenery, where many trees and flowers grow and bloom. They almost miss the train home because Felix still wanted to go shopping! Fortunately, they arrive just in time.

---

The weather in Vienna in summer is often hot and sunny. Sometimes the sky is cloudy and it rains lightly. One afternoon there is a thunderstorm — lightning and thunder roll over the city. The family stays in the hotel and waits for the rain to stop. Felix looks out the window and watches the wind make the flowers wilt. "Is that thunder?" he asks. "Yes," says Thomas, "the storm is rough, but it will pass quickly." The next day the sun shines again, the temperature is warm and pleasant. They go to a park where many flowers bloom and children play. The nature in Vienna is truly beautiful.

---

After the vacation, Thomas feels sick. He has a fever, a cough, and a severe headache. Anna says: "You have to go to the doctor!" He goes to the hospital and the doctor examines him thoroughly. The diagnosis: a cold with a mild case of flu. The doctor prescribes him medication — two tablets a day, one in the morning and one in the evening. Thomas takes the medication regularly and recovers after a week. He sweats at night and feels weak, but he is slowly getting better. Anna cooks light meals for him and gives him plenty of water. "Are you allergic to this medication?" she asks. "No," he answers. After ten days he is fit and healthy again.

---

At the end of the month, the family sits together and discusses their finances. Anna shares that they have spent a lot of money this month. Thomas transfers the rent and pays the electricity bill. They plan to save more next month — fewer purchases, fewer expenses. Felix asks: "Can I order a new video game?" Thomas answers: "Maybe, if you do well on your exam!" The family communicates daily by phone — they send messages and call each other. Anna reminds Thomas: "Don't forget to call your brother back!" He replies: "No, I'll call him this evening." After the conversation, everyone relaxes — Felix plays, Thomas reads, and Anna plans the family's next daily routine.
