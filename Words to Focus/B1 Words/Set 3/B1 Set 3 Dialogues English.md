## Dialogue 1: At the Supermarket

**Anna** and **Markus** run into each other while shopping.

**Anna:** Good morning, Markus! How are you today? **Markus:** I'm doing well, thanks! I still have a lot to buy today. **Anna:** What are you buying? **Markus:** I'm buying bread, milk, and vegetables. The prices here are always reasonable. **Anna:** Yes, the prices here are really affordable. Yesterday I bought cheese for two euros! **Markus:** Two euros? That's very cheap! Where was the cheese? **Anna:** Over there, next to the checkout. It was reduced — fifty percent off! **Markus:** Great! I love saving money. Do you have a credit card or do you pay with cash? **Anna:** I usually pay with a credit card. I don't have any cash today. **Markus:** I withdrew money yesterday. I withdrew a hundred euros. **Anna:** I usually transfer money — it's easier for me. **Markus:** True. Look at this table of deals — everything is reduced! **Anna:** Oh, these tomatoes are free? No, I don't understand — how are they free? **Markus:** Not free! They only cost one euro. The price is very affordable. **Anna:** Ah, I see! And look — is that refrigerator over there sold out? **Markus:** No, the refrigerator isn't sold out, but it's very expensive. It costs five hundred euros! **Anna:** That's too expensive! I don't spend that much money. **Markus:** Me neither. I'd rather save for vacation. **Anna:** How much money do you save per month? **Markus:** I save two hundred euros a month. It's not much, but it's regular. **Anna:** That's smart! I spent too much last month. **Markus:** What did you spend the money on? **Anna:** On new clothes. They were expensive, but worthwhile — very good quality! **Markus:** I understand. Did you get a receipt? **Anna:** Yes, I still have the receipt. I might want to exchange one item. **Markus:** Okay! I'm heading to the checkout now. Bye, Anna! **Anna:** Bye, Markus! Happy shopping!

## Dialogue 2: At the Café

**Lena** and **Tom** are sitting in a café talking about money.

**Lena:** Hello, Tom! Nice to see you. How are you? **Tom:** I'm great! I have the day off today. And you? **Lena:** I'm not doing well. I'm stressed — my account has almost no money left. **Tom:** Oh no! Did you spend too much this week? **Lena:** Yes, I paid a lot for the apartment. The bill was very high. **Tom:** How high was the bill? **Lena:** Three hundred euros! The VAT was included too — it was expensive. **Tom:** That's really expensive. Do you earn enough money? **Lena:** I earn a thousand euros a month. It's not much for Munich. **Tom:** I earn more, but I also spend a lot. Yesterday I bought a new TV. **Lena:** What did the TV cost? **Tom:** It cost four hundred euros. But it was reduced — normally it costs six hundred! **Lena:** That was a great deal! Was the deal online or in the store? **Tom:** In the store. I paid with my credit card. **Lena:** I sometimes borrow money from my mom. She's very kind. **Tom:** Do you still owe her money? **Lena:** Yes, I owe her fifty euros. I'll transfer the money to her tomorrow. **Tom:** Are you a customer at Stadtbank? **Lena:** Yes, my account is there. And you? **Tom:** Me too. The transfer is free there — no charge for it! **Lena:** Really? That's very affordable! At my old bank the transfer was expensive. **Tom:** What would you like to order? I'm ordering a coffee. **Lena:** I'll have a coffee too, please. Who's paying today? **Tom:** I'll pay! You paid last time. **Lena:** Thank you, Tom! You're very kind. **Tom:** No problem! Good evening, Lena — relax a little! **Lena:** Yes, you're right. Bye!

## Dialogue 3: At the Doctor

**Mr. Fischer** visits **Dr. Müller** because of pain.

**Dr. Müller:** Good day, Mr. Fischer! How are you? **Mr. Fischer:** I'm not well, Doctor. I've had a bad headache for three days. **Dr. Müller:** For three days? That's not good. Do you also have a fever? **Mr. Fischer:** Yes, I think I have a fever. I feel weak and tired. **Dr. Müller:** Are you also coughing or sneezing a lot? **Mr. Fischer:** I'm coughing a little, but not much. I'm also sweating at night. **Dr. Müller:** I'll examine you now. Please breathe deeply. **Mr. Fischer:** _breathes_ Is everything okay? **Dr. Müller:** You have a cold, possibly the flu as well. I'll treat it with medication. **Mr. Fischer:** Is the medication expensive? My account is tight right now. **Dr. Müller:** The tablets cost about twenty euros. Do you have health insurance? **Mr. Fischer:** Yes, I do. Will I get a receipt for the cashier? **Dr. Müller:** Of course. You'll get the invoice and the receipt at the reception desk. **Mr. Fischer:** I'll pay with cash — is that okay? **Dr. Müller:** Yes, no problem. We also accept credit cards. **Mr. Fischer:** Good. I have cash with me today. How long will the illness last? **Dr. Müller:** Normally one week. You should sleep a lot and drink water. **Mr. Fischer:** Can I work? I'm a teacher and have school tomorrow. **Dr. Müller:** No, please don't! A cold is contagious. Stay home. **Mr. Fischer:** I understand. When should I take the tablets? **Dr. Müller:** Three times daily — morning, noon, and evening. Regularly, please! **Mr. Fischer:** And if the pain doesn't heal? **Dr. Müller:** Then come back next week. We'll take another look then. **Mr. Fischer:** Thank you, Dr. Müller. What do I owe you today? **Dr. Müller:** The bill comes to fifty euros. VAT is included. **Mr. Fischer:** Here are fifty euros. Goodbye! **Dr. Müller:** Get well soon, Mr. Fischer! Goodbye!

## Dialogue 4: Apartment Viewing

**Mr. Bauer** (landlord) and **Sophie** (tenant) view an apartment.

**Mr. Bauer:** Good day! You're Sophie Klein, right? **Sophie:** Yes, exactly! Good day, Mr. Bauer. How are you? **Mr. Bauer:** I'm doing well, thank you. Welcome — this is the apartment! **Sophie:** Oh, it's lovely! How many rooms does the apartment have? **Mr. Bauer:** Three rooms — a bedroom, a living room, and a dining room. **Sophie:** And the bathroom? Does it have a bathtub or just a shower? **Mr. Bauer:** It has both — a bathtub and a shower. Very comfortable! **Sophie:** Fantastic! What does the apartment cost per month? **Mr. Bauer:** The price is eight hundred euros. That's very affordable for this location. **Sophie:** Eight hundred euros… is that with or without VAT? **Mr. Bauer:** That's the total price — utilities included. No extra charge. **Sophie:** Very affordable! I compared apartments in the city center — they're much more expensive. **Mr. Bauer:** Yes, this apartment is truly a great deal. Many customers are interested. **Sophie:** Is the apartment still available? **Mr. Bauer:** Yes, it's still available. But only until Friday — then I'll decide. **Sophie:** I'd like to take it! How do I pay the rent? **Mr. Bauer:** By bank transfer to my account, monthly on the first day. **Sophie:** And the deposit? How much is the deposit? **Mr. Bauer:** The deposit is twice the rent — so sixteen hundred euros. **Sophie:** That's a lot of money. Can I pay the deposit in two installments? **Mr. Bauer:** Hmm, normally not. But you can transfer the first part and then… **Sophie:** I have the money in my account. I'll withdraw it tomorrow. **Mr. Bauer:** Great! I'll give you the invoice and a receipt for everything. **Sophie:** Perfect. I earn well — I'm a doctor. The account is stable. **Mr. Bauer:** Very nice! Do you have any more questions about the apartment? **Sophie:** Does the apartment have a balcony? **Mr. Bauer:** Yes! The balcony is large and has a beautiful view of the garden. **Sophie:** Wonderful! I'll take the apartment. Goodbye, Mr. Bauer! **Mr. Bauer:** Goodbye, Ms. Klein! I'm delighted!

## Dialogue 5: At the Restaurant

**Felix** and **Maria** are having dinner together at a restaurant.

**Felix:** Good evening, Maria! So glad you could come! **Maria:** Good evening, Felix! The restaurant is very nice — and expensive? **Felix:** Not that expensive! The prices are reasonable for such a good restaurant. **Maria:** Oh good! I don't have much cash with me today. **Felix:** No problem — I think they accept credit cards. **Waiter:** Good evening! What would you like to order? **Felix:** I'll order the steak, please. What does the steak cost today? **Waiter:** The steak costs twenty-five euros. A very good deal today! **Maria:** I'll order the pasta, please. Is the pasta also reduced? **Waiter:** Yes! The pasta is reduced today — only eighteen euros instead of twenty-five. **Maria:** Fantastic! And two glasses of water, please — free of charge? **Waiter:** Yes, water is free. I'll bring it right away. **Felix:** Thank you! Maria, did you spend a lot of money this week? **Maria:** Yes, unfortunately. I spent too much on clothes. The prices were expensive! **Felix:** I'm saving right now — I want to buy a new bicycle. **Maria:** What does the bicycle cost? **Felix:** It costs three hundred euros. But I got a discount — ten percent! **Maria:** That's worthwhile! A good bicycle at three hundred euros is affordable. **Felix:** Yes! I already have the money in my account — I'm buying it tomorrow. **Waiter:** Here is your food. Enjoy your meal! **Felix:** Thank you very much! **Maria:** It looks delicious! Felix, who's paying the bill today? **Felix:** I'm treating you! I'll pay the bill today. **Maria:** You're so kind! I'll pay next time. **Felix:** It's a deal! How's your pasta? **Maria:** It's great — very affordable for this quality! Your steak? **Felix:** Also very good. I'm satisfied with the price and the food. **Waiter:** Shall I bring you the bill? **Felix:** Yes, please. Will I also get a receipt? **Waiter:** Of course! The bill comes to forty-three euros including VAT. **Felix:** Here's my credit card. Thank you! **Maria:** A wonderful evening! Bye, Felix! **Felix:** Bye, Maria! See you next week!

## Dialogue 6: At the Office

**Manager Mr. Wagner** and **employee Clara** discuss the budget.

**Mr. Wagner:** Good morning, Clara! How are you? **Clara:** Good morning, Mr. Wagner! I'm doing well, thank you. And you? **Mr. Wagner:** Good too. Please come in — we need to talk about the budget. **Clara:** Of course. I've prepared all the invoices. **Mr. Wagner:** Good. What did we spend this month? **Clara:** We spent five thousand euros — more than planned. **Mr. Wagner:** That's too much! What did we spend so much money on? **Clara:** The client ordered new computers. We delivered them. **Mr. Wagner:** How much did the computers cost? **Clara:** They cost three thousand euros. I have the invoice here. **Mr. Wagner:** Did we get a receipt? **Clara:** Yes, I have the receipt in the office. Shall I get it? **Mr. Wagner:** Yes, please. Has the client paid yet? **Clara:** No, not yet. He owes us three thousand euros. **Mr. Wagner:** When will he transfer the money? **Clara:** He said he'll transfer it to our account next week. **Mr. Wagner:** That's too late! We need the money now. The account is tight. **Clara:** I can call him today. Shall I do that? **Mr. Wagner:** Yes, please do that. Don't offer him a discount — the price is fixed. **Clara:** Understood. And the new offer for the other client — shall I prepare it? **Mr. Wagner:** Yes, but make the price attractive. We want to win him over. **Clara:** Should I also withdraw cash from the account for small expenses? **Mr. Wagner:** No, use the company credit card. Cash isn't necessary. **Clara:** Okay. I also work here as an IT specialist — should I improve the system for transfers? **Mr. Wagner:** Good idea! That would be very valuable for the company. **Clara:** Perfect. I'll start on it after lunch. **Mr. Wagner:** Excellent. Goodbye, Clara! **Clara:** Goodbye, Mr. Wagner!

## Dialogue 7: Shopping for Clothes

**Nina** and her friend **Petra** are buying clothes.

**Nina:** Hello, Petra! Are you ready to go shopping? **Petra:** Yes! I'm feeling great today — I want to buy a lot! **Nina:** Me too, but I need to save. My account isn't very full. **Petra:** Look here — this jacket is reduced! Normally expensive, now affordable! **Nina:** Oh, what does it cost now? **Petra:** It costs sixty euros. The original price was a hundred euros — forty euros off! **Nina:** That's affordable! I'll buy it. Is it available in size medium? **Sales assistant:** Good day! Can I help you? **Nina:** Yes, please. I'd like to buy this jacket — in size medium, please. **Sales assistant:** One moment… unfortunately size medium is sold out. We only have large left. **Nina:** Oh no! When will it be available again? **Sales assistant:** We're getting new jackets delivered next week. I can order it for you. **Nina:** Yes, please order it for me! **Sales assistant:** Gladly! I'll need your name and phone number. **Nina:** My name is Nina Schulz. My phone number is… could you say that again please? **Sales assistant:** Your name and phone number, please. **Nina:** Ah! Nina Schulz, and my number is zero-one-seven-three-four-five. **Petra:** Nina, look here — this scarf is free with a purchase over eighty euros! **Nina:** Really? That's a great deal! I'll also buy a pair of trousers. **Petra:** What do the trousers cost? **Nina:** Twenty-five euros. So together eighty-five euros — then I get the scarf! **Petra:** Smart! Are you paying by credit card or cash? **Nina:** By credit card. I don't have any cash with me today. **Petra:** I withdrew twenty euros yesterday. I'll pay for my things with cash. **Nina:** I never overspend — I always watch the price! **Sales assistant:** Here is your receipt, Ms. Schulz. And your invoice for the account. **Nina:** Thank you! Bye! **Sales assistant:** Goodbye! Enjoy your purchases!

## Dialogue 8: Meeting Friends

**Jonas** and **Lukas** meet up after a long time.

**Jonas:** Hello, Lukas! How are you? I haven't seen you in a long time! **Lukas:** Jonas! I'm doing well! How are you? **Jonas:** I'm great! I have a new job now — I'm earning well! **Lukas:** Really? What do you do? **Jonas:** I'm an IT specialist. I work in a large office in the city. **Lukas:** That's fantastic! Do you earn a lot? **Jonas:** I earn three thousand euros a month. I'm very satisfied! **Lukas:** I'm still working as a waiter at the restaurant. The earnings are tight. **Jonas:** What do you earn as a waiter? **Lukas:** I earn one thousand five hundred euros. It's not much, but I save regularly. **Jonas:** That's smart! I'm saving too — for a car. Cars are expensive! **Lukas:** What does your dream car cost? **Jonas:** It costs twenty thousand euros. I save five hundred euros a month for it. **Lukas:** That'll take forty months! Will the bank lend you money? **Jonas:** Yes, the bank will lend me money — a loan. Then I'll owe the bank money. **Lukas:** Do you transfer to the bank monthly? **Jonas:** Yes, I transfer a monthly installment. The cost for that is manageable. **Lukas:** I'd like to buy a car too. Are there any affordable deals? **Jonas:** Yes! I found a great deal online — very affordable and available. **Lukas:** Send me the link! I'll withdraw some cash and check it out. **Jonas:** Sure! Hey, would you like a coffee now? I'll pay — my treat! **Lukas:** Really? You're so kind! Thanks, Jonas! **Jonas:** No problem. We'll go to the café over there — the prices are very reasonable. **Lukas:** Perfect! I don't need an expensive credit card — a cheap coffee is enough! **Jonas:** Haha! Okay, let's go! Bye for now!

## Dialogue 9: At the Travel Agency

**Mrs. Hoffmann** (customer) and **Mr. Klein** (travel agency employee) plan a trip.

**Mr. Klein:** Good day! Welcome to the travel agency. How can I help you? **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Good day! I'd like to book a trip to Tunisia. What are the prices? **Mr. Klein:** We have very affordable deals for Tunisia! The price starts at five hundred euros. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** That's very reasonable! What's included in the price? **Mr. Klein:** Flight, hotel, and breakfast — all for five hundred euros. A very good deal! **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Fantastic! When is the deal available? **Mr. Klein:** The deal is available in July and August. In June it's more expensive. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** I like to travel in August — in the summer. Is August still available? **Mr. Klein:** Yes, August is still available, but it's limited — only a few spots left! **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Oh! Then I'd like to book right away. What do I need to pay? **Mr. Klein:** You pay a deposit of one hundred euros today. You transfer the rest by May. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Can I pay by credit card? **Mr. Klein:** Yes, of course. We accept all credit cards and also cash. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Good. I'll pay by credit card. Will I get a receipt? **Mr. Klein:** Yes, you'll get a receipt and a detailed invoice. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Is there a discount for families? **Mr. Klein:** Yes! Children under twelve travel free. That's our family deal. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Wonderful! I'm traveling with my daughter — she's eight years old. **Mr. Klein:** Perfect! Your daughter travels free then. The price for you is five hundred euros. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Very good. I'd also like to upgrade the hotel — what does that cost? **Mr. Klein:** An upgrade to a five-star hotel costs two hundred euros more. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** That's a little expensive. Is it worthwhile? **Mr. Klein:** Yes, very worthwhile! Pool, spa, and a direct sea view — a wonderful experience! **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Okay, I'll take the upgrade! Total price is seven hundred euros then? **Mr. Klein:** Exactly! Here is your invoice and receipt. Goodbye, Mrs. Hoffmann! **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Goodbye, Mr. Klein! Thank you for the affordable deal!

## Dialogue 10: At the Neighbor's

**Emma** and her neighbor **Paul** talk about everyday things and money.

**Emma:** Good evening, Paul! Are you home? **Paul:** Emma! Yes, come in! How are you? **Emma:** I'm not doing so well. I have a problem with my washing machine. **Paul:** Oh no! What happened? **Emma:** It's broken. The technician costs two hundred euros — that's too expensive for me! **Paul:** Two hundred euros! That really is expensive. Do you have the money in your account? **Emma:** No, my account is tight right now. I spent too much this month. **Paul:** What did you spend money on? **Emma:** New furniture! I bought a sofa and a table. The prices were high. **Paul:** Were they at least good value? **Emma:** The sofa was reduced — thirty percent off! But the table was expensive and high quality. **Paul:** Do you have a receipt for the furniture? Sometimes you can exchange items. **Emma:** Yes, I have all the receipts. But I want to keep the furniture — it's beautiful! **Paul:** I understand. Can I lend you money for the washing machine? **Emma:** You want to lend me money? That's so kind of you, Paul! **Paul:** Of course! I'll lend you a hundred euros. You can save the other hundred yourself. **Emma:** Then I'll owe you a hundred euros. I'll transfer the money to you next month. **Paul:** No problem! I trust you. You're a hardworking and friendly neighbor. **Emma:** Thank you! I'm earning more next month — I've been working overtime. **Paul:** Great! Do you also have cash at home? For small expenses? **Emma:** Yes, I have twenty euros cash. I withdrew some this morning. **Paul:** Good. By the way — the supermarket next door has a great deal today! **Emma:** Really? What kind of deal? **Paul:** Refrigerators and stoves — all reduced! The prices are very affordable! **Emma:** Oh! My stove is also old. But I'm not buying anything now — I'd rather save! **Paul:** That's smart! Good night, Emma! **Emma:** Good night, Paul! Thanks for everything — you're the best neighbor!
