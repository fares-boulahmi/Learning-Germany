# Everyday Life in German – Eating & Cooking

---

Good morning! I'm Anna, and I live in a small apartment in Germany. Every morning I get up at seven o'clock and prepare breakfast. Breakfast is very important to me, because I can't work without a good meal. I like to cook eggs and cut fresh vegetables to go with them. My husband Peter is still sleeping when I enter the kitchen. He prefers a late breakfast on weekends. Today I'm cutting tomatoes and peeling an orange for the fruit salad. The fruit salad tastes sweet and fresh – Peter likes it very much. I season the eggs with a little salt and pepper. Breakfast is ready at half past seven. Peter comes into the kitchen and says: "Good morning, this tastes wonderful!" We eat breakfast together at the table in the dining room. After breakfast I wash the dishes and get ready for work. Every day I remind myself to drink enough water. Without a good breakfast I feel tired and weak.

---

Peter works as a computer scientist in an office in the city. He starts work at nine o'clock and finishes at six in the evening. During his lunch break he often goes to a restaurant near the office. The menu there is extensive and the portions are very tasty. Today he orders the main course with meat and a side dish of cooked vegetables. The waiter recommends the soup of the day as a starter. Peter tries the soup and finds it a little salty, but good. He also eats dessert – a sweet cake with cream. After lunch he drinks a coffee and pays the bill at the register. The meal costs fifteen euros, and he pays by credit card. He quickly takes care of an appointment and then returns to the office. In the afternoon he feels fit and focused. Sometimes he brings a piece of cake for his colleague – she is always kind and friendly to him. He never forgets to tell her about good restaurants. A good restaurant recommendation is very valuable to Peter.

---

My mother Helga cooks the best dinners in the family. She bakes fresh bread every Friday and fries meat in a pan on Saturdays. The recipe for her bread is very old – she learned it from her grandmother. The ingredients for the bread are simple: flour, water, salt and yeast. She also peels potatoes and cooks them as a side dish with the meat. The fried meat tastes spicy and delicious at the same time. My brother Klaus prefers the meat without spicy seasonings because he has an allergy. Helga therefore only seasons his portion with a little salt. She serves dinner punctually at seven in the evening in the dining room. There are five people in the family and we all sit together at the table. Father looks at the meal and says: "The portion is large – just right!" After dinner we relax comfortably in front of the TV. Helga plans the menu for the following week as early as Sunday. Her cooking habits are regular and very organized. We all love her fresh, homemade dishes.

---

On Wednesday I go with my cousin Lisa to the supermarket to buy the ingredients for a new recipe. We need fresh vegetables, spices and a piece of meat for the main course. Lisa recommends buying the vegetables at the market, because they are fresher and cheaper there. I sometimes order ingredients online, but today we buy everything in the store. The prices at the supermarket are sometimes expensive, but today there is a deal on meat – reduced by twenty percent. I like to save money and take advantage of every discount. We pay at the register with cash. The receipt shows that we spent thirty euros. Lisa finds a tasty dessert on the shelf – a chocolate cake that looks fresh. We put it in the shopping basket and laugh. At home we cut the vegetables and peel the potatoes together. Cooking is more fun with two people. Lisa fries the meat while I prepare the side dish. We taste everything before serving and season to taste. The result tastes sweet, salty and a little spicy – perfect!

---
My son Tim is twelve years old and is currently learning to cook. He likes to bake cakes and tries a new recipe every week. Last week he baked an apple cake – the ingredients were apples, flour, sugar and butter. He peeled the apples and cut them into small pieces. The cake tasted very delicious – sweet and fresh at the same time. Tim prefers sweet dishes over spicy or sour ones. I recommend that he also cook savory dishes so that he gets all the nutrients he needs. He is diligent and never forgets to clean the kitchen afterward. His grandmother Helga taught him how to bake. She says Tim has a talent for it. Next week he wants to fry pancakes – he has already prepared all the ingredients. The portion for the family is always large, because we are all hungry. He serves the food with a smile and asks: "Does it taste good to you?" The family always answers: "Yes, delicious!" This makes Tim very happy and motivates him to keep going.

---

On weekends we sometimes go to a restaurant in the city. We prefer a quiet restaurant with a large menu. The waiter greets us warmly: "Good evening! How are you?" We sit down at a table by the window. I order the starter – a fresh vegetable soup – and Peter orders the main course with fried meat. The children choose their meal from the children's menu. The dessert tastes very sweet and is served generously with cream. The portion is large and we are all full. The chef recommends a special dish today with fresh, seasonal ingredients. I try it and find it wonderfully seasoned – spicy and at the same time slightly salty. We spend two hours in the restaurant and talk a lot. The bill comes to seventy-five euros – a little expensive, but the food is worth it. We pay by credit card and leave a small tip for the kind waiter. After dinner we stroll through the city and relax. It is a lovely evening out with the family.

---

My colleague Sara is a vegetarian and prefers dishes without meat. She cooks fresh vegetables every day and likes to experiment with new spices. Her favorite recipe is a spicy curry with vegetables and rice as a side dish. She peels the pumpkin, cuts it into cubes and fries it in a pan with oil. Then she seasons everything with turmeric, cumin and a little red pepper. The dish tastes spicy, but also slightly sweet from the pumpkin. Sara recommends this recipe to me highly and says it is affordable and healthy. The ingredients cost less than ten euros and are enough for two portions. She sometimes brings the curry to the office as lunch. All colleagues like to try it and find it delicious. I normally prefer dishes with meat, but Sara's curry is an exception. She saves a lot of money because vegetables are cheaper than meat. Her recipe is now in our little office recipe collection. We regularly cook together after work – it's fun and strengthens our friendship. The fresh ingredients from the market make all the difference.

---

My father Klaus is retired and spends a lot of time in the kitchen. He cooks lunch every day for himself and grandma. He starts cooking at eleven o'clock and finishes punctually at half past twelve. His specialty is a cooked vegetable dish with various side dishes. He peels the potatoes, cuts the vegetables and seasons everything carefully. The meal always tastes fresh and healthy. Grandma says Klaus cooks better than many a restaurant cook. He tastes the dish before serving and sometimes adds another spice. He most enjoys cooking dishes with bitter-tasting herbs that are good for your health. He sometimes orders special spices online because they are not available in the supermarket. Delivery takes two to three days. He plans the meals for the whole week in advance and writes all the ingredients on a list. He does the shopping on Mondays because the supermarket is less crowded then. Klaus always remembers to buy fresh vegetables – spoiled vegetables have no place in his kitchen. He is a diligent and organized cook.

---

In summer we often have a barbecue in the garden. We grill meat and bake bread in the oven to go with it. I prepare the side dishes in the morning – I cut tomatoes, peel cucumbers and dress the salad with oil and vinegar. The salad tastes fresh and slightly sour – perfect for hot summer days. We sometimes order extra meat from the butcher when the family gathering is large. The portion per person is generous, because we are all hungry after swimming in the garden. Uncle Dieter always recommends marinating the meat with a special spice mixture. This makes it more tender and delicious. We serve the food on the table in the garden under a large parasol. Dessert is a fresh fruit salad with sweet strawberries and melon. The children try everything enthusiastically and ask for more. We spend the whole afternoon outside and relax after the meal. The barbecue is a lovely tradition we keep up every summer. It is always delicious, cozy and unforgettable.

---

At the end of the week I take stock of the kitchen. I look in the fridge and check which ingredients are still available and which I need to buy. I throw away spoiled vegetables immediately – fresh ingredients are very important to me. I plan the menu for the following week and write down all the recipes and ingredients. Cooking for me is not just a daily task, but also a joy. I prefer simple dishes with few ingredients that are well seasoned and taste delicious. My family tries at least one new dish every week – that makes everyday life more interesting. Peter sometimes recommends dishes from the restaurant, which I then recreate at home. The recipe is not always identical, but it usually tastes good. We also save money when we cook more at home instead of going to a restaurant. Meals at home are warmer, cozier and healthier. Cooking and eating together brings the family closer. I am grateful for every delicious evening at the table with my loved ones. Good night – and until the next delicious breakfast!
