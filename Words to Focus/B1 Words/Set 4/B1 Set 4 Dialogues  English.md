## Dialogue 1: At the Restaurant

**Context:** Formal — Waiter and customer. Focus: to order, to recommend, to taste, menu, starter, main course, dessert, delicious, fresh

---

**Waiter (Klaus):** Good evening! Welcome to the Zur Linde Restaurant. My name is Klaus. I am your waiter this evening.

**Customer (Sara):** Good evening, Klaus! Thank you. I reserved a table for two people. My name is Sara Meier.

**Klaus:** Yes, Ms. Meier, very good. Here is the menu. May I recommend something to you?

**Sara:** Yes, please! What do you recommend today?

**Klaus:** I recommend the fresh tomato soup as a starter. The chef cooked it this morning with fresh tomatoes from the garden.

**Sara:** Hmm, fresh tomato soup sounds delicious. And what is today's main course?

**Klaus:** Today's main course is roasted chicken with a delicious vegetable side dish. We received the vegetables this morning — they are very fresh.

**Sara:** That sounds wonderful. I would like to order the fresh tomato soup as a starter and the roasted chicken as the main course, please.

**Klaus:** Excellent. And would you also like a dessert? I can particularly recommend the dessert — today we have a sweet chocolate cake.

**Sara:** Oh, sweet chocolate cake! I'd love to order that too. But first I want to try the starter.

**Klaus:** Of course. May I ask what you would like to drink?

**Sara:** I prefer a glass of water, please. I've been thirsty for an hour!

**Klaus:** Certainly, Ms. Meier. The water will come right away. I also tasted our new red wine yesterday — it is very good, in case you would like wine later.

**Sara:** Thank you, Klaus. How long will the starter take?

**Klaus:** We will serve the tomato soup in about ten minutes. The chef is preparing it right now.

**Sara:** Very good. I'm happy to wait. The restaurant is very nice and quiet here.

**Klaus:** Thank you! Enjoy your meal later, Ms. Meier. I will come back when you have finished the starter.

**Sara:** Thank you, Klaus. See you in a moment!

## Dialogue 2: In the Kitchen

**Context:** Informal — Two friends cooking together. Focus: to cook, to bake, to cut, to peel, to season, recipe, ingredient, spice, vegetable, salty, spicy

---

**Lena:** Hello, Tom! Great that you came over to cook today.

**Tom:** Hello, Lena! Yes, I'm excited! What are we cooking today?

**Lena:** I found a new recipe — we're making a spicy vegetable soup. Have you ever made a spicy soup before?

**Tom:** Yes, I made a spicy lentil soup last month. It was very tasty, but a little too salty. I used too much salt as a spice.

**Lena:** I know that feeling! What ingredients do we need for your recipe?

**Tom:** The recipe needs fresh vegetables: carrots, onions, and bell peppers. I'm peeling the carrots now — can you cut the onions?

**Lena:** Of course! I'll cut the onions. But I always cry when I cut onions!

**Tom:** Me too! Yesterday I cut onions for another recipe and my eyes were burning.

**Lena:** What spice do we season the soup with?

**Tom:** We season it with cumin and chili. I prefer spicy dishes. Do you have these spices?

**Lena:** Yes, I have both spices. But don't season too much — my sister is coming to eat too and she is allergic to very spicy spices.

**Tom:** No problem. I'll only season the soup lightly spicy. That way it works for everyone.

**Lena:** Great! And we're also baking bread to go with it. I already prepared the dough yesterday.

**Tom:** You already baked? You're so diligent! What did you put in the dough?

**Lena:** Just simple ingredients: flour, water, salt, and yeast. The bread is in the oven now. It needs another twenty minutes.

**Tom:** Delicious! I love trying fresh bread. May I try some when it's done?

**Lena:** Of course! We'll taste everything together. So — the vegetables are cut. Are you cooking the soup now?

**Tom:** Yes! I'm cooking the soup now. In thirty minutes we'll eat!

**Lena:** Wonderful. I'm already looking forward to it!

## Dialogue 3: At the Bakery

**Context:** Formal/Semi-formal — Customer and baker. Focus: to bake, to buy, to cost, breakfast, recipe, sweet, fresh, delicious, portion, ingredient

---

**Baker (Mr. Braun):** Good morning! What can I get for you?

**Customer (Mrs. Weber):** Good morning, Mr. Braun! I'd like to buy fresh bread for breakfast. What have you baked today?

**Mr. Braun:** I started baking at four o'clock this morning. We have fresh rolls, a large whole grain loaf, and sweet croissants.

**Mrs. Weber:** Oh, sweet croissants! How much do the croissants cost?

**Mr. Braun:** One sweet croissant costs one euro twenty. Six croissants cost six euro fifty — that's a good deal.

**Mrs. Weber:** I'll buy six croissants for breakfast. My family loves sweet things in the morning.

**Mr. Braun:** Very good! And may I also recommend a fresh whole grain loaf? The recipe comes from Switzerland — it is a traditional recipe with special ingredients.

**Mrs. Weber:** Interesting! What are the special ingredients in the recipe?

**Mr. Braun:** The recipe contains sunflower seeds and flaxseeds as ingredients. I learned the recipe from my grandfather. He worked as a baker in Switzerland for twenty years.

**Mrs. Weber:** That sounds wonderful. I'll buy a whole grain loaf too. What does the bread cost?

**Mr. Braun:** The large whole grain loaf costs three euro eighty. Together with the six croissants you will pay ten euro thirty.

**Mrs. Weber:** Good. I'll pay by credit card. May I ask — how long did the baking take today?

**Mr. Braun:** I baked for three hours. The bread needs a long time in the oven, but it's worth it! The bread is always fresh and delicious.

**Mrs. Weber:** Yes, your bread is always so delicious! My children eat your fresh bread for breakfast every week.

**Mr. Braun:** That makes me very happy! Here are your croissants and the bread. Enjoy your meal!

**Mrs. Weber:** Thank you, Mr. Braun! Goodbye!

**Mr. Braun:** Goodbye, Mrs. Weber! Have a lovely day!

## Dialogue 4: At the Supermarket

**Context:** Informal — Two friends shopping together. Focus: to buy, to cost, to save, vegetable, meat, side dish, fresh, spoiled, raw, meal, portion

---

**Anna:** Hello, Jonas! What are you doing here at the supermarket?

**Jonas:** Hello, Anna! I'm buying ingredients for dinner tonight. And you?

**Anna:** I'm also buying vegetables for the week. What are you cooking tonight?

**Jonas:** I'm making spaghetti with fresh vegetables and raw meat. I still need meat and vegetables.

**Anna:** Here's the vegetable section. Oh, these vegetables don't look fresh — they're almost spoiled!

**Jonas:** Oh no! You're right, they look spoiled. I won't buy spoiled vegetables. Let's go to the other shelf.

**Anna:** Over there is the fresh produce. These tomatoes are really fresh — look!

**Jonas:** Yes, they're nicely fresh and red. I'll take these tomatoes. How much does a kilogram of tomatoes cost?

**Anna:** One kilo costs two euro ninety. Not expensive! And next to the vegetables is the meat. Which meat are you buying?

**Jonas:** I prefer chicken — it's affordable and healthy. How much does the chicken cost here?

**Anna:** The chicken costs four euro fifty per kilo. That's reasonable. Last week I bought chicken for six euros per kilo!

**Jonas:** Really? Then I'm saving money today. I'll buy a kilo of chicken. What are you having as a side dish with your meal?

**Anna:** I'm making a simple meal — rice with vegetables as a side dish. Rice is cheap and makes a good side dish.

**Jonas:** Good idea. A healthy meal! I still need spaghetti. Where is the spaghetti here?

**Anna:** In aisle three, next to the canned goods. I'll come with you! Afterwards we'll go to the checkout together.

**Jonas:** Great! Thanks for your help, Anna. You know this supermarket very well.

**Anna:** No problem! I shop here every Saturday.

## Dialogue 5: Dinner with Friends

**Context:** Informal — Friends at a dinner party. Focus: to cook, to serve, to try, to taste, to prefer, dinner, meal, dessert, delicious, sweet, bitter, portion

---

**Host (Mia):** Hello everyone! Welcome to my dinner! Please sit down.

**Guest (Felix):** Hello, Mia! Thanks for the invitation. What have you cooked for tonight?

**Mia:** I've prepared a big meal! For the main course there is roasted vegetables with rice. I cooked for two hours!

**Guest (Sophie):** Wow, you cooked for so long! That's very kind of you, Mia.

**Mia:** My pleasure! I'll serve the food now. Here — I'll serve you the starter first.

**Felix:** That looks so delicious! What is it?

**Mia:** It's a fresh salad plate with bitter arugula and sweet tomatoes. I prefer salad as a starter — it's light and fresh.

**Sophie:** I also prefer light starters. The salad looks wonderful!

**Felix:** May I try some? — Mmm! It tastes fantastic! Isn't the arugula too bitter?

**Mia:** No, the bitter arugula goes well with the sweet tomatoes. Together it tastes delicious!

**Sophie:** You're right. It really does taste good. How did you season the vegetables?

**Mia:** I seasoned the starter with olive oil and a little lemon. Simple but delicious!

**Felix:** After the salad I'm looking forward to the main course. Can I have another portion of salad?

**Mia:** Of course! I'll serve you another portion right away. — And for dessert I've prepared something sweet!

**Sophie:** Oh! What is there for dessert?

**Mia:** A fresh fruit cake — it tastes sweet and light. I baked it this afternoon.

**Felix:** I tried your cake last time and it tasted so good! I'm already looking forward to dessert.

**Mia:** Thank you, Felix! Enjoy your meal, everyone!

**Both:** Thank you, Mia! Enjoy your meal!

## Dialogue 6: A Cooking Class

**Context:** Formal — Teacher and students. Focus: to cook, to bake, to fry, to peel, to cut, to season, ingredient, spice, recipe, raw, cooked, spicy, salty

---

**Teacher (Ms. König):** Good morning, everyone! Welcome to cooking class. Today we are learning how to fry a traditional dish.

**Student (Markus):** Good morning, Ms. König! What are we frying today?

**Ms. König:** We are frying chicken with spicy seasonings. But first — what ingredients do we need?

**Student (Nina):** For the recipe we need chicken, oil, and spices, right?

**Ms. König:** Exactly, Nina. We need raw chicken, oil, garlic, and various spices. Who can tell me how to handle raw meat safely?

**Markus:** You should always keep raw meat separate from other ingredients.

**Ms. König:** Very good, Markus! Now we peel the garlic. Nina, can you please peel the garlic?

**Nina:** Yes, of course. I'm peeling the garlic now. — Done! Should I also cut it?

**Ms. König:** Yes, please cut it into small pieces. — Good. Now we season the meat. What spice should we season the chicken with?

**Markus:** The recipe says cumin and paprika. Are these spices hot?

**Ms. König:** Cumin is not hot, but paprika can be spicy — there are mild and hot varieties. I prefer to season the chicken with mild paprika so it doesn't get too spicy.

**Nina:** I love eating spicy dishes! Can you take more hot spice?

**Ms. König:** In your portion you can add more hot spice, Nina. But for the class we'll season the meat only lightly.

**Markus:** How long do we fry the chicken?

**Ms. König:** We fry it for twenty minutes at high heat. The chicken must be thoroughly cooked — raw meat is dangerous!

**Nina:** How do you check if the chicken is cooked?

**Ms. König:** You cut it open and look to see if it is still raw inside. Cooked meat is white inside — raw meat is pink.

**Markus:** Thank you, Ms. König! I've fried chicken at home before, but I seasoned it too salty.

**Ms. König:** That happens! You should always use salt as a seasoning sparingly. You can add more later, but you can't take away too much salt!

**Nina:** That's a good tip. I'll try the recipe at home.

**Ms. König:** Wonderful! Then good luck cooking at home!

## Dialogue 7: At the Café

**Context:** Informal — Two colleagues on a lunch break. Focus: to order, to recommend, to try, to taste, to prefer, to be thirsty, lunch, menu, portion, delicious, fresh, sweet, sour

---

**Colleague (Laura):** Hello, Ben! Lunch break at last! Let's go to the café.

**Colleague (Ben):** Yes, gladly! I've been thirsty for two hours — I need something to drink immediately.

**Laura:** Me too! I prefer fresh orange juice. What do you normally order here?

**Ben:** I always order the coffee with milk. But today I might try something different. Have you already looked at the menu?

**Laura:** Yes, I had a quick look at the menu. Today there is a daily special — fresh tomato soup and a sandwich for lunch.

**Ben:** That sounds good! Last Tuesday I ordered the tomato soup here and it tasted really good.

**Laura:** Really? I've never tried it. Do you recommend it?

**Ben:** Yes, I highly recommend it! The soup is fresh and delicious — not too salty, not too spicy. Just right.

**Laura:** Then I'll also order the tomato soup. What are you having as a main course?

**Ben:** I prefer the chicken sandwich. It's fresh and the portion is large. You're full after one portion!

**Laura:** I'd rather have the fresh salad with sour vinaigrette. I like to eat light at lunchtime.

**Ben:** Do you like sour things? I don't enjoy sour dishes.

**Laura:** Yes! I prefer sour or bitter dishes over sweet ones. I'd rather eat sweet things as dessert.

**Ben:** Speaking of dessert — yesterday I tried the chocolate muffin here. It tastes so sweet and delicious!

**Laura:** Oh, chocolate! I'd love to try that. How big is the portion?

**Ben:** One portion is a large muffin. It's plenty after lunch.

**Laura:** Good. I'll order the muffin as dessert. — Excuse me! We'd like to order please!

**Waiter:** Of course! What would you like?

**Ben:** I'll order the tomato soup, the chicken sandwich, and a coffee, please.

**Laura:** And I'll order the tomato soup, the fresh salad with sour vinaigrette, an orange juice, and a chocolate muffin for dessert, please.

**Waiter:** Very good! That will be right out.

**Ben:** Thank you! — Cheers, Laura! To a short but delicious lunch break!

**Laura:** Cheers! Enjoy your meal!

**Context:** Informal — Family at the breakfast table. Focus: to cook, to bake, to cut, breakfast, meal, ingredient, sweet, fresh, delicious, portion, to taste

---

**Mother (Claudia):** Good morning! Breakfast is ready! Everyone come to the table!

**Son (Lukas, 14):** Good morning, Mom! What's for breakfast today? I'm already starving!

**Daughter (Emma, 17):** Good morning! Mmm, it smells so delicious in the kitchen!

**Claudia:** I cooked and baked a special breakfast today. There are fresh pancakes with jam!

**Lukas:** Pancakes! That's my favourite breakfast! How many pancakes did you bake?

**Claudia:** I baked twenty pancakes — that should be enough for everyone! I made them with fresh ingredients.

**Emma:** What ingredients did you use for the pancakes, Mom?

**Claudia:** The recipe is simple: flour, eggs, milk, and a little salt. And for the sweet flavour, jam goes on top.

**Lukas:** I prefer sweet jam on my pancakes. Do you have strawberry jam?

**Claudia:** Yes, I have strawberry jam. And for Emma I also cut some fresh fruit — you don't like very sweet things, right?

**Emma:** That's right! I prefer fresh fruit with my pancakes. The jam is too sweet for me in the morning.

**Lukas:** Can I please have two portions of pancakes? I'm really hungry!

**Claudia:** Of course! Here — I'll serve you two portions. But don't eat too fast!

**Emma:** The pancakes taste so good, Mom! How long did you cook?

**Claudia:** I worked in the kitchen for half an hour. Breakfast is the most important meal of the day — I love making it special.

**Lukas:** That's true! I eat a good breakfast every morning. It gives me energy for school.

**Emma:** Yesterday I forgot breakfast and I was tired all morning. That won't happen to me again!

**Claudia:** Good! A warm meal in the morning is very important. Have another portion if you'd like!

**Lukas:** Thanks, Mom! You cook and bake the best in the family!

**Claudia:** Thank you, Lukas! That is the nicest compliment!

## Dialogue 9: At the Doctor – Stomach Problems

**Context:** Formal — Doctor and patient. Focus: to taste, to digest, to eat, meal, meat, vegetables, spicy, salty, fresh, spoiled, cooked, raw

---

**Doctor (Dr. Hoffmann):** Good day, Mr. Bauer. How are you? What are your complaints?

**Patient (Stefan):** Good day, Dr. Hoffmann. I'm not feeling well. I've had stomach pain for two days. I can't digest well.

**Dr. Hoffmann:** I'm sorry to hear that. Tell me — what have you eaten in the last few days? What meals have you had?

**Stefan:** On Monday I ate raw meat — a steak that was still raw inside. After that my stomach started hurting.

**Dr. Hoffmann:** Raw meat can be very dangerous! Did the meat taste strange?

**Stefan:** Yes, it tasted odd — not fresh. I think the meat was perhaps spoiled.

**Dr. Hoffmann:** That is probably the problem. Spoiled or raw meat can cause an infection. Did the food also taste bad?

**Stefan:** Yes! Normally I really enjoy the taste of meat, but this time it didn't taste good to me. I should have stopped eating.

**Dr. Hoffmann:** Yes, unfortunately. What else did you eat? Did you eat spicy or very salty meals?

**Stefan:** I often eat spicy food — I prefer spicy seasonings. And I always season my food salty. Is that a problem?

**Dr. Hoffmann:** Very spicy and very salty food can irritate the stomach, especially when you are ill. I recommend you now eat only light meals.

**Stefan:** What should I eat? Only cooked vegetables?

**Dr. Hoffmann:** Exactly — cooked vegetables, fresh soup, and no spicy seasonings. You must not eat raw meat until you are well again.

**Stefan:** And how long do I have to eat like that?

**Dr. Hoffmann:** About a week. After that you can eat normal meals again. But always buy fresh meat and check whether it is spoiled!

**Stefan:** Yes, I'm learning that from this experience. How can I help my digestion?

**Dr. Hoffmann:** Drink plenty of water and eat small portions. Large portions are not good for your stomach right now.

**Stefan:** Thank you, Dr. Hoffmann. I will follow your recommendations.

**Dr. Hoffmann:** Get well soon, Mr. Bauer! Goodbye.

## Dialogue 10: A Cooking Competition

**Context:** Informal — Two friends competing in a home cooking challenge. Focus: to cook, to fry, to bake, to peel, to cut, to season, to recommend, to try, to taste, to serve, menu, main course, dessert, side dish, portion, delicious, spicy, sweet, sour, salty, fresh, raw, cooked, spoiled

---

**Friend (Max):** Hello, Jana! Are you ready for our cooking competition today?

**Jana:** Hello, Max! Yes, I'm ready! I've already prepared my ingredients. What are you cooking?

**Max:** I'm frying spicy chicken with a fresh vegetable side dish. And you?

**Jana:** I'm making a sweet and sour vegetable soup as the main course — and for dessert I'm baking a fresh fruit cake!

**Max:** A sweet dessert! Good. But my main course will be more delicious!

**Jana:** You think so! I'm peeling the vegetables now. Have you already cut your meat?

**Max:** Yes, I've already cut it into pieces. The meat is raw — I'm frying it in the pan now.

**Jana:** Be careful — is the meat fresh too? It looks a bit dark...

**Max:** It's fresh! I bought it this morning. I would never buy spoiled meat.

**Jana:** Good! What spice are you seasoning the chicken with?

**Max:** I'm seasoning it with hot paprika, cumin, and garlic. Very spicy! What are you seasoning your soup with?

**Jana:** I prefer a sweet and sour combination — I season with vinegar for the sour flavour and sugar for the sweetness. It sounds strange, but it tastes good!

**Max:** I love trying new flavours. Can I try the soup when it's ready?

**Jana:** Of course! And you'll tell me if the chicken tastes good, right?

**Max:** Absolutely! — Mmm, my chicken already smells delicious. It's almost done frying.

**Jana:** My soup is already cooking too. I'm adding the side dish now — fresh cooked vegetables on the side.

**Max:** I'll serve my dish first! Here — a large portion of spicy chicken with fresh vegetable side dish. Please try some!

**Jana:** Mmm... It tastes good! Very spicy, but delicious. The chicken is very well fried.

**Max:** Thank you! Now you're serving your soup?

**Jana:** Yes! Here — a portion of my sweet and sour soup. And the side dish is cooked rice.

**Max:** I'll try it... Wow! That tastes fantastic! Sweet and sour together — very interesting!

**Jana:** Thank you! And now the dessert — my fresh fruit cake. I baked it with sweet apples and a little sour lemon.

**Max:** You baked apples and lemon together? That sounds sour!

**Jana:** Try it first, then judge! — Well? How does it taste?

**Max:** ...It tastes wonderful! Sweet and slightly sour — perfect as a dessert. Jana, I think you're winning the cooking competition today!

**Jana:** Yes! I'm the winner! But your chicken was also very delicious, Max.

**Max:** Thank you! Next time I'll win. I'm still learning new recipes!

**Jana:** It's a deal! Same competition in a month?

**Max:** Yes! Until then, Jana. Bye!

**Jana:** Bye, Max! Enjoy your meal today!
