## Dialogue 1: The Apartment Viewing

**Anna** is visiting an apartment. **Mr. Becker** is the landlord.

---

**Mr. Becker:** Good day! You are Ms. Müller? **Anna:** Yes, good day. I'm Anna Müller. Thank you for making time. **Mr. Becker:** Of course. Please, come in. This is the apartment on the third floor. **Anna:** Very nice. The apartment is very bright! I like bright rooms. **Mr. Becker:** Yes, we have large windows. The windows face south. **Anna:** How many rooms does the apartment have? **Mr. Becker:** Three rooms – a bedroom, a living room, and a kitchen. The bathroom is here on the left. **Anna:** Is the apartment furnished or unfurnished? **Mr. Becker:** It's unfurnished. But the wardrobe in the bedroom stays. **Anna:** I see. And how much is the rent? **Mr. Becker:** The rent is eight hundred euros per month. **Anna:** Are utilities included? **Mr. Becker:** No, utilities are on top – approximately one hundred and fifty euros. That covers heating, water, and electricity. **Anna:** Okay. And how much is the deposit? **Mr. Becker:** The deposit is two months' rent, so one thousand six hundred euros. **Anna:** That's a lot. When can I move in? **Mr. Becker:** You can move in on the first of March. The lease begins then. **Anna:** I still need to think about the lease. Can I take it with me today? **Mr. Becker:** Yes, of course. I'll give you the lease to take with you. **Anna:** Thank you very much. The apartment is really cozy. I like it a lot. **Mr. Becker:** I'm glad to hear that. Do you have any more questions? **Anna:** Yes. Is there an elevator? **Mr. Becker:** Unfortunately not. But the staircase is wide and bright. **Anna:** And a basement? **Mr. Becker:** Yes! Every tenant gets a storage room in the basement. The basement is large. **Anna:** That's practical. I'll get back to you by Friday. **Mr. Becker:** Very good. Goodbye, Ms. Müller. **Anna:** Goodbye, Mr. Becker.

## Dialogue 2: Moving In and Furnishing

**Lena** is moving into a new apartment. Her friend **Sophie** is helping her.

---

**Sophie:** Hi Lena! Wow, your new apartment is really spacious! **Lena:** Yes, I'm so happy! The old apartment was so cramped. Here I finally have space. **Sophie:** How long did you look for an apartment? **Lena:** Two months. It was very stressful. But now I've moved in and I'm happy. **Sophie:** When did you move in? **Lena:** I moved in yesterday. The furniture came this morning. **Sophie:** Have you already set up the apartment? **Lena:** Just a little. I put the sofa in the living room and the table in the dining room. **Sophie:** And the bedroom? **Lena:** The bed is already there. But the wardrobe hasn't been assembled yet. **Sophie:** I'll help you! Show me the wardrobe. **Lena:** It's still in the kitchen. That's not practical, I know. **Sophie:** No problem. We'll carry it together into the bedroom. And what are you doing with the attic? **Lena:** I'll store suitcases and old things in the attic. **Sophie:** Good. And have you already checked the heating? **Lena:** Yes, the heating works very well. It's warm and cozy here. **Sophie:** That's nice. Are you renovating anything? **Lena:** I'd like to renovate the living room. The walls are old-fashioned. **Sophie:** Do you want to paint the walls? **Lena:** Yes! I'll paint them next week. I want a light color, maybe white or light gray. **Sophie:** White makes a room even brighter. Good idea! **Lena:** Thanks. And I'd like to air out the place – please open the window! **Sophie:** Of course. Fresh air is important after a move. **Lena:** Exactly. Would you like a coffee? The kitchen is already fully set up. **Sophie:** Yes, gladly! You've set up the kitchen very practically. **Lena:** Thanks, Sophie. I'm so glad you're helping me!

## Dialogue 3: Problems with the Apartment

**Mr. Schulz** (tenant) calls his **landlord, Ms. Wagner**.

---

**Mr. Schulz:** Good morning, Ms. Wagner. This is Schulz from the apartment on the second floor. **Ms. Wagner:** Good morning, Mr. Schulz. What can I do for you? **Mr. Schulz:** I have a problem. The heating is no longer working. It's very cold in the apartment. **Ms. Wagner:** Oh, I'm sorry to hear that. Since when has the heating not been working? **Mr. Schulz:** Since yesterday evening. I was freezing the whole night. That is not acceptable. **Ms. Wagner:** I understand. I'll send a technician today. He'll repair the heating. **Mr. Schulz:** Thank you. I have a second problem as well. **Ms. Wagner:** What is it? **Mr. Schulz:** The staircase is broken. One step is cracked. That's dangerous. **Ms. Wagner:** I know. I had the staircase renovated last week. But there's still a broken step? **Mr. Schulz:** Yes, the third step from the bottom. I almost fell. **Ms. Wagner:** That's very serious. I'll have the staircase repaired immediately. **Mr. Schulz:** Good. And one more thing – the trash. Where should I take the trash? **Ms. Wagner:** The trash goes in the basement. The bins are there. **Mr. Schulz:** Ah, in the basement. I haven't found the basement yet. **Ms. Wagner:** The basement is behind the elevator. The elevator is to the left of the entrance. **Mr. Schulz:** Good, thank you. And when can I expect the technician? **Ms. Wagner:** Between ten and twelve o'clock. Will you be home then? **Mr. Schulz:** Yes, I'll be home. I'll wait for the technician. **Ms. Wagner:** Very good. I'll call him right away. **Mr. Schulz:** Thank you, Ms. Wagner. One more question – do I have to pay for the repair? **Ms. Wagner:** No, I'll pay for that as the landlord. The heating is my responsibility. **Mr. Schulz:** That's very kind. Thank you for your help. **Ms. Wagner:** No problem. Goodbye, Mr. Schulz. **Mr. Schulz:** Goodbye, Ms. Wagner.

## Dialogue 4: Ringing the Neighbor's Doorbell

**Timo** rings the doorbell of his neighbor **Ms. Klein**.

---

**Timo:** _(rings the doorbell)_ **Ms. Klein:** Yes? Who's there? **Timo:** Good evening, Ms. Klein. I'm Timo, your neighbor from the fourth floor. **Ms. Klein:** Ah, Timo! One moment, I'll open up. _(opens the door)_ Good evening! What can I do for you? **Timo:** Sorry for ringing so late. I've lost my keys. Can you help me? **Ms. Klein:** Oh no! Have you already locked the apartment? **Timo:** Yes, unfortunately. I locked the apartment and then lost the key. **Ms. Klein:** Do you have a spare key? **Timo:** Yes, with my brother. But he lives far away. **Ms. Klein:** Can you call your landlord? **Timo:** I called the landlord. He's not answering. **Ms. Klein:** That's a problem. Would you like to wait here? Come in. **Timo:** Thank you, that's very kind of you. Your apartment is very cozy! **Ms. Klein:** Thank you! I've invested a lot of time in the furnishings. Would you like some tea? **Timo:** Yes, please. Thank you very much. **Ms. Klein:** How long have you been living here? **Timo:** I moved in a year ago. I really like it here. **Ms. Klein:** Me too. I've been living here for twenty years. The landlord is very nice. **Timo:** Yes, normally. But today he's not answering. **Ms. Klein:** Try again. **Timo:** _(calls)_ He's still not answering. **Ms. Klein:** Then call a locksmith. It costs something, but it's quick. **Timo:** Yes, that's a good idea. Do you have the number? **Ms. Klein:** Yes, I have the number here. One moment. **Timo:** You're very helpful, Ms. Klein. Thank you! **Ms. Klein:** That goes without saying. We're neighbors! **Timo:** True. I'm glad to have neighbors like you. **Ms. Klein:** _(laughs)_ You're welcome. Here's the number. Good luck! **Timo:** Thank you. Good night, Ms. Klein! **Ms. Klein:** Good night, Timo!

## Dialogue 5: Rent or Buy?

**Jonas** and his girlfriend **Mia** discuss their future home.

---

**Mia:** Jonas, I've been thinking. Should we rent or buy an apartment? **Jonas:** That's a big question. I think we should rent. Buying is very expensive. **Mia:** But if we rent, we pay rent every month and have nothing to show for it. **Jonas:** True. But if we buy, we need a lot of money for the down payment and the loan. **Mia:** How much does an apartment cost here in the city? **Jonas:** A spacious apartment costs around three hundred thousand euros. **Mia:** That's a lot. And the rent? **Jonas:** The rent for a similar apartment is about nine hundred euros per month. **Mia:** Plus utilities – heating, electricity, gas, water… **Jonas:** Exactly. Utilities are about two hundred euros extra. **Mia:** I like the idea of having our own apartment. We could renovate however we like. **Jonas:** That's true. In a rented apartment you can't change everything. The landlord has to agree. **Mia:** For example, I'm not allowed to paint the walls without asking. **Jonas:** Right. And when you move out, you sometimes have to renovate the apartment back to its original state. **Mia:** That's annoying. I want a bright, modern apartment. Bright and modern – that's important to me. **Jonas:** Me too. But for me it's important that the apartment is practical. A large basement, an elevator… **Mia:** Yes, an elevator is important. I don't like long staircases. **Jonas:** Agreed. And an attic or basement for our things. **Mia:** Should we talk to a real estate agent? **Jonas:** Good idea. An agent can help us find the right apartment. **Mia:** I'll arrange an appointment for next week. **Jonas:** Great. And until then we'll look at listings online. **Mia:** Wonderful. I'm excited! A new home! **Jonas:** Me too. Together we'll find something nice. **Mia:** I'm sure of it. Bye for now! **Jonas:** Bye, Mia!

## Dialogue 6: Cleaning and Tidying Up

**Felix** and his roommate **Laura** talk about household chores.

---

**Laura:** Felix, when did you last clean the kitchen? **Felix:** Uh… three days ago? Or four? **Laura:** I cleaned the kitchen this morning. It was very dirty. **Felix:** I'm sorry, Laura. I forgot to tidy up. **Laura:** That happens often. We need a plan. **Felix:** You're right. We can divide up the tasks. **Laura:** Good. I'll clean the bathroom if you clean the kitchen. **Felix:** Agreed. And who does the living room? **Laura:** We can tidy up the living room together. **Felix:** Okay. How often should we clean? **Laura:** Weekly. Every Saturday – that's regular and not stressful. **Felix:** And the trash? **Laura:** I'll take the trash down on Tuesdays. You take it on Fridays. **Felix:** Practical. And who buys the cleaning supplies? **Laura:** We'll take turns buying them. This week me, next week you. **Felix:** Great. By the way – I'd like to air out the bedroom. It smells a bit stuffy. **Laura:** Yes, airing out is important! Open the window every morning for ten minutes. **Felix:** I'll do that. And the staircase – who cleans the staircase? **Laura:** The staircase is a shared responsibility. All the tenants in the building take turns cleaning it. **Felix:** Ah, I didn't know that. When is my turn? **Laura:** Your turn is in March. I have the schedule here. **Felix:** Okay, thanks. I won't forget. **Laura:** And one more thing – the basement. The basement is very messy. **Felix:** I'll tidy up the basement this weekend. I promise. **Laura:** Thanks, Felix. When we work together, it's not so much work. **Felix:** True. And then we can relax in the evening. **Laura:** Exactly! Order makes life cozier. **Felix:** You are absolutely right!

## Dialogue 7: Helping with the Move

**Kerim** is moving out and his friend **Marco** is helping him.

---

**Marco:** Good morning, Kerim! Are you ready for the move? **Kerim:** Hi Marco! Yes, almost. I haven't packed all the boxes yet. **Marco:** No problem. I'll help you. What isn't finished yet? **Kerim:** The stuff in the attic. I still need to clear out the attic. **Marco:** Okay, I'll head up to the attic. Where's the elevator? **Kerim:** The elevator is back there. But it's small – the large furniture has to go via the staircase. **Marco:** I see. The sofa over the staircase – that'll be tough. **Kerim:** Yes, I know. That's why I also asked my brother to help. **Marco:** Good. When is he coming? **Kerim:** At ten o'clock. We still have an hour. **Marco:** Then let's start with the boxes. What's in these boxes? **Kerim:** Books and clothes. The boxes are heavy. **Marco:** No problem. I'm strong! _(laughs)_ Where are you moving to? **Kerim:** I'm moving to a new apartment in the next neighborhood. The apartment is modern and spacious. **Marco:** Why are you moving out? **Kerim:** The rent here is too high. My landlord raised the rent. **Marco:** That's annoying. By how much did he raise the rent? **Kerim:** By one hundred and fifty euros per month. That was too much for me. **Marco:** I understand that. And the new apartment? **Kerim:** The new rent is cheaper. And utilities are included in the price. **Marco:** That's practical. Have you already signed the new lease? **Kerim:** Yes, I signed the lease last week and transferred the deposit. **Marco:** Great. I'm happy for you. **Kerim:** Thanks, Marco. I'm glad you're helping me. Without you it would be very stressful. **Marco:** Good friends help each other! Done with the boxes. What's next? **Kerim:** The fridge. But let's wait for my brother. **Marco:** Okay. How about you make us a coffee in the meantime? **Kerim:** Of course! Coffee and then back to the move!

## Dialogue 8: Viewing a Shared Apartment

**Nina** is visiting a shared apartment. **Tom** is one of the flatmates.

---

**Tom:** Hello! You're Nina? **Nina:** Yes, hi! I'm Nina Schmidt. Nice to meet you. **Tom:** I'm Tom. Come in! We've been looking forward to your visit. **Nina:** Thank you. The apartment looks nice from outside. It's very bright! **Tom:** Yes, we're lucky – the windows are large and the apartment faces south. **Nina:** How many people live here? **Tom:** Three people at the moment. We're looking for a fourth. **Nina:** And how are the rooms divided up? **Tom:** Everyone has their own bedroom. The living room, kitchen, and bathroom are shared. **Nina:** Okay. Can I see my room? **Tom:** Of course! The room is here on the right. It's not very large, but cozy. **Nina:** Hmm. It's a bit dark. Doesn't it have a bigger window? **Tom:** That's the only window. But we air it out regularly – that helps. **Nina:** Is the room furnished? **Tom:** Partially. There's a bed and a wardrobe. You'll need to bring your own desk and chair. **Nina:** That's fine. How much is the rent? **Tom:** Your share of the rent would be four hundred euros. Plus fifty euros for utilities. **Nina:** And the deposit? **Tom:** Two months' rent, so eight hundred euros. **Nina:** When can I move in? **Tom:** You can move in on the first of April. Or earlier, if you like. **Nina:** April is good. I want to move out of my current place and get organized first. **Tom:** No problem. Do you have any questions about the house rules? **Nina:** Yes – am I allowed to renovate or paint the room? **Tom:** You can paint the walls, but only in light colors. That's what the landlord has stipulated. **Nina:** I see. And is there a basement? **Tom:** Yes, every tenant has a storage room in the basement. The basement is clean and practical. **Nina:** Perfect. I think I like the apartment. I'll get back to you by tomorrow. **Tom:** Great. We look forward to your answer. Bye, Nina! **Nina:** Bye, Tom. Thanks for the viewing!

## Dialogue 9: Renovation Work

**Ms. Hoffmann** (landlord) and **Mr. Lang** (tradesman) discuss the renovation.

---

**Ms. Hoffmann:** Good morning, Mr. Lang. Thank you for being able to come so quickly. **Mr. Lang:** Good morning, Ms. Hoffmann. What would you like me to renovate? **Ms. Hoffmann:** The apartment on the first floor. The tenant has moved out. Now I need to renovate the apartment before a new tenant moves in. **Mr. Lang:** I see. What exactly needs to be done? **Ms. Hoffmann:** First, all the walls need to be painted. The paint is old and dirty. **Mr. Lang:** What color should the walls be painted? **Ms. Hoffmann:** White – a bright, modern color. I want the apartment to feel bright and inviting. **Mr. Lang:** Good. What else? **Ms. Hoffmann:** The heating needs to be repaired. It's not working properly. **Mr. Lang:** I'll take a look at the heating. Since when has it had problems? **Ms. Hoffmann:** Since last winter. The old tenant told me too late. **Mr. Lang:** That happens. And the staircase? **Ms. Hoffmann:** The staircase is fine. But the elevator needs servicing. **Mr. Lang:** I can't service the elevator myself – you'll need a specialist for that. **Ms. Hoffmann:** I know. I'll call the elevator technician separately. **Mr. Lang:** Good. And the bathroom? **Ms. Hoffmann:** The shower is dripping. Can you repair that? **Mr. Lang:** Yes, that's no problem. I'll repair the shower and also check the toilet. **Ms. Hoffmann:** Wonderful. How long will the renovation take in total? **Mr. Lang:** For the walls, the heating, and the bathroom I'll need about three days. **Ms. Hoffmann:** Can you start tomorrow? **Mr. Lang:** Yes, I'll start tomorrow morning at eight o'clock. **Ms. Hoffmann:** Good. And what will the renovation cost? **Mr. Lang:** That depends on the materials. I'll give you a detailed quote this afternoon. **Ms. Hoffmann:** Very well. I'll wait for your message. Thank you, Mr. Lang. **Mr. Lang:** My pleasure, Ms. Hoffmann. See you tomorrow! **Ms. Hoffmann:** See you tomorrow. Goodbye!

## Dialogue 10: Saying Goodbye to the Old Apartment

**Clara** is moving out of her apartment. Her landlord **Mr. Bauer** conducts the handover.

---

**Mr. Bauer:** Good day, Ms. Clara. So today is the big day – you're moving out. **Clara:** Yes, Mr. Bauer. It's a strange feeling. I've lived here for five years. **Mr. Bauer:** Five years! That's a long time. We viewed the apartment together back then. **Clara:** Exactly. I still remember it well. The apartment was unfurnished and the walls were dark. **Mr. Bauer:** And then you furnished it beautifully and painted the walls bright. **Clara:** Yes, I invested a lot of time and money. It was my home. **Mr. Bauer:** You can tell. The apartment is in very good condition. I'm satisfied. **Clara:** Thank you. I took good care of everything – cleaned the kitchen, tidied up the basement, regularly checked the heating. **Mr. Bauer:** Very good. Then let's do the official handover now. I'll look at all the rooms. **Clara:** Of course. Let's start with the living room. **Mr. Bauer:** _(looks around)_ Very clean. The walls are impeccable. The parquet too. **Clara:** I cleaned the parquet last week. **Mr. Bauer:** _(in the bedroom)_ Everything in order here too. Nicely bright. You've really taken good care of the apartment. **Clara:** Thank you. And the basement? **Mr. Bauer:** I'll look at the basement afterwards. First the bathroom. **Clara:** I cleaned the bathroom yesterday. The shower and toilet are clean. **Mr. Bauer:** Perfect. Everything looks very good. I'm very satisfied with the condition of the apartment. **Clara:** I'm glad to hear that. Will I get my deposit back? **Mr. Bauer:** Yes, of course. I'll transfer your deposit within two weeks. **Clara:** Wonderful. Thank you, Mr. Bauer. You were always a very kind landlord. **Mr. Bauer:** And you were a very good tenant, Ms. Clara. Where are you moving to now? **Clara:** I'm moving to a larger apartment. I need more space. **Mr. Bauer:** I understand that. All the best to you in the new apartment. **Clara:** Thank you. Here are the keys. _(hands over the keys)_ **Mr. Bauer:** Thank you. And now we'll lock up the apartment. _(locks the door)_ There. That's that. **Clara:** _(sighs)_ Goodbye, old apartment. Goodbye, Mr. Bauer. **Mr. Bauer:** Goodbye, Ms. Clara. All the best!
