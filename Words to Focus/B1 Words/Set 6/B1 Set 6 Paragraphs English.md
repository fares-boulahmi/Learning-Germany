# Deutsch – Set 6: Living and Life

---

My family and I have been living in a spacious apartment in Munich for three years. We rented the apartment in January because we moved from Berlin. The landlord is a nice, older man — he is friendly and punctual. The rent is not cheap, but the apartment is modern and bright. We have a large balcony, a spacious living room, and three bedrooms. My wife finds the kitchen practical because it is large and well-equipped. The utilities — electricity, gas, and water — we pay every month in addition to the rent. The lease runs for two years, and we paid a deposit of three months' rent. On the ground floor there is a small garden, but we rarely use it. Our son sleeps in the smallest room, but he finds it cozy. The staircase is a bit narrow, but there is also an elevator. In the basement we keep our old furniture and the suitcase for vacation. The attic is dark and old-fashioned, but we store our winter clothes there. I am glad that we finally moved into a spacious, bright apartment.

---

Every morning I wake up at six o'clock and get up immediately. I shower, brush my teeth, and have breakfast in the kitchen. Today I have breakfast with my daughter because she has no school. Breakfast is delicious — we eat fresh bread with butter and drink coffee. Afterward I tidy up the kitchen because it is always a little messy after breakfast. My wife cleans the bathroom — she cleans it daily and thoroughly. Today I am repairing the washing machine because it has not been working since yesterday. The repair takes an hour, but afterward it runs well again. At nine o'clock I leave the apartment and go to work. Work starts punctually at half past nine. I work in an office not far from our house. In the evening I come home at six o'clock and have dinner with the family. After dinner we relax in the living room and watch TV. Bedtime at our house always begins at half past ten.

---

Saturday is our big cleaning day. My wife and I clean the entire apartment — we divide the work fairly. I vacuum the living room and the dining room, and she cleans the bathroom and the bedrooms. We clean the kitchen together because it is large. We air out all the rooms because fresh air is important. In winter we heat the apartment with the heating system — it works well and is modern. In summer we air out the rooms in the morning and evening so that the rooms don't get too warm. After cleaning we drink coffee on the balcony. The balcony is cozy — we have two chairs, a small table, and many flowers there. My son tidies up his room, even though he doesn't enjoy it. My daughter helps with the tidying and is very diligent. We are not painting the walls in the hallway today, but we are planning to do that next month. Painting is fun when you do it together. In the afternoon the apartment is clean and tidy. We are all tired but happy.

---

Next month my sister and her husband are moving into a new apartment. They searched for a long time for a spacious, affordable apartment. The new apartment is bright and modern, with a large living room and two bedrooms. They are renting the apartment from a friendly landlord from the neighborhood. The lease was signed last week, and the deposit has already been paid. My sister is very happy because the apartment is unfurnished — she wants to set everything up herself. She is buying new furniture: a large sofa, a table, chairs, and a wardrobe. Her husband is renovating the bedroom — he is painting the walls white and repairing the staircase. The basement is large and practical, ideal for bicycles and suitcases. The elevator in the building is unfortunately often out of order, but the staircase is bright and not too narrow. The utilities — electricity, gas, and water — are not included in the rent. On the weekend we help with the moving and setting up. Setting up is a lot of fun — we spend the whole day doing it. In the evening we all eat pizza together and celebrate the new beginning.

---

I work as a computer scientist in a modern office in the city center. Every morning I take the tram to work — that takes twenty minutes. At the office I start work at nine o'clock. My boss is a friendly, intelligent man — he organizes the work well. We spend a lot of time at the computer and in meetings. At twelve o'clock I eat in the canteen — today there is vegetables with meat, and it tastes delicious. After the lunch break I continue working until half past five. At the end of the workday I am often tired but satisfied. Sometimes I spend the evening with colleagues in a café or restaurant. We talk about work, family, and everyday life. The stress is sometimes great, but I remind myself why I like this job. My colleagues are diligent and creative — we work well together. On Fridays we always plan the schedule for the following week. The weekend officially begins for us at 5 p.m. on Friday. I always look forward to free time.

---

My mother lives in a small, cozy house in a quiet village. The house has belonged to her for thirty years — she has never rented it out. The living room is bright and spacious, with a large sofa and a television. The kitchen is old-fashioned but practical — she cooks there daily for the family. Vegetables grow in the garden, and in summer we often sit on the terrace. The heating in the house is old and will soon need to be repaired or replaced. My father repairs many things himself — he is very diligent and skilled. The staircase leads to the attic, where many old things are stored. The basement is cool and dark — my mother stores wine and canned goods there. The house has no elevator, but that is no problem because it only has two floors. In winter we heat all the rooms well and air them out daily. The trash is collected twice a week. My parents pay no rent because the house belongs to them. I visit them every Sunday — that is our lovely family tradition.

---

Last week we went on vacation — one week in Vienna. We rented a furnished apartment because it is cheaper than a hotel. The apartment was modern, bright, and very cozy. It had a lovely bedroom, a practical kitchen, and a clean bathroom. The utilities were included in the price, and the landlord was very friendly. We quickly unpacked the luggage and settled into the apartment right away — everyone had their own room. On the first day we explored the city and saw many beautiful buildings. The weather was nice — warm and sunny, typical for autumn. We ate in good restaurants and drank delicious Austrian coffee. On the third day we made a trip to Schönbrunn Palace — it was beautiful. On the last evening we cooked in the apartment and relaxed. Packing up the next morning was done quickly. The train to Munich departed punctually at nine o'clock. We returned home tired but very happy.

---

Today I am going shopping — I need many things for the apartment. At the supermarket I buy fresh vegetables, meat, bread, and milk. At the checkout I pay by credit card because I have no cash with me. The bill is higher than usual today because I also buy cleaning supplies. At the hardware store I buy paint for painting the walls and tools for repairing the staircase. The salesperson recommends a white paint for the bedroom and a light color for the hallway. The offer is good value — twenty percent off all paints. I also order a new shower online because the old one is broken. Delivery takes three to five business days. Afterward I go to a café and drink a coffee — I need a short break. In the afternoon I bring everything home and put it away. The shopping took two hours, but everything is done. The fridge is now full and the house is well prepared for the week. I am satisfied and tired — but it is a good feeling.

---

My friend Anna is moving out of her old apartment next month. She has lived there for five years, but now the rent is too expensive. She has found a new, more affordable apartment nearby. The new apartment is smaller, but cozy and practical. Anna still needs to give notice on the old lease and get the deposit back. The landlord must return the deposit within three months. She plans to furnish the new apartment herself — she loves creative decorating. She wants to paint the walls white and buy new, modern furniture. The heating in the new apartment is new and works well. The electricity and gas are included in the utilities — that is practical and affordable. Anna is especially looking forward to the small basement, where she can store her bicycles and sports equipment. The attic is also accessible and bright. We are helping her move on Saturday — that will be exhausting but also fun. After the move we eat together and celebrate her new beginning.

---

This weekend we are renovating our bathroom — it is already old-fashioned and needs a refresh. My husband and I have been planning the renovation for weeks. First we are painting the walls a light, modern blue. The old shower is being replaced with a new, spacious one. The toilet and the sink are staying because they are still in good condition. We air out the bathroom thoroughly so that the paint dries quickly. My husband is also repairing the heating in the bathroom — it no longer heats properly. The repair is complicated, but he is very skilled and gets it done in two hours. In the afternoon we close off the bathroom and wait until everything is dry. On Sunday everything is finished — the bathroom is bright, modern, and cozy. The children are thrilled and want to shower immediately. The renovation did not cost much because we did everything ourselves. We are very satisfied with the result. The house feels fresher and more beautiful after the renovation. Next month we might renovate the kitchen.
