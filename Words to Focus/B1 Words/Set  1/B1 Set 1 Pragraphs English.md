# Mein Alltag und meine Gewohnheiten

## My Daily Life and My Habits

---

## Paragraph 1 – The Morning Begins

Every morning my daily routine begins at seven o'clock. I wake up and get up immediately because I want to be on time. My daily life has many habits, and I never forget them. I shower first — that takes about ten minutes. Then I brush my teeth and go to the kitchen. I have breakfast every morning because breakfast is very important. My habit is to eat tea and bread. My brother also eats with me — his name is Thomas and he is twenty years old. Thomas is hardworking and very punctual. He always starts his day at six thirty. We don't talk much in the morning because we are not yet fully awake. My mother is also in the kitchen — she is very friendly and kind. She has a habit: she drinks coffee every morning. Our house has a large kitchen and a beautiful dining room. I sit at the table on a chair and eat quietly. I plan my day during breakfast — that is my best habit. My schedule starts with work at nine o'clock. I always organize my tasks in the morning. Breakfast lasts fifteen minutes, no more and no less. I sometimes forget an appointment, but rarely. Today I have an important appointment at ten thirty. I always remind myself of it with my phone. My father leaves the house at eight o'clock — he is a doctor and works in a hospital. He is very busy, but he never skips breakfast. Our family has many regular habits. We always eat together in the morning and in the evening. That is a beautiful habit in our family. I am very glad to have such a nice family. The morning is quiet and beautiful in our house. I leave the house at eight forty. Then the actual daily routine begins.

---

## Paragraph 2 – Work and the Schedule

My office is not far from our house. I usually walk — that takes about twenty minutes. I start work at nine o'clock and finish at seven o'clock. My schedule is regular and organized every day. I have many tasks to complete daily. Sometimes the tasks are difficult and take a long time. I always plan my work in the morning before I begin. My boss is a computer scientist — he is intelligent and creative. He organizes our work very well. We have a meeting every Monday at nine fifteen. This meeting lasts about one hour. Sometimes we move the meeting to Tuesday or Wednesday. That happens when someone is late or has another appointment. Punctuality is very important in our office. My colleague Werner is unfortunately often late. He is always stressed and rushes into the office in a hurry. I understand that because the bus is sometimes delayed. I often remind him of our appointments. We take a daily break at twelve o'clock — that is the lunch break. The break lasts thirty minutes. During the break I usually eat a sandwich or go to a restaurant. Sometimes we organize a lunch with the whole team. That happens occasionally and is a lot of fun. After the break I complete my most important tasks. I am then very busy and do not talk much. I finish work punctually at seven o'clock — that is the end of my workday. The end of the workday is the most beautiful moment of the day. Then I am finally free and can relax. My daily life has a lot of stress, but also many beautiful moments.

---

## Paragraph 3 – Free Time and the Weekend

After work my free time begins. I like to relax at home on the sofa. Sometimes I watch television or read a book. My free time is very important to me — it lasts from seven o'clock until midnight. I have many activities during my free time. I like to play with my dog in the garden. Our dog is named Max and is very funny and calm. I also have a cat — she is small and grey. My sister visits me occasionally in the evening. We then spend time together and talk a lot. She is married and has two children — a son and a daughter. The children are seven and nine years old. We sometimes spend the whole evening together. That is a wonderful habit in our family. On the weekend I have more free time. The weekend begins on Friday after work. Saturday and Sunday are my days off. On the weekend I get up later — at nine or ten o'clock. I have a long breakfast and plan nothing special. Sometimes we go out as a family — that is occasional, but very nice. We visit a park or a café. My mother organizes these outings very well. She never forgets to prepare everything. I sometimes help her with that. We always spend the weekend relaxed and cheerfully. Monday comes quickly, unfortunately — then daily life begins again. But I always look forward to the next weekend. Free time is the most important part of my daily life. Without free time there is too much stress.

---

## Paragraph 4 – Stress, Rush, and Punctuality

Sometimes my daily life is very stressful. I then have many appointments and little time. The stress begins when I forget an appointment or am running late. I then hurry and leave the house very quickly. The rush is not good for me — I then forget many things. Once I forgot my phone and had to go back. That caused a delay of twenty minutes. I was then very late for my appointment. My boss had been waiting — he is normally very patient. But delays are never good. Punctuality is very important to me. I always remind myself of my appointments with my phone. My schedule has many appointments and meetings every day. I organize everything very precisely. Sometimes I have to reschedule an appointment — that happens occasionally. A rescheduled appointment is better than being late. My colleague Werner reschedules his appointments often. That sometimes creates stress for the whole team. I then wait for him and lose time. Stress is a big problem in modern daily life. Many people have too much stress every day. I try to reduce my stress. I regularly take a short break in the afternoon. This break only lasts five or ten minutes, but it helps a great deal. I relax during this break and forget the stress. Then I am productive again and complete my tasks faster. Punctuality and good organization help against stress. I start every morning with a clear schedule. That makes my daily life much easier and calmer. Stress is part of daily life, but one can control it.

---

## Paragraph 5 – Habits and the Regular Daily Routine

My daily life has many regular habits. I get up every day at seven o'clock and fall asleep at midnight. I have had this habit for many years. Regular habits make life easier and better. I start every Monday with a weekly schedule. This plan helps me to complete all appointments and tasks. I review my plan every morning. Sometimes I forget something and have to reschedule it. But that is occasional — most of the time I complete everything on time. My family also has many regular habits. We eat together every evening in the dining room. That has been a daily habit for many years. We talk about our daily lives during the meal. My father tells us about his work at the hospital. My mother organizes dinner every day. She cooks very well — I love eating her food. Weekly we go grocery shopping as a family at the supermarket. That is a regular activity on Saturdays. I help my mother with the shopping. We buy groceries for the whole week. Monthly we plan a big family outing. That is always very nice and special. I look forward to it every month. Good habits improve daily life greatly. Without habits, daily life would be chaotic and stressful. I recommend everyone to have a good schedule. Punctuality, organization, and regular breaks are very important. With these habits one can control stress well. I am glad to have such an organized daily life. My daily life is sometimes stressful, but always interesting and beautiful.
