### ⚠️ Notes on Skipped Forms

|Focus Word|Skipped Form|Reason|
|---|---|---|
|**nässen**|ich/du/wir/ihr/sie nässt — most persons beyond 3sg|Rarely used in 1st/2nd person in natural speech; "es nässt" (impersonal) is the natural form|
|**tauen**|Futur I, Präteritum of tauen|Only 2 sentences needed to cover natural usage; Futur I "es wird tauen" is covered implicitly via context|
|**der Schnee**|Genitiv (des Schnees)|Genitive of Schnee is grammatically valid but almost never used in natural spoken German|
|**regnerisch / neblig / wolkig**|Comparative & superlative|These weather adjectives are almost never used in comparative or superlative form in natural German|
|**blitzen / donnern**|1st/2nd person|These are impersonal verbs — they only occur as "es blitzt / es donnert"; using "ich blitze" is ungrammatical|
|**regnen / schneien / tauen**|1st/2nd person|Same as above — strictly impersonal verbs in German|
