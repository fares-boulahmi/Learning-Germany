## Dialogue 1: Apartment Viewing

**Context:** Formal – Potential tenant visits a landlord to view an apartment

**Landlord (Thomas):** Good day! You are Ms. Samir, yes? **Tenant (Lena):** Yes, good day! My name is Lena Samir. How are you? **Thomas:** I'm doing well, thank you. Please come in. The apartment is on the third floor. **Lena:** Thank you. The living room is very bright! I love the large balcony. **Thomas:** Yes, the balcony is very practical. In summer, the sun shines here all day long. **Lena:** Really? And in winter? Is it very cold on the balcony? **Thomas:** In winter, the plants freeze of course. It's cold, but the heating in the apartment is very good. **Lena:** Does the apartment also have a garden? **Thomas:** No garden. But the sky is beautiful to see from here. Especially when it's stormy. **Lena:** Hmm. How is the weather here in spring? **Thomas:** In spring it's mostly warm and sunny. The flowers grow nicely on the balcony. I planted roses last year. **Lena:** Oh, lovely! And the rain? Does it rain often here? **Thomas:** In April it rains regularly. Sometimes there's thunder at night too. **Lena:** That doesn't bother me. I love thunderstorms! The thunder is so dramatic. **Thomas:** Yes, the sky becomes very dark and the wind blows very strongly. **Lena:** How much is the rent, please? **Thomas:** The rent is eight hundred euros per month. Utilities for electricity, gas, and water cost another two hundred euros. **Lena:** And the deposit? **Thomas:** Two months' rent, so sixteen hundred euros. **Lena:** I understand. The room is spacious. Is the kitchen furnished? **Thomas:** Yes, the kitchen has a stove, a refrigerator, and a dishwasher. **Lena:** Perfect. It rained yesterday – is the bathroom dry and modern? **Thomas:** Yes, the bathroom has a shower and a bathtub. It's completely renovated. **Lena:** I find the apartment very cozy. I'd like to read the rental agreement. **Thomas:** Of course. When do you have time? Tomorrow or the day after? **Lena:** Tomorrow, Wednesday, works well for me. **Thomas:** Very good! Goodbye, Ms. Samir. **Lena:** Goodbye!

## Dialogue 2: At the Doctor

**Context:** Formal – Patient visits a doctor

**Doctor (Dr. Hoffmann):** Good morning! What is your name, please? **Patient (Ali):** Good morning, Doctor. My name is Ali Yilmaz. **Dr. Hoffmann:** How old are you, Mr. Yilmaz? **Ali:** I am thirty-three years old. **Dr. Hoffmann:** And how are you? What are your complaints? **Ali:** I'm not doing well. I feel very weak and tired. I'm also coughing a lot. **Dr. Hoffmann:** Since when have you had the cough? **Ali:** Since Monday. It rained a lot over the weekend and I was outside without a jacket. **Dr. Hoffmann:** Ah, the wet weather! Do you also have a fever? **Ali:** Yes, I think so. And I have a strong headache. **Dr. Hoffmann:** Have you been sneezing? **Ali:** Yes, I've been sneezing a lot. Especially in the mornings, when the temperature outside is cold. **Dr. Hoffmann:** Was it very cold this week? **Ali:** Yes! It was foggy and the wind was blowing very strongly. I even froze on the way to work. **Dr. Hoffmann:** That sounds like the flu. I'll examine you now. Please breathe deeply. **Ali:** Does rain make people sick? **Dr. Hoffmann:** The rain itself doesn't make you sick. But when you get wet and it's cold outside, the immune system becomes weak. **Ali:** And today it's still stormy outside! The thunder woke me up last night. **Dr. Hoffmann:** Yes, the thunderstorm was strong last night. I'll prescribe you tablets. Take one tablet twice a day. **Ali:** How long do I have to take the medication? **Dr. Hoffmann:** Seven days. And stay home when it's rainy and cold. **Ali:** Yes, Doctor. When can I go back to work? **Dr. Hoffmann:** In four or five days, when you're healthy. Drink plenty of water. **Ali:** Thank you very much, Doctor. Goodbye. **Dr. Hoffmann:** Get well soon, Mr. Yilmaz! Goodbye.

## Dialogue 3: At a Restaurant

**Context:** Informal + Formal mixed – Two friends having lunch and interacting with a waiter

**Waiter (Max):** Good day! My name is Max. What would you like to order? **Friend 1 (Sara):** Good day! What do you recommend today? **Waiter Max:** I recommend the tomato soup. The tomatoes are fresh – we picked them from the garden today. **Friend 2 (Julia):** Oh, delicious! Sara, what are you having? **Sara:** I'll have the soup. And a green salad. But I'm wondering – does the salad taste sweet or sour? **Waiter Max:** The salad tastes fresh and slightly sour. With a lemon seasoning. **Julia:** I'll order the chicken with vegetables, please. **Waiter Max:** Very good. And what would you like to drink? **Sara:** Two glasses of water, please. **Waiter Max:** Right away. _(walks away)_ **Julia:** Sara, did you see the thunderstorm yesterday? The lightning was so bright! **Sara:** Yes! The lightning lit up the sky. I was trembling with fear. **Julia:** Really? I love thunderstorms. The rain afterwards makes nature so green and fresh. **Sara:** The flowers really do grow better after rain. Did you see the roses in your garden? **Julia:** Yes, they've been blooming since last week. But yesterday the wind almost broke them. **Sara:** It was very windy and stormy. The sky was cloudy all day. **Julia:** But today it's sunny! The sun is shining so beautifully. **Sara:** Yes! And warm. The temperature is over twenty degrees today. **Waiter Max:** _(comes back)_ Here is the tomato soup and the salad for you. And the chicken for you. **Julia:** Thank you! The chicken smells wonderful. **Waiter Max:** Enjoy your meal! **Sara:** _(tries the soup)_ Mmm! The soup is delicious and not too spicy. **Julia:** Great. What are you doing this weekend? **Sara:** I'd like to work in the garden. I want to plant new flowers. **Julia:** Good idea! And if it rains? **Sara:** Then I'll stay home and cook. How much do you think lunch costs here? **Julia:** Not much. It's affordable here. I love coming to this restaurant. **Waiter Max:** Would you like a dessert? **Sara:** No thank you, I'm full. The bill, please. **Waiter Max:** Of course. That comes to thirty euros altogether. **Julia:** I'll pay by credit card. Is that alright? **Waiter Max:** Yes, of course. Goodbye!

## Dialogue 4: Meeting Friends in the Park

**Context:** Informal – Two old friends meet in a park

**Tobias:** Hey Malik! Bye, I mean – hello! Ha! How are you? **Malik:** Tobias! I'm doing great! And you? **Tobias:** I'm doing well too. What a beautiful day! The sun is shining so strongly today. **Malik:** Yes, it's so sunny and warm. Nature here in the park is wonderful. **Tobias:** Look at the trees! They're all blooming. Spring is finally here. **Malik:** I love spring. After the cold winter, everything grows again. **Tobias:** Did you see the snow last week? It snowed so much! **Malik:** Yes! Snow was everywhere. The streets were white and slippery. **Tobias:** And then it thawed – water was everywhere. **Malik:** Exactly. But now everything has dried out. Today the sky is so blue and clear. **Tobias:** No fog, no cloud! It's perfect. **Malik:** Did you experience the thunderstorm on Wednesday? The thunder was very loud! **Tobias:** Oh yes! The lightning shone so brightly. I was trembling. **Malik:** I watched the thunderstorm from my living room. It was stormy but beautiful. **Tobias:** After the rain, the flowers bloomed so wonderfully! **Malik:** That's true. Look – these red flowers here are beautiful. **Tobias:** Do you have a cat? I saw that you have a dog. **Malik:** No, I don't have a dog anymore. Now I have a cat. Her name is Luna. **Tobias:** Cool. Does Luna also like to go out when it's windy? **Malik:** No, she prefers to stay inside when it's stormy or rainy. But on sunny days she sits on the balcony. **Tobias:** Ha! Me too! When do we have time for a coffee? **Malik:** Friday? The weather should stay warm this week. **Tobias:** Great! Friday works for me. At what time? **Malik:** At ten o'clock? At the café on the market square? **Tobias:** Perfect. Until Friday then! **Malik:** Until then! Bye, Tobias!

## Dialogue 5: Shopping

**Context:** Formal – Customer and shop assistant

**Sales assistant (Nina):** Good day! How can I help you? **Customer (Peter):** Good day. I'm looking for a jacket for the stormy autumn weather. **Nina:** Ah, yes! Autumn has become very cool and windy already. Come, we have many jackets here. **Peter:** I need something warm. It has already been freezing this week. **Nina:** This blue jacket here is very warm. It's perfect for cold and rainy weather. **Peter:** How much does the jacket cost? **Nina:** It costs ninety euros. But we have an offer – twenty percent discount this week. **Peter:** So it costs seventy-two euros. That's affordable. Can I try it on? **Nina:** Of course! The changing room is over there. **Peter:** _(comes back)_ The jacket fits well. But do you have it in black too? **Nina:** Yes, let me check. _(searches)_ Here it is in black! **Peter:** Perfect. The sky was so grey and cloudy today – a black jacket suits it well. **Nina:** True. The weather is very foggy today. Have you seen a rain jacket? **Peter:** No. Do you have one? **Nina:** Yes, here. This jacket protects you from the rain. It's also wind- and waterproof. **Peter:** Very practical! The rain got me completely soaked last week. **Nina:** That was the big thunderstorm, yes? The thunder and lightning were very strong! **Peter:** Yes! I was outside without an umbrella. I froze afterwards. **Nina:** I'll take both jackets. How much is the rain jacket? **Nina:** It costs sixty euros. Without discount, as it's new. **Peter:** Good. I'll pay with cash. **Nina:** That comes to one hundred and thirty euros altogether, please. **Peter:** Here you go. Thank you! **Nina:** You're welcome. Goodbye and stay dry! **Peter:** Ha! Thank you! Goodbye!

## Dialogue 6: At the Workplace

**Context:** Informal – Two colleagues talking in the office

**Colleague 1 (Stefan):** Good morning, Yara! How was your weekend? **Colleague (Yara):** Good morning, Stefan! I'm doing well, thank you. The weekend was nice, but very rainy. **Stefan:** Yes, it rained the whole weekend! I stayed home. **Yara:** Me too. I watched TV and relaxed. But on Sunday the sun briefly came out. **Stefan:** Really? At my place the sky was cloudy and grey all day long. **Yara:** The fog in the morning was so thick! I could barely see the trees outside. **Stefan:** And the wind! It was so stormy. My balcony was full of leaves from the trees. **Yara:** Did you water your flowers before the rain? **Stefan:** No, I forgot. But the rain watered them! They're growing very nicely now. **Yara:** Ha! Rain is sometimes useful. Did you prepare for the two o'clock meeting today? **Stefan:** Yes, everything done. The boss is coming at two. I was on time with the preparation. **Yara:** Good. I've finished my tasks too. It was a stressful week. **Stefan:** Very stressful. I worked until eight in the evening yesterday. **Yara:** I also worked until late. No after-work time, no relaxing. **Stefan:** Look, it's snowing outside! The first snow! **Yara:** What?! _(looks out the window)_ Oh wow, the snow is falling so beautifully. The trees are already white. **Stefan:** In autumn it sometimes freezes, but snow already in October is rare. **Yara:** The temperature is very cold today. Did you close the window? **Stefan:** Yes, I aired out the room and then closed it. It was cool in the office. **Yara:** The coffee is warming me up. Do you want one too? **Stefan:** Yes, gladly! Thank you, Yara. Break until eleven? **Yara:** Yes, until eleven. And then we keep working!

## Dialogue 7: Train Station

**Context:** Formal – Traveler and ticket officer

**Traveler (Emma):** Good day! I'd like to buy a ticket to Munich, please. **Staff member (Klaus):** Good day! When would you like to travel? **Emma:** Today, if possible. What time does the next train leave? **Klaus:** The next train leaves at thirteen thirty. But there may be a delay. **Emma:** Why a delay? **Klaus:** Last night's thunderstorm damaged the tracks. The thunder and lightning were very strong. **Emma:** Oh! Was the thunderstorm that bad? **Klaus:** Yes, the storm threw trees onto the tracks. It has been stormy since yesterday. **Emma:** How long is the delay? **Klaus:** About twenty minutes. The sky is still cloudy and it's continuing to rain. **Emma:** And the wind? It's very windy today. **Klaus:** Yes, the wind is blowing very strongly. Please be careful on the platform. **Emma:** Thank you for the information. I'd like a ticket – one way or return? **Klaus:** That's your decision! A return ticket is cheaper. **Emma:** Then a return ticket, please. Second class. **Klaus:** That comes to fifty-two euros, please. **Emma:** I'll pay by credit card. Here you go. **Klaus:** Thank you. Here is your receipt. Platform seven, please. **Emma:** Is the station open when it snows? **Klaus:** Yes, always. Even when it's very cold and snowing, the trains run. **Emma:** Good to know. It snowed last week and I missed the train. **Klaus:** That was the big snowstorm! The temperature was minus ten degrees. **Emma:** Yes, very cold! Thank you for your help. Goodbye! **Klaus:** Have a good journey! Goodbye!

## Dialogue 8: At a Café

**Context:** Informal – Two students meeting to study

**Student 1 (Finn):** Hey Amina! Hi! Were you waiting for me? **Amina:** Hello Finn! Yes, I've been waiting ten minutes already. But it's fine. Sit down! **Finn:** Sorry, the bus was delayed. The weather is so rainy – everyone is driving slower. **Amina:** True. It rained all morning today. Look, the sky is still so dark. **Finn:** But at least it's warm here in the café. What did you order? **Amina:** A hot tea. It's cold outside and I was freezing. **Finn:** Yes, I'll order a coffee. The wind almost knocked me over today! **Amina:** Was it that stormy? **Finn:** Yes! The trees were bending in the wind. A flower from my balcony fell down. **Amina:** Oh no! Do you have flowers on the balcony? **Finn:** Yes, I planted many flowers in summer. They're still blooming, but the storm is making them wilt. **Amina:** What a shame. In summer the weather was so beautiful and sunny. **Finn:** And hot! The temperature was sometimes over thirty degrees. **Amina:** I loved the summer. The sun was shining so warmly. **Finn:** Now it's cool and foggy. Autumn is really here. **Amina:** Did you prepare the lecture about the weather in Germany? **Finn:** Yes! Germany has very varied weather. In the north it's often windy and rainy. In the south there's more snow in winter. **Amina:** Interesting. How is the weather in Tunisia, where I come from? **Finn:** Warmer, I think! Less rain and more sun? **Amina:** Exactly! But I sometimes miss the thunderstorms here. In Tunisia it rarely thunders. **Finn:** Really? I find thunderstorms sometimes scary. The lightning frightens me. **Amina:** Ha, I love the thunder! Shall we start studying now? **Finn:** Yes, we should hurry. The exam is tomorrow!

## Dialogue 9: At the Supermarket

**Context:** Formal – Customer and store employee

**Customer (Laila):** Excuse me! Where can I find fresh vegetables? **Employee (Georg):** Good day! The vegetables are in aisle three, on the left side. **Laila:** Thank you. And do you have fresh flowers? **Georg:** Yes, the flowers are at the entrance. We received new flowers today – they are blooming very beautifully. **Laila:** Oh, wonderful. I love planting flowers in my garden. **Georg:** How lovely! Was the weather good for the garden this week? **Laila:** Yes and no. It rained a lot, but the sun also shone often. **Georg:** Rain is good for the flowers. They grow better afterwards. **Laila:** True. But yesterday's thunderstorm was too strong. The lightning struck a tree in my garden. **Georg:** That's dangerous! Was the thunder very loud? **Laila:** Very loud! And the wind made many flowers wilt. **Georg:** That's a shame. Are you buying new flowers here? **Laila:** Yes, I'll take these red and these yellow flowers. How much do they cost? **Georg:** The red flowers cost five euros, the yellow ones four euros. **Laila:** Good. And the vegetables – are the tomatoes fresh? **Georg:** Yes, very fresh! The tomatoes come from a local garden. The farmer delivered them today. **Laila:** Great. I'll take one kilo of tomatoes and potatoes too. **Georg:** Do you need anything else? **Laila:** Yes – do you have sunscreen? It was very hot last week. **Georg:** Ha, yes! The sun was shining very strongly. The sunscreen is in aisle one. **Laila:** Thank you. The weather was really hot – the temperature was over thirty-five degrees. **Georg:** In summer it's always hot here. But autumn is coming soon. It will become cool and cloudy. **Laila:** Yes, I've already noticed – it's already cooler in the mornings. Thank you for your help! **Georg:** You're welcome! Goodbye!

## Dialogue 10: Moving into a New Apartment

**Context:** Informal – Two friends helping with a move

**Friend 1 (Leon):** Hello Nora! Are you ready? We're starting the move today! **Nora:** Hello Leon! Yes, I'm so excited! But look at the weather – it's raining so hard. **Leon:** Yes, unfortunately it's raining. Do you have an umbrella? **Nora:** Yes, but the wind is blowing so strongly – the umbrella barely helps! **Leon:** It's stormy today. Not ideal for moving! Did it rain all night? **Nora:** Yes! And there was thunder too. The thunder woke me up at three o'clock. **Leon:** Oh no. Was the lightning also visible? **Nora:** Yes, the lightning lit up the whole room! It was a real thunderstorm. **Leon:** And now it's still raining. We need to protect the furniture well. **Nora:** Right. I have plastic wrap for the wardrobe and the bed. **Leon:** Well planned! Did you also wrap the sofa? **Nora:** Yes! Everything prepared. Leon, how is the weather tomorrow? **Leon:** Tomorrow it should be sunny! The temperature will rise to twenty degrees. **Nora:** Great! Then we can open the windows and air the place out. **Leon:** Yes, and we can tidy up the garden. Does the new apartment have a garden? **Nora:** Yes! A small garden with trees and flowers. The flowers are still blooming. **Leon:** Lovely. In spring you'll be able to plant many new flowers. **Nora:** Yes! And I'll water them regularly. When it's not raining, of course. Ha! **Leon:** Ha! Good. Let's get started. I'll carry the dresser. **Nora:** I'll take the boxes with the kitchen things. The stove comes last. **Leon:** Alright. Thank you for calling me, Nora. **Nora:** Thank you for helping! Without you I'd be lost. Goodbye, old apartment! **Leon:** Ha! Welcome to your new apartment!
