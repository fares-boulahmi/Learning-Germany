## Why File 2 Wins for Each of Your Three Goals

### 🎓 Goethe B1 Exam

The Goethe B1 exam tests your ability to **handle real situations independently** — not just greet people or name furniture. File 2 covers exactly what the exam focuses on:

- **Set 19** (Opinions & Arguments) — the exam has a speaking section where you must express and defend opinions
- **Set 26** (Rules & Obligations) — _müssen, sollen, brauchen_ in context, which are heavily tested
- **Set 35** (Agreement & Disagreement) — critical for the discussion/dialogue tasks
- **Set 23** (Time & Duration) — tested in every written and reading section

File 1 is strong at A1–A2 level (greetings, colors, body parts, days of the week) but those are **not what decides your B1 score**.

---

### 🔧 Ausbildung Contract Context

When dealing with German vocational training paperwork and interviews, you need:

- **Set 9** — Bewerbung, Lebenslauf, Vorstellungsgespräch, Kündigung, Vertrag → _directly what you'll sign and discuss_
- **Set 17** (Law & Administration) — Antrag, Genehmigung, Vorschrift, Versicherung → _bureaucratic onboarding vocabulary_
- **Set 26** (Rules & Obligations) — understanding what you're _required_ vs _allowed_ to do in a contract
- **Set 34** (Planning & Organization) — workplace daily communication

File 1's work sets (28–30) are shallow by comparison — they list job titles and basic office words but don't build the **functional language** you need to actually navigate an Ausbildung environment.

---

### ✈️ Living and Integrating in Germany

File 2 covers daily survival far more practically:

- **Set 3** (Shopping) — includes VAT, returns, sold out, good value — real shopping situations
- **Set 6** (Housing) — rental contracts, deposits, heating costs, moving in/out — exactly what you'll face
- **Set 16** (Environment) — waste separation (_Mülltrennung_) is a real civic expectation in Germany
- **Set 13** (Relationships & Society) — integrating socially, not just naming family members
