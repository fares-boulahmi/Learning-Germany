# German Dialogues – Set 5: Travel & Transportation (English Version)

## Dialogue 1: At the Train Station

**Lena:** Good morning! Excuse me, I'm looking for the platform for the train to Munich. **Markus:** Good morning! The train to Munich departs from platform seven. **Lena:** Thank you! When does the train leave? **Markus:** It leaves at nine thirty. You still have twenty minutes. **Lena:** Good. I already booked my ticket. I reserved it online. **Markus:** Very good. Is it a one-way ticket or round trip? **Lena:** Round trip. I'm traveling to Munich today and coming back tomorrow. **Markus:** Do you have a lot of luggage? **Lena:** Yes, I have a large suitcase and a small bag. **Markus:** The train has comfortable seats. You can put the suitcase up top. **Lena:** Perfect. I love traveling by train. It's more comfortable than the plane. **Markus:** Yes, the train station is also right in the city center. That's convenient. **Lena:** True. Last year I flew by plane, but the airport was very far away. **Markus:** I rarely fly too. I prefer traveling by train or by bus. **Lena:** Where are you traveling today? **Markus:** I'm going to Berlin. My train leaves at ten o'clock. **Lena:** Nice! I've never traveled to Berlin. Is it a long journey? **Markus:** The trip takes about four hours. It's a direct train – no transfers. **Lena:** That's comfortable! Last time I had to transfer twice. That was very stressful. **Markus:** Yes, direct trains are much more pleasant. Have you already planned your trip? **Lena:** Yes, I'm visiting my aunt. We want to explore the city together. **Markus:** Lovely. Have a good trip! Goodbye! **Lena:** Thank you, you too! Goodbye!

## Dialogue 2: At the Travel Agency

**Sofia:** Good day! I'd like to book a vacation. Can you help me? **Mr. Bauer:** Good day! Of course. Where would you like to travel? **Sofia:** I'd like to travel to Spain. I've never been there. **Mr. Bauer:** Very nice. Would you like to book a flight or travel by bus? **Sofia:** I prefer flying. Can you book a flight from Frankfurt Airport? **Mr. Bauer:** Yes, certainly. When would you like to depart? **Sofia:** I'd like to leave in July. Perhaps on the fifteenth of July. **Mr. Bauer:** One moment, let me check... The fifteenth of July is unfortunately fully booked. **Sofia:** Oh no. What about the sixteenth? **Mr. Bauer:** The sixteenth is still available. There are still two tickets. **Sofia:** Good. I'm traveling with my husband. We need two round-trip tickets. **Mr. Bauer:** When would you like to return? **Sofia:** On the twenty-fifth of July. We're staying ten nights in Barcelona. **Mr. Bauer:** Have you already reserved a hotel? **Sofia:** No, not yet. Can you recommend a hotel for us? **Mr. Bauer:** Yes, I recommend the Hotel Central. It's located right next to the train station and is very convenient. **Sofia:** How much does the hotel cost? **Mr. Bauer:** Eighty euros per night. That's a good price for Barcelona. **Sofia:** That's affordable. We'll book the hotel too. We'll pack our suitcases next week. **Mr. Bauer:** Don't forget your passport! You'll need it at the airport. **Sofia:** Of course! I always pack on time. I never forget anything. **Mr. Bauer:** Very good. I'll prepare your tickets. Will you come back tomorrow? **Sofia:** Yes, I'll come back tomorrow at ten o'clock. Thank you for your help! **Mr. Bauer:** You're welcome! Goodbye and enjoy your vacation!

## Dialogue 3: At the Airport

**Tim:** Excuse me, where is the bus stop for the bus to the city center? **Anna:** Hello! The stop is outside, in front of the terminal. The bus runs every twenty minutes. **Tim:** Thank you. I just arrived. My flight was delayed. **Anna:** That's annoying. Where did you travel from? **Tim:** I flew in from London. The plane was two hours late. **Anna:** Oh, that's a long time. I'm flying to London next week too. **Tim:** Really? London is lovely. I was there for work. **Anna:** I'm traveling for vacation. I've been planning the trip for three months already. **Tim:** Have you already booked your ticket? **Anna:** Yes, I've booked everything – flight and hotel. I reserved a direct flight. **Tim:** Good. Direct flights are more comfortable. Do you have to transfer? **Anna:** No, thankfully not. The flight goes directly from Munich to London. **Tim:** How long is the flight? **Anna:** About two hours. I'm packing my suitcase this evening. **Tim:** What are you taking with you? **Anna:** Warm clothes! In London it's often cold and rainy. **Tim:** True. Luggage was also my problem – my suitcase was too heavy. **Anna:** Oh no! What happened? **Tim:** I had to take things out of my suitcase at the airport. That was very stressful. **Anna:** I know that feeling! I always pack too much. **Tim:** Me too. But you learn from experience. Next time I'll travel with less luggage. **Anna:** Good idea. Oh, there's my bus! I have to hurry. **Tim:** Goodbye! Have a good flight next week! **Anna:** Thanks! And welcome to Munich! Bye!

## Dialogue 4: On the Train

**Jonas:** Excuse me, is this seat next to you free? **Mrs. Klein:** Yes, the seat is free. Please sit down. **Jonas:** Thank you. I'm very tired. I started my journey today at five o'clock in the morning. **Mrs. Klein:** That's early! Where are you going? **Jonas:** I'm going to Hamburg. This is my first trip there. **Mrs. Klein:** I'm going to Hamburg too. I travel there every month for work. **Jonas:** How long does the journey take? **Mrs. Klein:** About three hours. The train departed on time today. **Jonas:** Good. I was nervous about missing the train. I almost arrived late. **Mrs. Klein:** That sometimes happens to me too. The station is large and I don't always find the platform right away. **Jonas:** Exactly! Today I was looking for platform four and went to platform fourteen. **Mrs. Klein:** That's a classic mistake! Do you have your ticket with you? **Jonas:** Yes, here it is. I have a round-trip ticket. I'm coming back on Friday. **Mrs. Klein:** Me too. Are you staying overnight in Hamburg? **Jonas:** Yes, I'm staying three nights at a hotel. I'd also like to explore the city. **Mrs. Klein:** Very nice. Hamburg has a beautiful harbor. I recommend a trip there. **Jonas:** That sounds good. I like exploring a city best on foot. **Mrs. Klein:** Me too. Last month I explored Hamburg on foot. It was beautiful. **Jonas:** Do you always take the train to Hamburg? **Mrs. Klein:** Yes, always. I never fly for such a short trip. **Jonas:** I understand that. The train is more comfortable and you don't have to be at the airport so early. **Mrs. Klein:** Exactly. Would you like a coffee? I'm just heading to the dining car. **Jonas:** Yes, please! Thank you. That's very kind.

## Dialogue 5: Planning a Vacation

**Mia:** Hi, Finn! Have you planned your summer vacation yet? **Finn:** Hi, Mia! Not yet. I want to take a big trip this summer. **Mia:** Where would you like to go? **Finn:** I'm deciding between Japan and Australia. What do you think? **Mia:** I was in Japan last year. The trip was fantastic! **Finn:** Really? Did you fly? **Mia:** Yes, I booked a flight from Berlin to Tokyo. I flew directly. **Finn:** How long did the flight take? **Mia:** The flight took about twelve hours. I slept a lot. **Finn:** That's long. I prefer a comfortable plane. **Mia:** The plane was very comfortable. I had a window seat. **Finn:** Did you book everything yourself or did you go to a travel agency? **Mia:** I booked everything myself online – flight, hotel, and the excursions. **Finn:** I prefer booking at the travel agency. They help me with the organizing. **Mia:** That works well too. Do you have a budget? Japan can be expensive. **Finn:** I'm saving money right now. I'd like to spend around two thousand euros. **Mia:** That's enough for Japan. I recommend booking early. Summer dates get fully booked quickly. **Finn:** I know. I need to hurry. Which cities did you visit in Japan? **Mia:** I explored Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka. Each city was different. **Finn:** That sounds great. Did you bring a lot of luggage? **Mia:** No, just a small suitcase. You can buy everything in Japan. **Finn:** Good idea. I always pack too much. **Mia:** Me too! But for Japan I packed on time and organized well. **Finn:** Thanks for the tips! I'll call the travel agency tomorrow. **Mia:** Good luck! You won't forget the trip. Bye!

## Dialogue 6: At the Tram Stop

**Laila:** Excuse me, does this tram go to the main train station? **Mr. Vogel:** No, this line doesn't go there directly. You have to transfer. **Laila:** Oh. Where do I have to transfer? **Mr. Vogel:** You transfer at the Marktplatz stop. There you take line four. **Laila:** I see. How long does the trip to the main station take? **Mr. Vogel:** With the transfer, about fifteen minutes. **Laila:** Good. I can't miss my train. It leaves at one o'clock. **Mr. Vogel:** Then you still have enough time. It's only twelve twenty. **Laila:** I almost ran late this morning too. I missed the bus. **Mr. Vogel:** I know the feeling. Last Tuesday I also arrived at the station too late. **Laila:** What happened? **Mr. Vogel:** The tram was delayed. I missed my train and had to wait. **Laila:** That's frustrating. Where are you going today? **Mr. Vogel:** I'm going to Vienna. I'm visiting my son there. **Laila:** Vienna is a beautiful city! I traveled there as a child. **Mr. Vogel:** Yes, I always enjoy exploring Vienna. Next week we want to take a day trip together. **Laila:** Where to? **Mr. Vogel:** We want to go to Salzburg. From Vienna it's a short trip. **Laila:** Nice. By train or by bus? **Mr. Vogel:** By train. The train is more comfortable and runs directly. **Laila:** That's true. Oh, the tram is coming! **Mr. Vogel:** Yes! Get on. Don't forget: Marktplatz – line four. **Laila:** Thank you very much! Goodbye! **Mr. Vogel:** Have a good trip! Goodbye!

## Dialogue 7: At the Hotel

**Tobias:** Good evening. I have a reservation. My name is Tobias Braun. **Receptionist:** Good evening, Mr. Braun! Let me check quickly... Yes, you're staying three nights with us. **Tobias:** Correct. I just arrived from abroad. My flight was very delayed. **Receptionist:** I'm sorry to hear that. Did you fly directly? **Tobias:** No, I had to transfer in Frankfurt. It was a very long journey. **Receptionist:** That sounds exhausting. We have a quiet room reserved for you. **Tobias:** Wonderful. Is there a parking space? I also have a rental car. **Receptionist:** Yes, the garage is right next to the hotel. You can park the car there. **Tobias:** Thank you. I also have a large suitcase and lots of luggage. **Receptionist:** No problem. The porter will help you with the luggage. **Tobias:** Very good. I travel often for work. I stay in a hotel almost every week. **Receptionist:** Then you know traveling well. Have you eaten yet? **Tobias:** No, I'm hungry. The airplane food wasn't good. **Receptionist:** Our restaurant is still open. I recommend the menu this evening. **Tobias:** Thank you. I'll go straight to the restaurant after checking in. **Receptionist:** Here is your key. Room three hundred ten on the third floor. **Tobias:** Thank you. I have one more question: When does the first bus to the train station run? **Receptionist:** At seven o'clock in the morning. The stop is right in front of the hotel. **Tobias:** Perfect. I need to catch a train early tomorrow morning. **Receptionist:** Shall I wake you at six o'clock? **Tobias:** Yes, please. That would be very kind. Good night! **Receptionist:** Good night, Mr. Braun! Rest well!

## Dialogue 8: Organizing a Day Trip

**Clara:** Good morning, Ben! Do you feel like going on a day trip today? **Ben:** Good morning! Yes, I'd love to. Where would you like to go? **Clara:** I'd like to visit the castle nearby. I've heard about it. **Ben:** Great idea! How are we getting there? By bus or by tram? **Clara:** By bus. The stop is five minutes from here. **Ben:** Do we have tickets? I didn't buy a ticket yesterday. **Clara:** I booked two tickets online. Don't worry. **Ben:** Great! You're always so organized. When does the bus leave? **Clara:** It leaves at ten thirty. We need to hurry a little. **Ben:** Okay. I'll quickly pack my backpack. What should I bring? **Clara:** Water, something to eat, and a warm jacket. It's supposed to be cold today. **Ben:** Good. How long does the trip take? **Clara:** The bus ride is one hour. We explore the castle for two hours and then head back. **Ben:** Perfect. Have you visited the castle before? **Clara:** No, I've never been there. I love traveling to new places. **Ben:** Me too. Last month I went on a day trip to Heidelberg. **Clara:** Oh, that's lovely! How was the trip? **Ben:** Very nice. I took the train and arrived right at the station. **Clara:** Was it a direct train or did you have to transfer? **Ben:** I had to transfer once. But that wasn't a problem. **Clara:** Let's go! The bus leaves in ten minutes. I don't want to miss it. **Ben:** I'm coming! Wait a moment, I'll lock up the apartment. **Clara:** Hurry up! Bye! **Ben:** I'm here! Let's go. This is going to be a great day trip!

## Dialogue 9: Train Delay

**Emma:** Excuse me! Do you know why the train to Cologne hasn't left yet? **Stefan:** Good day. Unfortunately the train is delayed. There's a technical problem. **Emma:** Oh no. How long do we have to wait? **Stefan:** The announcement said: about thirty minutes delay. **Emma:** That's bad. I need to catch another train in Cologne. **Stefan:** Do you have to transfer? **Emma:** Yes, I'm continuing on to Düsseldorf. The connecting train leaves at three o'clock. **Stefan:** That could be tight. Have you called the station? **Emma:** Not yet. I don't know what to do. **Stefan:** I recommend asking at the counter. You might be able to reserve the connecting train. **Emma:** Good idea. Are you also traveling to Cologne? **Stefan:** No, I'm going to Dortmund. I travel there every Monday for work. **Emma:** Have you experienced delays often? **Stefan:** Unfortunately yes. Last week my train was an hour late. **Emma:** That's very stressful when you have an appointment. **Stefan:** I always plan with an extra hour now. That way I don't miss my connection. **Emma:** That's clever. I'll do the same next time. **Stefan:** Good day! I have a question about the delay on platform three. **Conductor:** The train will depart in twenty minutes. We apologize for the delay. **Emma:** Thank you. I hope I still make my connection in Cologne. **Stefan:** Good luck! I've got my fingers crossed. **Emma:** Thank you! Goodbye and have a good trip!

## Dialogue 10: Return from Vacation

**Nico:** Hi, Jana! You're back! How was your vacation? **Jana:** Hi, Nico! Fantastic! I arrived yesterday evening. **Nico:** Where have you been? **Jana:** I traveled to Portugal. I spent ten days in Lisbon. **Nico:** Beautiful! Did you fly? **Jana:** Yes, I booked a direct flight from Munich Airport. It was very comfortable. **Nico:** Did you visit a lot of sights? **Jana:** Yes! I explored the city every day. The old town was especially beautiful. **Nico:** Did you also take any excursions? **Jana:** Yes, I went on a day trip to Sintra. I traveled by train. **Nico:** How was the journey there? **Jana:** The train departed on time and arrived on schedule. It was a direct train. **Nico:** Sounds perfect. Did you bring a lot of luggage? **Jana:** Just a medium-sized suitcase. I packed light this time. **Nico:** Well organized! I always bring too much. My suitcase is always too heavy. **Jana:** Me too, before! But I've learned to pack less. **Nico:** What did you do at the airport? **Jana:** I arrived early, checked in, and then waited in the lounge. **Nico:** Was the return flight on time? **Jana:** Almost. It was ten minutes late. But that was fine. **Nico:** Are you already planning the next trip? **Jana:** Yes! I'd like to travel to Scandinavia next winter. I've already been gathering ideas. **Nico:** Cool! Are you taking the train or flying? **Jana:** I'll probably fly. The train journey would be too long. **Nico:** True. I might come along! When are you booking? **Jana:** In September. By then the tickets are still affordable and not fully booked. **Nico:** Perfect. Then we'll plan together! Bye for now! **Jana:** Yes, I'd love that! See you soon, Nico!
