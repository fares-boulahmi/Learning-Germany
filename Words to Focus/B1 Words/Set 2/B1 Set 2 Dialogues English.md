# German Dialogues – Health & Everyday Situations (English Version)

## Dialogue 1: At the Doctor's

**Anna:** Good morning, Doctor. I'm Anna Becker. **Dr. Müller:** Good morning, Mrs. Becker. How are you feeling today? **Anna:** I'm not well. I feel very weak and tired. **Dr. Müller:** Since when have you been feeling this way? **Anna:** Since Monday. I also have a severe headache. **Dr. Müller:** Do you have a fever? **Anna:** Yes, I have a fever. Yesterday it was 39 degrees. **Dr. Müller:** I'll examine you now. Please breathe in deeply. **Anna:** Is it serious? Am I sick? **Dr. Müller:** You have a cold, possibly the flu. The illness is contagious. **Anna:** What should I take? **Dr. Müller:** I'll prescribe you tablets for the pain and for the fever. **Anna:** How long will the cold last? **Dr. Müller:** About a week. After that you'll recover. Please sleep a lot and drink water. **Anna:** Thank you, Doctor. Goodbye. **Dr. Müller:** Goodbye, Mrs. Becker. Get well soon!

## Dialogue 2: At the Pharmacy

**Lena:** Good day. I need medicine for a headache. **Pharmacist:** Good day. Is it a severe or mild headache? **Lena:** Very severe. The pain started this morning. **Pharmacist:** Have you ever had an allergic reaction to medication? **Lena:** No, I have no allergies. **Pharmacist:** Good. I recommend these tablets. Take two tablets with water. **Lena:** When should I take the tablets? **Pharmacist:** Three times daily – morning, noon, and evening. **Lena:** How long should I take the medication? **Pharmacist:** No longer than five days. If the pain persists, see a doctor. **Lena:** How much does the medication cost? **Pharmacist:** Eight euros fifty. **Lena:** Here you go. Thank you for your help. **Pharmacist:** You're welcome. Get well soon, and recover quickly! **Lena:** Thank you. Bye!

## Dialogue 3: Hospital Admission

**Nurse Tim:** Good evening. What happened? How can I help you? **Patient Felix:** Good evening. I've injured myself. My arm hurts very badly. **Nurse Tim:** Where are you injured? Please show me the injury. **Felix:** Here, on my right arm. I fell on the stairs. **Nurse Tim:** That looks painful. What is your name? **Felix:** My name is Felix Hartmann. I am twenty-three years old. **Nurse Tim:** Mr. Hartmann, are you allergic to any medication? **Felix:** No. But I'm shaking – I think it's from the pain. **Nurse Tim:** The doctor will examine your injury right away. Do you have a fever or any other pain? **Felix:** No, just my arm. I'm also sweating a little. **Nurse Tim:** That's normal after an accident. I'll treat the injury temporarily now. **Felix:** Will the injury heal quickly? **Nurse Tim:** With proper treatment, most injuries heal within two to four weeks. **Felix:** Thank you for your help. **Nurse Tim:** No problem. Wait here, the doctor will be right with you.

## Dialogue 4: Apartment Viewing

**Agent Sven:** Good day! I'm Sven Richter. Are you Ms. Karim? **Yasmin:** Yes, good day! My name is Yasmin Karim. I'm looking forward to the viewing. **Sven:** The apartment has three rooms – a bedroom, a living room, and a dining room. **Yasmin:** Very nice. Is there also a balcony? **Sven:** Yes, the balcony is large and faces south. Here is the kitchen. **Yasmin:** The kitchen is small, but it has a refrigerator, a stove, and a sink. **Sven:** Exactly. The bathroom has a shower and a bathtub. **Yasmin:** Is there a washing machine? **Sven:** Yes, the washing machine is next to the dishwasher. **Yasmin:** How old is the building? **Sven:** The building is twenty years old, but it's very quiet here. **Yasmin:** What is the monthly rent? **Sven:** One thousand euros excluding utilities, approximately twelve hundred euros including them. **Yasmin:** That's fair. I still need to discuss it with my family. **Sven:** Of course. You can call me tomorrow. Goodbye! **Yasmin:** Thank you, Mr. Richter. Goodbye!

## Dialogue 5: At the Restaurant

**Waiter Jonas:** Good evening! Welcome. Do you have a reservation? **Guest Maria:** Good evening. Yes, I reserved a table for two. My name is Maria Fischer. **Jonas:** Very good, Ms. Fischer. Your table is here by the window. Please have a seat. **Maria:** Thank you. Can we have the menu? **Jonas:** Of course. What would you like to drink? **Maria:** I'd like a water, please. My husband will be here shortly – he was tired from work. **Jonas:** No problem. I'll bring your water right away. **Husband Klaus:** Hello! Sorry, I'm a little late. How are you? **Maria:** I'm fine, thank you. I've already ordered. **Klaus:** Great. Waiter, what do you recommend this evening? **Jonas:** The schnitzel is very good – our chef prepares it fresh daily. **Klaus:** Good, I'll have the schnitzel. And I'm hungry – I've barely eaten today. **Maria:** I'll have the salad and the soup, please. **Jonas:** Certainly. The food will be ready in twenty minutes. Enjoy your meal! **Maria:** Thank you, that's very kind of you.

## Dialogue 6: Shopping

**Saleswoman Petra:** Good morning! Can I help you? **Customer Tobias:** Good morning. Yes, I'm looking for a gift for my mother. She's sick. **Petra:** Oh, I'm sorry to hear that. What does she need? **Tobias:** She feels weak and has headaches. I'd like to bring her something nice. **Petra:** How about a book or a bouquet of flowers? **Tobias:** Flowers – great idea! What colors do you have? **Petra:** We have red, yellow, pink, and white flowers. All are fresh and beautiful. **Tobias:** I'll take the yellow bouquet. How much does it cost? **Petra:** The bouquet costs fifteen euros. **Tobias:** Good. Do you also have tea for colds? **Petra:** Yes, next to the register. Ginger tea is very popular for illness. **Tobias:** Great, I'll take two packs. My mother drinks tea every day. **Petra:** That's very thoughtful. Shall I wrap the flowers? **Tobias:** Yes, please. Thank you for your help! **Petra:** You're welcome! I hope your mother recovers quickly. Goodbye!

## Dialogue 7: Office – A Colleague is Sick

**Boss Stefan:** Good morning, Nina. Where is Marc today? I don't see him. **Nina:** Good morning, Stefan. Marc is sick. He wrote to me this morning. **Stefan:** What does he have? Is it serious? **Nina:** He has the flu. He's coughing badly and has a high fever. **Stefan:** The flu is contagious. Good that he's staying home. **Nina:** Yes. He said he feels very weak and can't work. **Stefan:** Does he have a doctor's appointment? **Nina:** Yes, the doctor is treating him this afternoon. He's already taking tablets. **Stefan:** Good. When is he coming back? We have a lot to get done next week. **Nina:** He thinks he'll recover by Thursday or Friday. **Stefan:** Okay. We'll distribute his tasks among the team. Can you organize that? **Nina:** Yes, I'll do that this afternoon. No stress. **Stefan:** Thank you, Nina. Please write to Marc – we wish him a speedy recovery. **Nina:** Of course. I'll do it right away!

## Dialogue 8: Meeting Friends

**Lara:** Hi, Ben! How are you? I haven't seen you for a week! **Ben:** Hi, Lara! I'm finally feeling well again. I was sick last week. **Lara:** Oh no! What did you have? **Ben:** I had a bad cold. I was coughing, sneezing, and had headaches. **Lara:** That sounds painful! Are you fully recovered now? **Ben:** Almost. I'm still a little tired, but my body is recovering. **Lara:** Did you take any medication? **Ben:** Yes, the doctor prescribed me tablets. They helped. **Lara:** Good. You already look much healthier today. **Ben:** Thanks! The fresh air helps too. I was home for five days – no office, no sport. **Lara:** Five days! That must have been boring. **Ben:** Totally! But now I'm fit again and want to make up for everything. **Lara:** Great! Then let's go out to eat together tonight – to celebrate! **Ben:** Yes, great idea. At seven o'clock at the restaurant on the corner? **Lara:** Perfect. See you tonight! Bye! **Ben:** Bye, Lara!

## Dialogue 9: GP – Chronic Illness

**Patient Hana:** Good day, Doctor. I've been having health problems for months. **Dr. Weber:** Good day, Ms. Yildiz. What exactly are your symptoms? **Hana:** I feel regularly weak and tired. Sometimes I also shake. **Dr. Weber:** Since when have you had these symptoms? Are they daily or occasional? **Hana:** Daily, for about three months. I also sweat heavily at night. **Dr. Weber:** That sounds like a chronic problem. I'll examine you thoroughly. **Hana:** I'm afraid it might be something serious. My body doesn't feel strong. **Dr. Weber:** I understand your concern. Do you have any known allergies? **Hana:** Yes, I'm allergic to penicillin. That's important, right? **Dr. Weber:** Very important, thank you. I'll take blood – the results will show us what's going on. **Hana:** What could it be? An illness like the flu? **Dr. Weber:** There can be many causes. After the results, I'll treat you accordingly. **Hana:** When will I get the results? **Dr. Weber:** In two days. In the meantime, I'll prescribe you something for the fatigue. **Hana:** Thank you, Doctor. You're very kind. **Dr. Weber:** That's my job. Goodbye, Ms. Yildiz!

## Dialogue 10: Gym – Health and Sport

**Trainer Leo:** Hello! I'm Leo, your trainer. You're new here, right? **Member Sara:** Hello, Leo! Yes, I'm new. My name is Sara. I want to get fitter and healthier. **Leo:** Great motivation! How is your current health? Do you have any pain or injuries? **Sara:** I had a minor knee injury two weeks ago. It's almost healed. **Leo:** Good to know. We'll train lightly today – no stress on the knee. **Sara:** Thank you. I sometimes feel weak during exercise. Is that normal? **Leo:** At the beginning, that's normal. The body needs time to get strong. **Sara:** I'm also often tired after work. I work eight hours a day in an office. **Leo:** I know the feeling. But sport helps with fatigue – you sleep better after training. **Sara:** Really? I always thought sport makes you more tired. **Leo:** No, regular exercise improves overall health. After four weeks you'll feel much fitter. **Sara:** That sounds good! What should I take? Proteins or vitamins? **Leo:** Start with a healthy diet. Medication or supplements are rarely needed if you eat well. **Sara:** Understood. I'm motivated. When do we start? **Leo:** Right now! First, ten minutes of warm-up. Let's go, Sara! **Sara:** Great! I'm ready. Thank you, Leo!
