# Health and Everyday Life

---

## Paragraph 1 – My Morning and My Body

Every morning I wake up and feel either fit or tired. Today I don't feel well – I feel a slight pain in my head. Headaches often come after a long night. I get up slowly and go to the bathroom. There I shower and brush my teeth. My body is weak and tired today. I look in the mirror and see that my skin is pale. That's not a good sign. I go to the kitchen and have breakfast. I eat a small breakfast because my stomach hurts. The stomach pain is not strong, but it is unpleasant. I also feel a little cold, even though it's summer. My body shivers slightly. I drink a hot tea – the tea helps my body. After breakfast I sit on the sofa in the living room. My wife asks: "How are you?" I say: "I'm not feeling well." She looks at me and says I look sick. I feel that she is right. Maybe I have a cold. Colds are very common in winter, but sometimes they come in autumn too. I decide not to work today. My boss is waiting at the office, but health is more important than work. I call the office and explain the situation. My boss is friendly and says I should rest. I lie back down in bed and try to relax.

---

## Paragraph 2 – The Cold Begins

The next morning it is worse. I cough loudly and my wife wakes up. "You're coughing very hard," she says, worried. I've been coughing since yesterday evening – the cough is dry and painful. I also sneeze several times. The sneezing comes suddenly and is very loud. My daughter comes into the bedroom and asks: "Dad, are you sick?" I say: "Yes, I'm sick." My body aches everywhere. The headaches are stronger than yesterday. I also have a fever – the fever is not very high, but it makes me weak. With a fever it is hard to concentrate. I sweat in bed, even though the room is not warm. Sweating is a sign of fever. My skin is hot and red. The cold develops quickly. I feel very weak. My wife brings me a blanket and a hot tea. She is very kind and caring. She says I should drink a lot and sleep. Good health only returns with rest. I drink the tea slowly and feel a little better afterward. But the cough remains. My wife looks at me worriedly. She says: "Tomorrow we'll go to the doctor." I agree. The doctor can help me recover quickly. I hope I recover soon, because I have an important appointment at the office next week. Without good health, one cannot work well.

---

## Paragraph 3 – At the Doctor

On Wednesday we go to the doctor. The hospital is not far from our house. The doctor's name is Dr. Müller and he is very friendly and competent. He examines me thoroughly. He examines my throat, my lungs, and my abdomen. While he examines me, I listen carefully. He asks: "Since when have you been coughing?" I say: "For three days." He also asks: "Do you feel tired?" I answer: "Yes, very." He measures my fever – the fever is 38.5 degrees. That is quite high. The doctor says I have the flu, not a simple cold. The flu is more contagious than a normal cold. A contagious illness like the flu requires special attention. He treats me right away. First, he prescribes me a medication for the fever. I should take the medication three times a day. He also prescribes me tablets for the cough. The tablets are small and white. I should take the tablets after eating. He explains how to take the tablets correctly. The doctor also says I should drink plenty of water. Water helps the body fight the illness. He asks whether I am allergic to any medication. I say: "No, I am not allergic." That is good. He writes everything on a prescription. I take the prescription and go to the pharmacy. At the pharmacy I receive the medications quickly. The pharmacist explains to me again how to take the medications. I thank him and drive home.

---

## Paragraph 4 – Recovering at Home

At home I immediately begin taking the tablets. I take the first tablet with plenty of water. I lie down on the sofa in the living room. My mother comes to visit us – she is very worried about me. "You look sick and weak," she says. She cooks a warm soup for me. The soup is very good and helps my body. I eat slowly and feel warmer afterward. My body is still sweating, but the fever goes down slightly. That is a good sign. My daughter brings me a book from the bedroom. She is very diligent and helpful. I try to read, but my headaches make it hard. The headaches come and go. I close my eyes and rest. The television runs quietly in the background. My son sits next to me on the sofa doing his homework. The whole family is together in the living room today. That gives me a good feeling. Even though I am sick, I don't feel alone. In the evening I take the second tablet. I go to sleep early – by nine o'clock I'm already in bed. The bed is soft and comfortable. I sleep almost ten hours. The next morning I already feel somewhat better. The fever is lower. The cough is still there, but weaker. I am glad that I went to the doctor in time. Good health is a great gift.

## Paragraph 5 – My Brother's Injury

My brother has a different problem. Last week he injured himself playing sports. He injured his foot – an injury to the ankle. The injury is not very serious, but it is painful. He feels a strong pain when walking. The pain comes especially when he steps on the foot. He also goes to the doctor. The doctor examines the injury carefully. He examines the foot with a special light. After the examination he says the injury is not broken, only sprained. The doctor treats the injury with a bandage. He prescribes my brother a cream for the pain. He should apply the cream to the skin twice a day. The skin around the ankle is blue and swollen. The blood has collected there. The doctor says that is normal after such an injury. The blood will go away in a few days. My brother should keep his foot elevated and keep it cool. He should also not walk. That is hard for him, because he is very active and hardworking. He normally works in the garden every day. Now he must pause and rest. He feels frustrated because he cannot be active. The injury heals slowly, however. After a week the pain is less. The skin is no longer so swollen. He begins to recover again. Healing takes time and patience. My brother is now more careful when playing sports.

---

## Paragraph 6 – Allergies in Spring

My sister has an allergy. Every year in spring her problems begin. She is allergic to flower pollen. When she is outside, she sneezes constantly. The sneezing doesn't stop. She feels very unwell. Her eyes are red and they hurt her. The skin around her eyes is also irritated. That is very unpleasant for her. She sometimes sneezes twenty times a day. The sneezing makes her tired and weak. She goes to the doctor and the doctor examines her. He determines that she has a strong allergy. He explains what an allergy is. An allergy is a reaction of the body to certain substances. In my sister's case, her body reacts to pollen. The doctor prescribes her special tablets for the allergy. She should take these tablets daily. He also treats her with a nasal spray. The spray helps immediately. My sister takes the tablets regularly. After a few days the sneezing is less. She feels better. But the allergy remains chronic – it comes back every year. A chronic allergy requires ongoing treatment. In summer it is better, because there is less pollen in the air. My sister is glad when autumn comes. In autumn she feels healthy and fit. She then often goes for walks in the park. Health means for her being able to be outside without allergies.

## Paragraph 7 – Sports and Health

Sports are very important for health. My father is sixty years old, but he is very fit and healthy. He goes running every morning. He runs thirty minutes daily. While running he breathes deeply and regularly. Deep breathing is good for the lungs. He sweats during sports, but sweating is healthy. After sports he feels strong and energetic. My father says: "Health is the most important thing in life." He is right. Those who are fit get sick less often. My father used to have frequent headaches. Since he started doing sports, he almost never has headaches anymore. Sports heal many small problems. I also try to do sports regularly, but it is hard with everyday life. My everyday life is often stressful. I work from Monday to Friday in the office. After work I am tired. Nevertheless I try to do sports twice a week. Sports help me reduce stress. When I don't do sports, I feel heavier and more sluggish. My body needs movement. My children also do sports – my son plays football and my daughter dances. Sports for children are especially important. A healthy body needs regular movement. We all try to live healthily. Good health doesn't come on its own – you have to actively work for it.

---

## Paragraph 8 – A Chronic Illness

My grandfather has a chronic illness. He has diabetes – a chronic illness that does not disappear. He has been living with this illness for twenty years. He has gotten used to daily life with the illness. Every morning he measures his blood. The blood shows how much sugar he has. With a small device he makes a prick in the skin and takes some blood. The blood is then measured. When the blood has too much sugar, he feels weak and tired. He must then take his medications. The medications regulate the sugar in his blood. He takes the medications twice a day – in the morning and in the evening. The doctor treats him regularly. He goes to the doctor every month. The doctor examines him and prescribes new medications when necessary. My grandfather is very disciplined. He eats healthily and avoids sugar. He breathes calmly and takes light walks. Breathing fresh air helps him. Despite his chronic illness, he is positive and cheerful. He says: "One can live well with a chronic illness if one is disciplined." He is our role model in the family. His health is stable because he takes care of himself. The illness sometimes hurts him, especially when the weather is cold. Then he feels a dull pain in his legs. We help him as much as possible then. Health is a topic that affects the whole family.

---

## Paragraph 9 – In the Hospital

My aunt was in the hospital last week. She had a serious injury – she fell down the stairs. The injury to her shoulder was very serious. I visit her in the hospital. The hospital is large and bright. Many nurses and doctors work there. The nurses treat the patients in a friendly and professional manner. The doctor examines my aunt every morning. He examines the injury with an X-ray machine. The machine shows what the blood and bones look like. The injury is severe and healing takes a long time. My aunt receives strong medications for the pain. The medications help her endure the pain. Without the medications the pain would be unbearable. She takes the tablets regularly. Sometimes she feels dizzy from the tablets. That is a known side effect. The doctor says healing will take at least six weeks. During these six weeks she must remain still. She should not move her arm. That is hard for her, because she is normally very active. She feels weak and frustrated. We visit her daily. We bring her books, fruit, and fresh flowers. The flowers are yellow and colorful – they bring color to the room. My aunt feels better because of our visits. She begins to recover slowly. Recovery goes step by step. After two weeks she can laugh again. The doctor says she is recovering very well.

## Paragraph 10 – Health in Everyday Life

Health is not just the absence of illness. Health also means feeling well – physically and mentally. In my everyday life I try to live healthily. I have breakfast every morning – a healthy breakfast with fruit and yogurt. I breathe fresh air on the balcony in the morning. Breathing fresh air clears the mind. I try to sleep enough – at least eight hours per night. With little sleep one feels weak and sluggish. I drink plenty of water – water is good for the skin and the blood. My skin looks better when I drink a lot. I eat little sugar and little fat. I prefer to eat fresh vegetables and healthy meat. Sometimes I still feel tired – that is normal in a stressful everyday life. Stress is a big problem for health. Too much stress can cause headaches, sleep problems, and illnesses. That is why I try to relax regularly. On weekends I go to the park with the family. We breathe fresh air and move around. The children play and laugh – that makes us parents fit and happy too. Sometimes we cough or sneeze in winter, but mostly we recover quickly because we live healthily. If I do get sick, I immediately take medications and rest. I go to the doctor when the pain or fever doesn't stop. Health is the most valuable thing in life. Without health one cannot enjoy everyday life – not work, not family, not leisure. That is why I take care of my body every day and feel grateful for every healthy day.
