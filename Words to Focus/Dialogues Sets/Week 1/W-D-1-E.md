## Dialogue 1: The Morning Begins – A Busy Morning Routine

**Anna:** Good morning, Felix! You look tired. Did you sleep well? **Felix:** Good morning, Anna. No, I'm not doing well. I slept badly. **Anna:** Why? What happened? **Felix:** I forgot to plan my appointment tomorrow morning. Now I'm very stressed. **Anna:** Oh no! When does the appointment start? **Felix:** It starts at eight o'clock. I have to hurry! **Anna:** Okay, okay. Have you showered yet? **Felix:** No, not yet. I just woke up. **Anna:** Then hurry up! The shower is free. **Felix:** Thanks. Can you please organize the schedule for me? **Anna:** Yes, of course. What's on the schedule? **Felix:** First the appointment at eight, then work until noon, then a break. **Anna:** And when is your end of workday? **Felix:** Usually at six o'clock. But today work will go on longer. **Anna:** How long will it go today? **Felix:** Maybe until eight in the evening. I have many tasks to complete. **Anna:** That's too much! You need to relax too. **Felix:** Yes, I know. On the weekend I'll relax. **Anna:** Good. Now eat breakfast quickly! Here's your coffee. **Felix:** Thank you, Anna. You're very kind and friendly. **Anna:** No problem. Punctuality is important. Don't forget that! **Felix:** No, I won't forget it. Goodbye! **Anna:** Goodbye and good luck!

## Dialogue 2: The Appointment – Planning a Meeting

**Lena:** Hello, Thomas! How are you? **Thomas:** Hello, Lena! I'm doing well, thank you. And you? **Lena:** I'm doing great. I have a question. When can we plan our meeting? **Thomas:** I'm very busy this week. How about next week? **Lena:** Next week is good. Which day works for you? **Thomas:** Monday or Tuesday. I'm free on those days. **Lena:** Monday is better for me. What time shall we start? **Thomas:** At ten o'clock? Is that early enough? **Lena:** Yes, that's perfect. Where shall we meet? **Thomas:** At the café next to the school. Do you know it? **Lena:** Yes, I know it. It's small but nice. **Thomas:** Good. What do we need to prepare? **Lena:** I need to prepare some documents. That will take two hours. **Thomas:** And I need to organize the schedule. I'll take care of that this evening. **Lena:** Great. Should we reschedule the meeting if it rains? **Thomas:** No, we won't reschedule it. The café is indoors. **Lena:** That's true! I sometimes forget things like that. **Thomas:** No problem. I'll remind you on Sunday. **Lena:** Thank you, Thomas. You're very organized and hardworking. **Thomas:** Thank you very much. Until Monday then! **Lena:** Until Monday! Bye!

## Dialogue 3: Stress at the Office

**Markus:** Good day, Sabine. Do you have a moment? **Sabine:** Good day, Markus. I'm very busy right now. What do you need? **Markus:** I have a question about the project schedule. **Sabine:** Okay, ask quickly. I have a meeting in ten minutes. **Markus:** When does the next phase of the project begin? **Sabine:** It begins on Wednesday. We have to get everything done by Friday. **Markus:** That's only three days? That's a lot of stress! **Sabine:** Yes, I know. But we can do it. We have to hurry. **Markus:** Should we postpone the deadline? **Sabine:** No! We are not postponing the deadline. The boss is waiting. **Markus:** Understood. What do I need to prepare? **Sabine:** You need to prepare the presentation. That is your task. **Markus:** Okay. How long does the preparation take? **Sabine:** Usually two to three hours. But you're hardworking, you'll get it done faster. **Markus:** Thank you. And you? What are you taking care of today? **Sabine:** I'm organizing the meetings and writing emails. Many emails. **Markus:** When do you take a break? **Sabine:** Maybe at noon. But sometimes I forget to take a break. **Markus:** You need to relax, Sabine! That's important. **Sabine:** I know, I know. On the weekend I'll relax. **Markus:** Good. And when is your end of workday today? **Sabine:** Late today. Maybe at nine o'clock. **Markus:** That's too late! You work too much. **Sabine:** That's everyday life in the office. But thank you for your concern, Markus!

## Dialogue 4: The Weekend – Weekend Plans

**Julia:** Hello, Peter! What are you doing this weekend? **Peter:** Hello, Julia! I'm planning a little free time. Finally! **Julia:** Oh, nice! You're always so busy during the week. **Peter:** Yes, I work a lot every day. The weekend is my time to recover. **Julia:** What have you planned? What are you doing Saturday? **Peter:** On Saturday I'll sleep in and then relax at home. **Julia:** That sounds good. And Sunday? **Peter:** On Sunday I have a date with my brother. We're spending the day together. **Julia:** What will you do together? Where are you going? **Peter:** We're going to the park. He has a dog and a cat. **Julia:** Cute! What's the dog's name? **Peter:** The dog's name is Bear. He's big and brown. **Julia:** Funny! And the cat? **Peter:** The cat's name is Mouse. She's small and white. **Julia:** That's very creative! I find that funny. **Peter:** Yes, my brother is very creative and funny. **Julia:** Do you have plans for the evening too? **Peter:** Yes, we're spending the evening at a restaurant. We're eating together. **Julia:** Which restaurant? Do you know a good one? **Peter:** Yes, there's a new restaurant next to the supermarket. It's very nice. **Julia:** Oh, I know it! The food there is great. **Peter:** Come along then! We have room for three people. **Julia:** Really? That's very kind of you. When does dinner start? **Peter:** At seven o'clock. Are you punctual? **Julia:** Yes, I'm always punctual! Until Sunday then, Peter! **Peter:** Until Sunday! Bye, Julia!

## Dialogue 5: The Holiday – Holiday Plans

**Emma:** Good morning, Klaus! Do you know that there's a holiday next week? **Klaus:** Good morning, Emma! Yes, I know. I'm really looking forward to it. **Emma:** What are you planning for the holiday? **Klaus:** I'm planning a small trip. I'm spending the day with my family. **Emma:** Oh, where are you going? **Klaus:** We're going to Switzerland. My grandmother lives there. **Emma:** How lovely! How old is your grandmother? **Klaus:** She's eighty years old. She's still very active and calm. **Emma:** That's wonderful! How long does the trip take? **Klaus:** The trip takes about six hours by car. **Emma:** That's long! Are you starting early in the morning? **Klaus:** Yes, we're starting at five in the morning. We want to arrive on time. **Emma:** Five o'clock! That's very early. I always forget to get up early. **Klaus:** Me too! But my wife always reminds me. **Emma:** That's good. And you, Emma? Do you have plans for the holiday? **Emma:** Me? I don't have big plans. Sometimes I just stay home. **Klaus:** That's nice too. Relaxing is important. **Emma:** Yes. Maybe I'll visit my cousin. She lives nearby. **Klaus:** That's a good idea. What's your cousin's name? **Emma:** Her name is Sara. She's very funny and nice. **Klaus:** Great. Then you'll spend the holiday together. **Emma:** Yes, I hope so. I'll remind her today. **Klaus:** Good. Then have a wonderful holiday! **Emma:** Thank you, Klaus! You too. Say hello to your grandmother from me! **Klaus:** Will do! Goodbye, Emma!

## Dialogue 6: Running Late

**Nina:** Hello, Stefan! Where are you? You're twenty minutes late! **Stefan:** Hello, Nina! I'm sorry. I'm stressed. The bus is delayed. **Nina:** I see. I understand that. But we've been waiting here a long time. **Stefan:** I know, I know. I'm hurrying right now. **Nina:** We have an appointment at three o'clock. It's now twenty past two. **Stefan:** I'll arrive in ten minutes. Can we reschedule the appointment? **Nina:** No, we are not rescheduling it. The doctor doesn't wait long. **Stefan:** Okay, I understand. I'm walking quickly. **Nina:** Good. Will you be punctual next time? **Stefan:** Yes! I'm usually punctual. Today is an exception. **Nina:** You say that every time, Stefan! **Stefan:** No, that's not true! I'm regularly punctual. **Nina:** You're occasionally late. That's your habit. **Stefan:** Okay, maybe a little. But I'm working on it. **Nina:** I always remind you, but you forget it anyway. **Stefan:** I don't forget it on purpose. I just have a lot of stress in everyday life. **Nina:** I understand that. But punctuality is very important. **Stefan:** You're right. I'm truly sorry. **Nina:** It's okay. Ah, there you are! Finally! **Stefan:** I'm here! I'm sorry, Nina. Is the appointment still on? **Nina:** Yes, we still have five minutes. Come quickly! **Stefan:** Thank you for your patience, Nina. You're very kind. **Nina:** Yes, yes. But next time hurry earlier!

## Dialogue 7: Daily Habits

**Tobias:** Good evening, Laura! How was your day? **Laura:** Good evening, Tobias! Very busy as always. And yours? **Tobias:** Also busy. I completed many tasks. **Laura:** What do you do every day? Tell me about your daily routine. **Tobias:** I get up at six o'clock, have breakfast, and then start working. **Laura:** When exactly does your work start? **Tobias:** It starts at eight o'clock. I work until noon without a break. **Laura:** Without a break? That's not healthy! **Tobias:** I know. I sometimes forget to take a break. It's a bad habit. **Laura:** I regularly take a break at ten o'clock. That's important. **Tobias:** That's a good habit. How long do you pause? **Laura:** I pause for about fifteen minutes. Then I continue working. **Tobias:** And when is your end of workday? **Laura:** Usually at five o'clock. Sometimes at six. **Tobias:** That's good. What do you do in your free time? **Laura:** I like to relax. I read books or watch TV. **Tobias:** Nice. I spend my free time in the garden. That relaxes me. **Laura:** Oh, you have a garden? How big is it? **Tobias:** It's not very big, but nice. I have flowers and trees. **Laura:** That sounds wonderful. Is that a daily activity? **Tobias:** No, only weekly. On Saturday I spend time in the garden. **Laura:** And on Sunday? **Tobias:** On Sunday I relax completely. That's my habit. **Laura:** That's a very good and regular routine, Tobias! **Tobias:** Thank you, Laura. Regularity is the key to happiness, I believe.

## Dialogue 8: Taking a Break

**Ben:** Hello, Mia! Are you on break right now? **Mia:** Hello, Ben! Yes, I'm taking a short break. I'm very tired today. **Ben:** Why are you so tired? Did you sleep badly? **Mia:** No, I slept well. But the stress today is great. **Ben:** What did you take care of today? **Mia:** I organized three appointments and prepared five reports. **Ben:** That's a lot! No wonder you're tired. **Mia:** Yes. I need a longer break. Maybe ten minutes. **Ben:** Ten minutes isn't enough. You need to relax more. **Mia:** I know, but I still have a lot to do. **Ben:** What's still coming up? **Mia:** I still need to prepare two meetings. And finish the documents. **Ben:** When will you finish the documents? **Mia:** I'll finish them before the end of the workday. Maybe at five o'clock. **Ben:** And when does the next meeting start? **Mia:** It starts at three o'clock. I still have one hour. **Ben:** Then use the time! Relax now. **Mia:** You're right. The rush isn't helping me. **Ben:** Exactly. Punctuality is important, but so is health. **Mia:** True. I'll have a coffee now and relax for five minutes. **Ben:** Good! Can I help you? I'm free right now. **Mia:** Really? That would be great! Can you take care of the emails? **Ben:** Yes, of course. I'll take care of that for you. **Mia:** Thank you, Ben! You're very kind and helpful. **Ben:** No problem. Colleagues help each other!

## Dialogue 9: Weekly Planning

**Sophie:** Good morning, David! Have you organized your weekly plan yet? **David:** Good morning, Sophie! No, not yet. I'm just starting with it. **Sophie:** I'm happy to help you. What do you have to do this week? **David:** On Monday and Tuesday I have two appointments each day. **Sophie:** And Wednesday? **David:** Wednesday is a holiday. I'm free! **Sophie:** Oh, nice! What are you planning for the holiday? **David:** I'm planning a quiet day at home. I want to relax. **Sophie:** That sounds good. You work very regularly. You need recovery. **David:** Yes. And Thursday? I have an important meeting then. **Sophie:** What time does the meeting start? **David:** It starts at nine o'clock. I have to prepare everything. **Sophie:** What do you need to prepare? **David:** I need to organize data and prepare a presentation. **Sophie:** How long does the preparation take? **David:** It takes about four hours. It's a lot of work. **Sophie:** Then start on it this evening! Don't postpone it. **David:** You're right. I'll start on it today at eight o'clock. **Sophie:** Good. And Friday? What are you taking care of on Friday? **David:** On Friday I'll take care of all the small tasks. Then the weekend is free! **Sophie:** Perfect! That's a good schedule, David. **David:** Thank you, Sophie. You always remind me of the important things. **Sophie:** I'm happy to do that. Punctuality and organization are my strengths. **David:** Yes, you're very hardworking and organized. I'm learning from you! **Sophie:** That makes me happy. Good luck this week, David! **David:** Thank you! You too, Sophie. Goodbye!

## Dialogue 10: End of the Workday

**Clara:** Good evening, Max! When is your end of workday today? **Max:** Good evening, Clara! Actually at six o'clock. But I'm still busy. **Clara:** What are you still taking care of? **Max:** I still need to finish a report. It won't take long. **Clara:** How much longer will it take? **Max:** Maybe twenty minutes. Then I'm done. **Clara:** Okay, I'll wait for you. Are we going home together? **Max:** Yes, gladly. Thank you, Clara. Are you here on time? **Clara:** Yes, I'm always punctual. That's my habit. **Max:** I know. You're very regular and reliable. **Clara:** Thank you. And you? Was today's daily routine very stressful? **Max:** Yes, very. I had many appointments and a lot of stress. **Clara:** I'm sorry to hear that. What was particularly stressful? **Max:** An appointment was postponed, and I had to reorganize everything. **Clara:** Oh no! That's a lot of work. Did you still manage to take a break? **Max:** Yes, once. At noon I paused briefly. Only five minutes. **Clara:** Five minutes! That's too little. You need to relax more. **Max:** I know. But the rush at the office leaves no time. **Clara:** That's not a good habit, Max. Your health is important. **Max:** You're right. I sometimes forget to take care of myself. **Clara:** I'll remind you of your break tomorrow. That I promise you. **Max:** That's very kind of you. Thank you, Clara. **Clara:** No problem. So, are you done with the report now? **Max:** Yes! I'm just finishing it up. One more minute. **Clara:** Good. Then we'll go together. The evening belongs to us! **Max:** Yes! End of the workday is the most beautiful time of the day. Let's go! **Clara:** Goodbye, office! See you tomorrow! **Max:** Goodbye! And tomorrow — punctual, as always!
