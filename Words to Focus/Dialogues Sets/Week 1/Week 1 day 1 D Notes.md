# German Grammar Notes – A1–A2 Learner Guide

## Dialogues: Alltag & Zeitmanagement

---

1. Guten Morgen, Felix! Du siehst müde aus. 🇬🇧 Good morning, Felix! You look tired. → **Separable verb** _aussehen_ (to look/appear): the prefix _aus-_ moves to the end in main clauses → _siehst … aus_ (not _aussiehst_).

---

2. Hast du gut geschlafen? 🇬🇧 Did you sleep well? → **Perfekt tense** with _haben_: auxiliary _hast_ + past participle _geschlafen_ (irregular: schlafen → geschlafen). Participle goes to the end.

---

3. Nein, mir geht's nicht gut. 🇬🇧 No, I'm not doing well. → _Mir_ is **dative** (not nominative _ich_) — the phrase _es geht + dative_ expresses how someone feels: _mir geht es_ = "it goes to me."

---

4. Ich habe schlecht geschlafen. 🇬🇧 I slept badly. → **Perfekt** with _haben_ + past participle at the end. _Schlecht_ (adverb) modifies _geschlafen_, placed before the participle.

---

5. Warum? Was ist passiert? 🇬🇧 Why? What happened? → **Perfekt** with _sein_: _passieren_ (to happen) uses _sein_ as auxiliary → _ist passiert_. Motion/change-of-state verbs often take _sein_.

---

6. Ich habe meinen Termin morgen früh vergessen zu planen. 🇬🇧 I forgot to plan my appointment tomorrow morning. → _Meinen_ is **accusative masculine** (_der Termin_ → accusative → _den/meinen_). The infinitive _zu planen_ is pushed to the very end (infinitive clause).

---

7. Jetzt bin ich sehr gestresst. 🇬🇧 Now I am very stressed. → _Gestresst_ used as a **predicate adjective** after _sein_ — no inflection needed (compare attributive: _ein gestresster Mann_).

---

8. Oh nein! Wann beginnt der Termin? 🇬🇧 Oh no! When does the appointment start? → In **W-questions** (wann, wo, was…) the verb comes second and the subject follows: _Wann – beginnt – der Termin_.

---

9. Er beginnt um acht Uhr. 🇬🇧 It starts at eight o'clock. → _Um_ + time = **fixed time preposition** taking accusative, but with clock expressions it is idiomatic and the case is absorbed. No article needed after _um_ with _Uhr_.

---

10. Ich muss mich beeilen! 🇬🇧 I have to hurry! → **Reflexive verb** _sich beeilen_: with _ich_ the reflexive pronoun is _mich_ (accusative). Modal _muss_ sends the infinitive _beeilen_ to the end.

---

11. Hast du dich schon geduscht? 🇬🇧 Have you already showered? → **Reflexive Perfekt**: _duschen_ is reflexive → _sich duschen_ → auxiliary _haben_ + reflexive pronoun _dich_ + participle _geduscht_ at the end.

---

12. Nein, noch nicht. Ich bin gerade aufgewacht. 🇬🇧 No, not yet. I just woke up. → **Separable verb Perfekt**: _aufwachen_ uses _sein_ (change of state). Participle formation: _ge-_ inserted between prefix and stem → _auf-ge-wacht_.

---

13. Dann beeile dich! 🇬🇧 Then hurry up! → **Imperative + reflexive**: for _du_-imperatives, drop _-st_ → _beeil-_ + reflexive _dich_. The _e_ ending (_beeile_) is optional but common with stems ending in _-l_.

---

14. Die Dusche ist frei. 🇬🇧 The shower is free/available. → _Die Dusche_ is **feminine** (_die_) — important to note as bathroom items have grammatical genders that must be memorised.

---

15. Kannst du mir bitte den Zeitplan organisieren? 🇬🇧 Can you please organise the schedule for me? → _Mir_ = **dative** (indirect object, "for me"); _den Zeitplan_ = **accusative masculine** (direct object, _der_ → _den_). Both cases triggered by the same verb _organisieren_.

---

16. Was steht auf dem Zeitplan? 🇬🇧 What is on the schedule? → _Auf_ is a **two-way preposition**: location → dative → _dem Zeitplan_ (masculine dative).

---

17. Zuerst der Termin um acht, dann Arbeit bis Mittag, dann eine Pause. 🇬🇧 First the appointment at eight, then work until noon, then a break. → Elliptical sentence (no verb). _Eine Pause_ uses the **indefinite article feminine accusative** (_eine_), agreeing with _die Pause_.

---

18. Und wann ist dein Feierabend? 🇬🇧 And when is your end of the workday? → _Dein_ = **possessive adjective masculine nominative** (_der Feierabend_ is masculine), so no extra ending in predicative position.

---

19. Gewöhnlich um sechs Uhr. Aber heute dauert die Arbeit länger. 🇬🇧 Usually at six o'clock. But today work lasts longer. → _Länger_ is the **comparative of lang** (_lang → länger_). Also note the adverb _gewöhnlich_ at position 1 doesn't change verb-second word order.

---

20. Wie lange dauert sie heute? 🇬🇧 How long does it last today? → _Sie_ refers back to _die Arbeit_ — **feminine pronoun** replacing a feminine noun. Pronoun gender must match the noun's grammatical gender.

---

21. Vielleicht bis acht Uhr abends. 🇬🇧 Maybe until eight o'clock in the evening. → _Bis_ + time = "until." _Abends_ (in the evenings) is an **adverb of time** formed by adding _-s_ to _Abend_; it doesn't inflect.

---

22. Ich muss viele Aufgaben erledigen. 🇬🇧 I have to complete many tasks. → _Viele Aufgaben_ = **accusative plural** — plural nouns take no article gender change, but _viele_ (many) stays uninflected in this strong-adjective position.

---

23. Das ist zu viel! Du musst auch entspannen. 🇬🇧 That's too much! You must also relax. → _Auch_ (also) position: adverbs of focus like _auch_ come directly before the element they modify — here before _entspannen_, emphasising the need to relax too.

---

24. Ja, ich weiß. Am Wochenende werde ich entspannen. 🇬🇧 Yes, I know. At the weekend I will relax. → **Futur I**: _werden_ + infinitive at the end. _Am Wochenende_ (on the weekend) = _an_ + dative _dem_ contracted → _am_.

---

25. Jetzt frühstücke schnell! 🇬🇧 Now eat breakfast quickly! → **Du-imperative** of _frühstücken_: drop _-st_ → _frühstück-_ + optional _-e_ → _frühstücke_. Verb comes first in imperative sentences.

---

26. Hier ist dein Kaffee. 🇬🇧 Here is your coffee. → _Dein Kaffee_ — _Kaffee_ is **masculine** (_der_), so possessive is _dein_ (no ending in nominative masculine).

---

27. Du bist sehr nett und freundlich. 🇬🇧 You are very nice and friendly. → **Predicate adjectives** after _sein_ are uninflected (_nett_, not _netter_ or _nette_) — no case ending needed.

---

28. Kein Problem. Pünktlichkeit ist wichtig. Vergiss das nicht! 🇬🇧 No problem. Punctuality is important. Don't forget that! → **Negated imperative**: _vergiss_ (du-imperative of _vergessen_, irregular: _vergisst_ → _vergiss_) + _nicht_ at the end to negate the whole command.

---

29. Nein, ich vergesse es nicht. 🇬🇧 No, I won't forget it. → _Es_ = **accusative neuter pronoun** replacing _das_ (that/it). _Nicht_ at the end negates the whole verb phrase.

---

30. Auf Wiedersehen und viel Erfolg! 🇬🇧 Goodbye and good luck! → _Viel Erfolg_ (literally "much success") = fixed expression. _Viel_ here is **uninflected** before a masculine singular noun without article.

---

31. Hallo, Thomas! Wie geht es dir? 🇬🇧 Hello, Thomas! How are you? → _Dir_ = **dative of _du_** — the full form of _es geht dir_ (formal version of _dir geht's_). Dative used because _gehen_ + dative expresses wellbeing.

---

32. Mir geht's gut, danke. Und dir? 🇬🇧 I'm doing well, thanks. And you? → Contraction _geht's_ = _geht es_. _Dir_ alone at the end is an **elliptical question** ("And [how does it go] for you?").

---

33. Ich habe eine Frage. Wann können wir unsere Verabredung planen? 🇬🇧 I have a question. When can we plan our meeting? → _Eine Frage_ = **accusative feminine** (_haben_ takes accusative direct object; _die Frage_ → _eine Frage_). _Unsere_ = **accusative feminine possessive** (_unsere Verabredung_).

---

34. Ich bin diese Woche sehr beschäftigt. 🇬🇧 I am very busy this week. → _Diese Woche_ = **accusative time expression** (duration/point in time without preposition uses accusative) — _diese_ agrees with feminine _die Woche_.

---

35. Wie wäre es mit nächster Woche? 🇬🇧 How about next week? → _Mit_ always takes **dative** → _nächster Woche_ (feminine dative: _-er_ ending on adjective in strong declension after no article).

---

36. Nächste Woche ist gut. Welcher Tag passt dir? 🇬🇧 Next week works. Which day suits you? → _Welcher Tag_ — interrogative _welch-_ takes **strong adjective endings** (here nominative masculine → _-er_). _Dir_ = dative (suits _to you_).

---

37. Montag oder Dienstag. Ich bin an diesen Tagen frei. 🇬🇧 Monday or Tuesday. I am free on those days. → _An_ + dative for days → _an diesen Tagen_: _diese_ becomes _diesen_ in **dative plural** (all genders: _-en_ ending).

---

38. Montag ist besser für mich. Um wie viel Uhr beginnen wir? 🇬🇧 Monday is better for me. What time do we start? → _Besser_ = **comparative of _gut_** (gut → besser, irregular). _Für_ always takes **accusative** → _für mich_.

---

39. Um zehn Uhr? Ist das pünktlich genug? 🇬🇧 At ten o'clock? Is that punctual enough? → _Genug_ (enough) always follows the adjective/adverb it modifies (_pünktlich genug_, never _genug pünktlich_) — opposite of English word order.

---

40. Ja, das ist perfekt. Wo treffen wir uns? 🇬🇧 Yes, that's perfect. Where do we meet? → **Reflexive verb** _sich treffen_ (to meet each other): with _wir_ the reflexive pronoun is _uns_ (accusative plural).

---

41. Im Café neben der Schule. Kennst du es? 🇬🇧 In the café next to the school. Do you know it? → _Im_ = _in dem_ (dative contraction — location). _Neben_ + dative for location → _der Schule_ (feminine dative, _die_ → _der_).

---

42. Ja, ich kenne es. Es ist klein aber nett. 🇬🇧 Yes, I know it. It is small but nice. → _Es_ refers back to _das Café_ (**neuter** → _es_). _Klein_ and _nett_ are uninflected predicate adjectives after _sein_.

---

43. Gut. Was müssen wir vorbereiten? 🇬🇧 Good. What do we need to prepare? → **Separable verb** _vorbereiten_: in modal constructions the infinitive stays whole at the end → _vorbereiten_ (not split here, splitting only in main clause without modal).

---

44. Ich muss ein paar Dokumente vorbereiten. Das dauert zwei Stunden. 🇬🇧 I need to prepare a few documents. That will take two hours. → _Ein paar_ = "a few" — uninflected fixed expression. _Dokumente_ is **accusative plural** (direct object of _vorbereiten_).

---

45. Und ich muss den Zeitplan organisieren. Ich erledige das heute Abend. 🇬🇧 And I need to organise the schedule. I'll do that this evening. → _Heute Abend_ = "this evening" — compound time adverb, **no preposition needed**. _Den Zeitplan_ = accusative masculine.

---

46. Sollen wir die Verabredung verschieben, falls es regnet? 🇬🇧 Shall we postpone the meeting if it rains? → _Falls_ (if/in case) introduces a **subordinate clause** → verb _regnet_ goes to the end. _Die Verabredung_ = accusative feminine.

---

47. Nein, wir verschieben sie nicht. Das Café ist drinnen. 🇬🇧 No, we're not postponing it. The café is indoors. → _Sie_ replaces _die Verabredung_ — **accusative feminine pronoun**. _Nicht_ at the end negates the whole action.

---

48. Stimmt! Ich vergesse manchmal solche Dinge. 🇬🇧 True! I sometimes forget such things. → _Solche Dinge_ = **accusative plural** (direct object). _Solch-_ takes strong endings without preceding article: plural accusative → _solche_.

---

49. Ich erinnere dich am Sonntag. 🇬🇧 I'll remind you on Sunday. → _Erinnern_ here is transitive: _dich_ = **accusative** (the person reminded). _Am Sonntag_ = _an dem Sonntag_ (dative, location in time).

---

50. Danke, Thomas. Du bist sehr organisiert und fleißig. 🇬🇧 Thank you, Thomas. You are very organised and hardworking. → Past participle _organisiert_ used as **predicate adjective** — no inflection. Note _Thomas_ is in the **vocative** position (set off by comma).

---

51. Guten Tag, Sabine. Hast du einen Moment Zeit? 🇬🇧 Good day, Sabine. Do you have a moment? → _Einen Moment_ = **accusative masculine** (_der Moment_ → _einen Moment_ after _haben_). _Zeit_ (time) here is a partitive appositive with no article.

---

52. Ich bin gerade sehr beschäftigt. Was brauchst du? 🇬🇧 I'm very busy right now. What do you need? → _Gerade_ = "right now/currently" — temporal adverb placed after the verb. _Brauchen_ takes **accusative** direct object (_was_ = accusative here).

---

53. Ich habe eine Frage zum Zeitplan für das Projekt. 🇬🇧 I have a question about the schedule for the project. → _Zum_ = _zu dem_ (dative contraction). _Zu_ + dative is used for topics/reference ("about/regarding"). _Das Projekt_ = neuter accusative after _für_.

---

54. Okay, frag schnell. Ich habe in zehn Minuten einen Termin. 🇬🇧 OK, ask quickly. I have an appointment in ten minutes. → _Frag_ = **du-imperative** of _fragen_ (drop _-st_, no _-e_ needed). _In zehn Minuten_ = time expression using _in_ + dative for future time ("in X minutes").

---

55. Wann beginnt die nächste Phase des Projekts? 🇬🇧 When does the next phase of the project begin? → _Des Projekts_ = **genitive masculine/neuter** (_das Projekt_ → _des Projekts_). Genitive expresses possession/relationship between nouns; _-s_ added to neuter noun.

---

56. Sie beginnt am Mittwoch. Wir müssen alles bis Freitag erledigen. 🇬🇧 It starts on Wednesday. We have to finish everything by Friday. → _Am Mittwoch_ = dative (day names are masculine; _an dem_ → _am_). _Bis Freitag_ = "by Friday" — _bis_ + bare noun (no article) for deadlines.

---

57. Das dauert nur drei Tage? Das ist viel Stress! 🇬🇧 That's only three days? That's a lot of stress! → _Drei Tage_ = **accusative** time span (duration without preposition uses accusative). _Viel Stress_ — _viel_ uninflected before masculine uncountable noun.

---

58. Ja, ich weiß. Aber wir können es schaffen. Wir müssen uns beeilen. 🇬🇧 Yes, I know. But we can manage it. We have to hurry. → _Es_ = accusative neuter (refers to the task). _Uns beeilen_ — reflexive _uns_ (accusative, 1st person plural) required by _sich beeilen_.

---

59. Sollen wir den Termin verschieben? 🇬🇧 Should we postpone the appointment? → _Sollen_ (should/shall) expresses **obligation from others' expectations**. _Den Termin_ = accusative masculine direct object.

---

60. Nein! Wir verschieben den Termin nicht. Der Chef wartet. 🇬🇧 No! We're not postponing the appointment. The boss is waiting. → _Nicht_ placed after the accusative object _den Termin_ to negate the entire predicate (not just the object). Standard negation position: after objects, before predicates.

---

61. Verstehe. Was muss ich vorbereiten? 🇬🇧 I understand. What do I have to prepare? → _Was_ = **accusative** (direct object of _vorbereiten_). In modal + infinitive, the infinitive (_vorbereiten_, separable) goes unsplit to the end.

---

62. Du musst die Präsentation vorbereiten. Das ist deine Aufgabe. 🇬🇧 You have to prepare the presentation. That is your task. → _Die Präsentation_ = **accusative feminine** (_haben_ / _vorbereiten_ takes accusative). _Deine Aufgabe_ — _dein-_ + _-e_ ending: feminine nominative predicate.

---

63. Gewöhnlich zwei bis drei Stunden. Aber du bist fleißig, du schaffst es schneller. 🇬🇧 Usually two to three hours. But you're hardworking, you'll manage it faster. → _Schneller_ = **comparative of _schnell_** (regular: add _-er_). _Es schaffen_ = "to manage/accomplish it" — idiomatic expression, _es_ is accusative.

---

64. Und du? Was erledigst du heute? 🇬🇧 And you? What are you taking care of today? → Subject _du_ follows the verb because _was_ (interrogative accusative object) occupies position 1 → verb second → subject third: _Was – erledigst – du_.

---

65. Ich organisiere die Meetings und schreibe E-Mails. Viele E-Mails. 🇬🇧 I'm organising the meetings and writing emails. Lots of emails. → _Die Meetings_ = **accusative plural** (definite, direct object). Coordinated clause with _und_ shares the same subject (_ich_) without repetition.

---

66. Wann machst du Pause? 🇬🇧 When do you take a break? → _Pause machen_ = fixed idiomatic expression ("to take a break") — _Pause_ used **without article** here, making it part of a verb-noun compound.

---

67. Vielleicht um Mittag. Aber gelegentlich vergesse ich die Pause. 🇬🇧 Maybe around noon. But occasionally I forget the break. → _Gelegentlich_ (occasionally) in position 1 → **verb-second**: _vergesse – ich_. _Die Pause_ = accusative feminine (direct object of _vergessen_).

---

68. Du musst entspannen, Sabine! Das ist wichtig. 🇬🇧 You must relax, Sabine! That is important. → _Entspannen_ at the end — modal _musst_ sends the dependent infinitive to the **final position**.

---

69. Ich weiß, ich weiß. Am Wochenende entspanne ich. 🇬🇧 I know, I know. On the weekend I'll relax. → _Am Wochenende_ in position 1 → verb-second → subject follows: _Am Wochenende – entspanne – ich_ (inversion).

---

70. Gut. Und wann ist dein Feierabend heute? 🇬🇧 Good. And when is your end of work today? → _Dein Feierabend_ — _Feierabend_ is masculine, nominative → possessive _dein_ (no ending in masculine nominative).

---

71. Heute spät. Vielleicht um neun Uhr. 🇬🇧 Late today. Maybe at nine o'clock. → Elliptical answer (verb omitted). _Um neun Uhr_ — fixed clock-time structure: _um_ + cardinal number + _Uhr_ (no article, no case inflection visible).

---

72. Das ist zu spät! Du arbeitest zu viel. 🇬🇧 That is too late! You work too much. → _Zu_ before adjective/adverb = "too" (excess). Not to be confused with _zu_ as preposition (dative) or _zu_ + infinitive.

---

73. Das ist der Alltag im Büro. Aber danke für deine Sorge, Markus! 🇬🇧 That's everyday office life. But thanks for your concern, Markus! → _Im Büro_ = _in dem Büro_ — **dative** (location). _Für deine Sorge_ — _für_ + accusative → _deine Sorge_ (feminine accusative, _dein-_ + _-e_).

---

74. Hallo, Peter! Was machst du am Wochenende? 🇬🇧 Hello, Peter! What are you doing on the weekend? → _Am Wochenende_ = _an dem Wochenende_ — **dative** (time expression with _an_). _Das Wochenende_ is neuter → dative _dem_.

---

75. Hallo, Julia! Ich plane ein bisschen Freizeit. Endlich! 🇬🇧 Hello, Julia! I'm planning a little free time. Finally! → _Ein bisschen_ = "a little" — **uninflected** fixed quantifier, no case ending regardless of what follows.

---

76. Oh, schön! Du bist immer so beschäftigt unter der Woche. 🇬🇧 Oh, nice! You're always so busy during the week. → _Unter der Woche_ = idiomatic expression for "during the week" — _unter_ + dative → _der Woche_ (feminine dative).

---

77. Ja, täglich arbeite ich sehr viel. Das Wochenende ist meine Erholung. 🇬🇧 Yes, I work very much every day. The weekend is my recovery. → _Täglich_ (daily) in position 1 → **inversion**: verb (_arbeite_) before subject (_ich_). _Meine Erholung_ — feminine nominative predicate (_die Erholung_).

---

78. Was hast du geplant? Was machst du Samstag? 🇬🇧 What have you planned? What are you doing on Saturday? → **Perfekt** of _planen_: _geplant_ (regular, _ge-_ + stem + _-t_). _Samstag_ used without preposition or article as a **bare time adverb** (day of the week can stand alone).

---

79. Samstag schlafe ich lange und dann entspanne ich zu Hause. 🇬🇧 On Saturday I'll sleep in and then relax at home. → _Samstag_ in position 1 → inversion (_schlafe – ich_). _Zu Hause_ = fixed dative expression meaning "at home" (not directional).

---

80. Sonntag habe ich eine Verabredung mit meinem Bruder. Wir verbringen den Tag zusammen. 🇬🇧 On Sunday I have a meeting with my brother. We'll spend the day together. → _Mit meinem Bruder_ — _mit_ always dative → _meinem_ (masculine dative _-em_ ending). _Den Tag_ = **accusative masculine** time span (direct object of _verbringen_).

---

81. Was verbringt ihr zusammen? Wohin geht ihr? 🇬🇧 What are you spending together? Where are you going? → _Wohin_ (where to) signals **direction** (accusative context), contrasting with _wo_ (where, location/dative). Use _wohin_ for motion toward a place.

---

82. Wir gehen in den Park. 🇬🇧 We're going to the park. → _In_ + accusative for **direction** (_gehen in_ = motion toward) → _den Park_ (masculine accusative). Compare: _im Park_ (dative, location).

---

83. Er hat einen Hund und eine Katze. 🇬🇧 He has a dog and a cat. → _Einen Hund_ = **accusative masculine** (_haben_ → accusative; _der Hund_ → _einen Hund_). _Eine Katze_ = **accusative feminine** (_die Katze_ → _eine Katze_).

---

84. Süß! Wie heißt der Hund? 🇬🇧 Sweet! What is the dog called? → _Heißen_ (to be called) takes a **nominative complement** — both subject (_der Hund_) and name (_Bär_) are in nominative.

---

85. Der Hund heißt Bär. Er ist groß und braun. 🇬🇧 The dog is called Bear. It is big and brown. → _Er_ refers to _der Hund_ — **masculine pronoun** matching grammatical gender. Predicate adjectives (_groß_, _braun_) uninflected after _sein_.

---

86. Und die Katze? Die Katze heißt Maus. Sie ist klein und weiß. 🇬🇧 And the cat? The cat is called Mouse. She is small and white. → _Sie_ refers to _die Katze_ — **feminine pronoun**. In German, pronoun gender follows **grammatical gender**, not natural sex.

---

87. Das ist sehr kreativ! Ich finde das lustig. 🇬🇧 That is very creative! I find that funny. → _Finden_ + accusative = "to find/consider something [adjective]". _Das_ = **accusative neuter** (direct object). _Lustig_ = uninflected predicate-like complement.

---

88. Ja, mein Bruder ist sehr kreativ und lustig. 🇬🇧 Yes, my brother is very creative and funny. → _Mein Bruder_ — masculine nominative subject → possessive _mein_ (no ending in masculine nominative).

---

89. Habt ihr auch Pläne für den Abend? 🇬🇧 Do you also have plans for the evening? → _Pläne_ = **accusative plural** of _der Plan_ (irregular plural: _Plan → Pläne_, umlaut). _Für den Abend_ — _für_ + accusative masculine → _den Abend_.

---

90. Ja, wir verbringen den Abend im Restaurant. Wir essen zusammen. 🇬🇧 Yes, we're spending the evening at the restaurant. We eat together. → _Den Abend_ = **accusative** time expression (direct object of _verbringen_). _Im Restaurant_ = _in dem_ — **dative** (location, neuter _das Restaurant_).

---

91. Welches Restaurant? Kennst du ein gutes? 🇬🇧 Which restaurant? Do you know a good one? → _Welches_ — interrogative adjective: neuter nominative (_das Restaurant_ → _welches_). _Ein gutes_ = **accusative neuter** with adjective ending _-es_ (strong declension, no article before adjective visible).

---

92. Ja, es gibt ein neues Restaurant neben dem Supermarkt. 🇬🇧 Yes, there is a new restaurant next to the supermarket. → _Es gibt_ + **accusative** = "there is/are." _Ein neues Restaurant_ = accusative neuter (strong adj. ending _-es_). _Neben dem Supermarkt_ — _neben_ + dative (location).

---

93. Oh, ich kenne es! Das Essen dort ist super. 🇬🇧 Oh, I know it! The food there is great. → _Es_ = accusative neuter pronoun replacing _das Restaurant_. _Das Essen_ = verbal noun (infinitive used as noun, always **neuter**).

---

94. Komm doch mit! Wir haben Platz für drei Personen. 🇬🇧 Come along! We have room for three people. → **Separable verb imperative**: _mitkommen_ → imperative _komm … mit_ (prefix _mit-_ goes to the end). _Doch_ softens the invitation (modal particle).

---

95. Wirklich? Das ist sehr freundlich von dir. Wann beginnt das Abendessen? 🇬🇧 Really? That's very nice of you. When does dinner start? → _Von dir_ — _von_ always **dative** → _dir_ (dative of _du_). _Das Abendessen_ is neuter (compound noun gender = gender of last component: _das Essen_).

---

96. Um sieben Uhr. Bist du pünktlich? 🇬🇧 At seven o'clock. Are you punctual? → Yes/no question: **verb first**, subject second → _Bist – du_. No inversion needed for W-questions; this is a polar question (no W-word).

---

97. Ja, ich bin immer pünktlich! Bis Sonntag dann, Peter! 🇬🇧 Yes, I'm always on time! Until Sunday then, Peter! → _Bis Sonntag_ = "until/see you Sunday" — _bis_ + bare day name (no article, no preposition), common farewell formula.

---

98. Guten Morgen, Klaus! Weißt du, dass nächste Woche ein Feiertag ist? 🇬🇧 Good morning, Klaus! Did you know that next week is a public holiday? → _Dass_ introduces a **subordinate clause** → verb _ist_ goes to the very end. _Dass_-clauses always send the finite verb to the final position.

---

99. Guten Morgen, Emma! Ja, ich weiß. Ich freue mich sehr darauf. 🇬🇧 Good morning, Emma! Yes, I know. I'm really looking forward to it. → **Reflexive verb** _sich freuen_ + _darauf_: _freuen_ requires _sich_ (accusative → _mich_). _Darauf_ = _da-_ + preposition _auf_ — used instead of _auf es_ when referring to a thing/event.

---

100. Was planst du am Feiertag? 🇬🇧 What are you planning for the holiday? → _Am Feiertag_ = _an dem Feiertag_ — **dative** (masculine, time expression with _an_).

---

101. Ich plane eine kleine Reise. Ich verbringe den Tag mit meiner Familie. 🇬🇧 I'm planning a small trip. I'll spend the day with my family. → _Eine kleine Reise_ = **accusative feminine** (_haben/planen_ takes accusative). Adjective _kleine_ gets _-e_ ending (weak declension after indefinite article accusative feminine). _Mit meiner Familie_ — _mit_ + dative → _meiner_ (feminine dative _-er_ ending).

---

102. Oh, wohin fahrt ihr? 🇬🇧 Oh, where are you going? → _Wohin_ (where to) for **direction**; _fahren_ (to drive/travel) is a motion verb → direction with _wohin_. _Ihr_ = 2nd person plural (addressing a group).

---

103. Wir fahren in die Schweiz. Meine Oma wohnt dort. 🇬🇧 We're driving to Switzerland. My grandmother lives there. → _In die Schweiz_ — country names with articles (_die Schweiz_ is feminine) → _in_ + **accusative** for direction → _die_ stays _die_ (feminine accusative same form). Most countries have no article; _die Schweiz_ is an exception.

---

104. Sie ist achtzig Jahre alt. Sie ist noch sehr aktiv und ruhig. 🇬🇧 She is eighty years old. She is still very active and calm. → _Achtzig Jahre alt_ — age expressed as: number + _Jahre_ (nominative plural) + _alt_ (adjective). No _sein_ phrase inflection needed.

---

105. Das ist toll! Wie lange dauert die Reise? 🇬🇧 That's great! How long does the journey take? → _Wie lange_ (how long) asks about **duration**. _Die Reise_ = feminine nominative subject of _dauern_.

---

106. Die Reise dauert etwa sechs Stunden mit dem Auto. 🇬🇧 The journey takes about six hours by car. → _Mit dem Auto_ — _mit_ + **dative** → _dem Auto_ (neuter dative). Used for means of transport in German.

---

107. Das ist lang! Beginnt ihr früh am Morgen? 🇬🇧 That's a long time! Are you starting early in the morning? → Polar question: **verb first** (_Beginnt – ihr_). _Am Morgen_ = _an dem Morgen_ — dative (time with _an_).

---

108. Ja, wir beginnen um fünf Uhr morgens. Wir wollen pünktlich ankommen. 🇬🇧 Yes, we start at five in the morning. We want to arrive on time. → **Separable verb** _ankommen_ with modal _wollen_: infinitive stays whole at the end → _ankommen_ (not split). _Morgens_ = adverb "in the morning" (_-s_ suffix, uninflected).

---

109. Fünf Uhr! Das ist sehr früh. Ich vergesse immer, früh aufzustehen. 🇬🇧 Five o'clock! That's very early. I always forget to get up early. → _Aufzustehen_ = **zu-infinitive of separable verb**: _zu_ is inserted between prefix and stem → _auf-zu-stehen_.

---

110. Ich auch! Aber meine Frau erinnert mich immer. 🇬🇧 Me too! But my wife always reminds me. → _Erinnern_ + accusative: _mich_ = **accusative of _ich_** (person being reminded is the direct object). Compare Dialogue 2: _erinnere dich_ (accusative _dich_ for _du_).

---

111. Und du, Emma? Hast du Pläne für den Feiertag? 🇬🇧 And you, Emma? Do you have plans for the holiday? → Note: grammatically Klaus says _"Und du, Emma?"_ — addressing Emma though he is Klaus. _Du_ is **nominative** subject of implied _hast du_.

---

112. Ich? Ich habe keine großen Pläne. Gelegentlich bleibe ich zu Hause. 🇬🇧 Me? I have no big plans. Occasionally I stay at home. → _Keine großen Pläne_ — negation with **kein** (no/not any): _keine_ = accusative plural. Adjective _großen_ gets _-en_ (weak/mixed declension after _kein-_ plural accusative).

---

113. Das ist auch schön. Entspannen ist wichtig. 🇬🇧 That is also nice. Relaxing is important. → _Entspannen_ as **nominalized infinitive** (subject of sentence): infinitives used as nouns are always **neuter** (_das Entspannen_), but the article is often dropped in general statements.

---

114. Ja. Vielleicht besuche ich meine Kusine. Sie wohnt in der Nähe. 🇬🇧 Yes. Maybe I'll visit my cousin. She lives nearby. → _Meine Kusine_ = **accusative feminine** (direct object of _besuchen_; _die Kusine_ → _meine Kusine_, same form accusative/nominative for feminine). _In der Nähe_ — _in_ + dative (location) → _der Nähe_ (feminine dative).

---

115. Das ist eine gute Idee. Wie heißt deine Kusine? 🇬🇧 That's a good idea. What is your cousin called? → _Deine Kusine_ — feminine nominative → _dein-_ + _-e_ (feminine nominative ending). _Eine gute Idee_ = feminine nominative predicate with adjective _-e_ ending (weak after _eine_).

---

116. Sie heißt Sara. Sie ist sehr lustig und nett. 🇬🇧 She is called Sara. She is very funny and nice. → _Sie heißt_ — _heißen_ + nominative name (_Sara_ in nominative as complement). _Lustig_ and _nett_ = uninflected predicate adjectives.

---

117. Dann verbringt ihr den Feiertag zusammen. 🇬🇧 Then you'll spend the holiday together. → _Ihr_ = **2nd person plural** (addressing both Emma and Sara implicitly). _Den Feiertag_ = accusative masculine (direct object of _verbringen_).

---

118. Ja, ich hoffe es. Ich erinnere sie heute noch. 🇬🇧 Yes, I hope so. I'll remind her today still. → _Ich hoffe es_ — _es_ is a **placeholder accusative** for the content of hope (not referring to a specific noun). _Sie_ here = **accusative feminine** (her = Sara, direct object of _erinnern_).

---

119. Gut. Habt ihr dann einen schönen Feiertag! 🇬🇧 Good. Have a wonderful holiday then! → **Wish expressed as statement** (not imperative, but uses _ihr_-form). _Einen schönen Feiertag_ = **accusative masculine** (_haben_ → accusative; adjective _-en_ ending, weak after _einen_).

---

120. Danke, Klaus! Dir auch. Grüß deine Oma von mir! 🇬🇧 Thank you, Klaus! Same to you. Say hello to your grandmother from me! → **Imperative of _grüßen_**: du-imperative → _grüß_. _Deine Oma_ = **accusative feminine** (direct object). _Von mir_ — _von_ + dative → _mir_.

---

121. Hallo, Stefan! Wo bist du? Du bist zwanzig Minuten verspätet! 🇬🇧 Hello, Stefan! Where are you? You are twenty minutes late! → _Verspätet_ = past participle used as **predicate adjective** ("belated/late"). _Zwanzig Minuten_ = **accusative** time extent (without preposition).

---

122. Hallo, Nina! Es tut mir leid. Ich habe Stress. Der Bus hat Verspätung. 🇬🇧 Hello, Nina! I'm sorry. I'm stressed. The bus is delayed. → _Es tut mir leid_ = "I'm sorry" — fixed expression: _es_ = subject, _mir_ = **dative** (it does sorrow _to me_). _Verspätung haben_ = idiomatic "to be delayed."

---

123. Ach so. Das verstehe ich. Aber wir warten schon lange hier. 🇬🇧 I see. I understand that. But we've been waiting here for a long time. → _Schon lange_ = "for a long time (already)" — German **present tense** expresses ongoing actions that started in the past + _schon_ (unlike English "have been waiting").

---

124. Ich weiß, ich weiß. Ich beeile mich gerade. 🇬🇧 I know, I know. I'm hurrying right now. → _Mich_ = **accusative reflexive** (1st person singular) with _sich beeilen_. _Gerade_ = "right now/at this moment."

---

125. Wir haben einen Termin um drei Uhr. Es ist jetzt zwanzig nach zwei. 🇬🇧 We have an appointment at three o'clock. It is now twenty past two. → _Zwanzig nach zwei_ = colloquial time expression: "twenty past two." German also uses the 24-hour format or _halb drei_ (half past two = 2:30, lit. "half three").

---

126. Ich komme in zehn Minuten an. Können wir den Termin verschieben? 🇬🇧 I'll arrive in ten minutes. Can we postpone the appointment? → **Separable verb** _ankommen_: prefix _an-_ goes to end in main clause → _komme … an_. _Den Termin_ = accusative masculine.

---

127. Nein, wir verschieben ihn nicht. Der Arzt wartet nicht lange. 🇬🇧 No, we're not postponing it. The doctor doesn't wait long. → _Ihn_ = **accusative masculine pronoun** replacing _den Termin_ (_der Termin_ → accusative → _ihn_).

---

128. Okay, ich verstehe. Ich laufe schnell. 🇬🇧 OK, I understand. I'll run quickly. → _Laufen_ (to run/walk fast) — present tense used for immediate future action. _Schnell_ = adverb, uninflected.

---

129. Gut. Bist du pünktlich beim nächsten Mal? 🇬🇧 Good. Will you be punctual next time? → _Beim nächsten Mal_ = _bei dem nächsten Mal_ — **dative** (_das Mal_ → _dem Mal_, neuter). Adjective _nächsten_ has _-en_ ending (weak declension after _dem_).

---

130. Ja! Gewöhnlich bin ich pünktlich. Heute ist eine Ausnahme. 🇬🇧 Yes! I'm usually punctual. Today is an exception. → _Gewöhnlich_ in position 1 → **verb-second inversion**: _bin – ich_. _Eine Ausnahme_ = feminine nominative predicate.

---

131. Das sagst du jedes Mal, Stefan! 🇬🇧 You say that every time, Stefan! → _Jedes Mal_ = **accusative neuter** time expression (_das Mal_ → _jedes Mal_, neuter accusative). _Das_ = accusative neuter object of _sagen_.

---

132. Nein, das stimmt nicht! Ich bin regelmäßig pünktlich. 🇬🇧 No, that's not true! I'm regularly punctual. → _Das stimmt nicht_ = "That's not right/true" — negation with _nicht_ after the subject/verb, negating the whole statement.

---

133. Gelegentlich bist du verspätet. Das ist deine Gewohnheit. 🇬🇧 Occasionally you are late. That is your habit. → _Gelegentlich_ in position 1 → inversion: _bist – du_. _Deine Gewohnheit_ — _die Gewohnheit_ is feminine → possessive _deine_ (feminine nominative predicate).

---

134. Okay, vielleicht ein bisschen. Aber ich arbeite daran. 🇬🇧 OK, maybe a little. But I'm working on it. → _Daran_ = _da-_ + _an_ — **da-compound** replacing _an es/ihm_ when referring to a thing/idea. Used because _an_ + thing → _daran_ (not _an es_).

---

135. Ich erinnere dich immer, aber du vergisst es trotzdem. 🇬🇧 I always remind you, but you forget it anyway. → _Trotzdem_ (nevertheless/anyway) — **concessive adverb** that can stand in position 1 or after the verb. Here after the verb for emphasis.

---

136. Ich vergesse es nicht absichtlich. Ich habe einfach viel Stress im Alltag. 🇬🇧 I don't forget it on purpose. I simply have a lot of stress in daily life. → _Absichtlich_ (on purpose) — manner adverb. _Einfach_ here is a **modal particle** meaning "simply/just," softening the explanation rather than meaning "easy."

---

137. Du hast recht. Ich entschuldige mich wirklich. 🇬🇧 You are right. I'm truly sorry. → _Sich entschuldigen_ = **reflexive verb** "to apologise": _mich_ = accusative reflexive (1st person singular). Literally "I excuse myself."

---

138. Schon gut. Ah, da bist du ja! Endlich! 🇬🇧 It's alright. Ah, there you are! Finally! → _Schon gut_ = fixed expression "it's OK/never mind." _Da bist du ja_ — _ja_ is a **modal particle** expressing mild surprise or relief ("well, there you are!").

---

139. Ich bin da! Tut mir leid, Nina. Beginnt der Termin noch? 🇬🇧 I'm here! I'm sorry, Nina. Is the appointment still starting? → _Beginnt der Termin noch?_ — polar question (verb first). _Noch_ = "still" — asking if the appointment is still going ahead.

---

140. Ja, wir haben noch fünf Minuten. Komm schnell! 🇬🇧 Yes, we still have five minutes. Come quickly! → _Noch fünf Minuten_ — _noch_ = "still" modifying the time remaining. _Komm_ = **du-imperative** of _kommen_ (drop _-st_, no _-e_ needed).

---

141. Danke für deine Geduld, Nina. Du bist sehr nett. 🇬🇧 Thank you for your patience, Nina. You are very nice. → _Für deine Geduld_ — _für_ + **accusative** → _deine Geduld_ (feminine accusative, _dein-_ + _-e_ ending).

---

142. Ja, ja. Aber beim nächsten Mal beeile dich früher! 🇬🇧 Yes, yes. But next time hurry up earlier! → _Früher_ = **comparative of _früh_** (early → earlier). _Beeile dich_ = du-imperative of reflexive _sich beeilen_ → _beeile + dich_ (accusative reflexive).

---

143. Guten Abend, Laura! Wie war dein Tag? 🇬🇧 Good evening, Laura! How was your day? → _Wie war dein Tag?_ — **simple past (Präteritum)** of _sein_: _war_ (was). _Dein Tag_ — _der Tag_ is masculine → possessive _dein_ (masculine nominative, no ending).

---

144. Guten Abend, Tobias! Sehr beschäftigt wie immer. Und deiner? 🇬🇧 Good evening, Tobias! Very busy as always. And yours? → _Und deiner?_ = elliptical "And yours [= your day]?" — _deiner_ is a **pronoun replacing _dein Tag_**: masculine nominative pronoun form of possessive → _deiner_ (strong ending _-er_).

---

145. Ich habe viele Aufgaben erledigt. 🇬🇧 I completed many tasks. → **Perfekt** of _erledigen_ (regular): _ge-_ + _erledig-_ + _-t_ → _erledigt_. _Viele Aufgaben_ = accusative plural (direct object).

---

146. Was erledigst du täglich? Erzähl mir von deinem Alltag. 🇬🇧 What do you do every day? Tell me about your daily life. → _Erzähl mir von deinem Alltag_ — **du-imperative** of _erzählen_ + _mir_ (dative, "to me") + _von_ + dative → _deinem Alltag_ (masculine dative _-em_ ending).

---

147. Ich stehe um sechs Uhr auf, frühstücke, und beginne dann mit der Arbeit. 🇬🇧 I get up at six o'clock, have breakfast, and then start work. → **Separable verb** _aufstehen_: prefix _auf-_ to the end → _stehe … auf_. Three coordinated verbs share the subject _ich_ with commas.

---

148. Sie beginnt um acht Uhr. Ich arbeite bis Mittag ohne Pause. 🇬🇧 It starts at eight o'clock. I work until noon without a break. → _Ohne Pause_ — _ohne_ always + **accusative** → but _Pause_ here has no article (zero article, often used with _ohne_ for general reference). _Ohne_ + accusative is the rule.

---

149. Ohne Pause? Das ist nicht gesund! 🇬🇧 Without a break? That's not healthy! → _Nicht gesund_ — _nicht_ before a **predicate adjective** to negate it. Here _nicht_ modifies _gesund_ specifically (not the whole sentence).

---

150. Ich weiß. Ich vergesse manchmal die Pause. Es ist eine schlechte Gewohnheit. 🇬🇧 I know. I sometimes forget the break. It's a bad habit. → _Die Pause_ = **accusative feminine** (direct object of _vergessen_). _Eine schlechte Gewohnheit_ — adjective _schlechte_: _-e_ ending (weak declension after indefinite article, feminine nominative predicate).

---

151. Ich mache regelmäßig eine Pause um zehn Uhr. Das ist wichtig. 🇬🇧 I regularly take a break at ten o'clock. That is important. → _Eine Pause machen_ = idiomatic "to take a break." _Regelmäßig_ (regularly) placed after the verb but before the object — standard **adverb position** for manner.

---

152. Das ist eine gute Gewohnheit. Wie lange pausierst du? 🇬🇧 That's a good habit. How long do you pause? → _Pausieren_ used as a full verb ("to pause/take a break") — note this is slightly informal; more standard would be _Pause machen_. _Wie lange_ asks duration.

---

153. Ich pausiere etwa fünfzehn Minuten. Dann arbeite ich weiter. 🇬🇧 I pause for about fifteen minutes. Then I keep working. → **Separable verb** _weiterarbeiten_: prefix _weiter-_ goes to end → _arbeite … weiter_. _Etwa fünfzehn Minuten_ = accusative time duration (no preposition).

---

154. Gewöhnlich um fünf Uhr. Manchmal um sechs. 🇬🇧 Usually at five o'clock. Sometimes at six. → Elliptical answers (verb omitted, understood from context). _Manchmal_ (sometimes) contrasts with _gewöhnlich_ (usually) — both are **frequency adverbs**.

---

155. Ich entspanne gern. Ich lese Bücher oder sehe fern. 🇬🇧 I like to relax. I read books or watch TV. → **Separable verb** _fernsehen_ (to watch TV): prefix _fern-_ to the end → _sehe … fern_. _Gern_ after verb = "like to [do something]."

---

156. Ich verbringe meine Freizeit im Garten. Das entspannt mich. 🇬🇧 I spend my free time in the garden. That relaxes me. → _Meine Freizeit_ = **accusative feminine** (direct object; _die Freizeit_ → _meine Freizeit_). _Im Garten_ = dative (location). _Mich_ = accusative object of _entspannen_.

---

157. Oh, du hast einen Garten? Wie groß ist er? 🇬🇧 Oh, you have a garden? How big is it? → _Einen Garten_ = **accusative masculine** (_haben_ → accusative; _der Garten_ → _einen Garten_). _Er_ = masculine pronoun replacing _der Garten_.

---

158. Er ist nicht sehr groß, aber schön. Ich habe Blumen und Bäume. 🇬🇧 It is not very big, but beautiful. I have flowers and trees. → _Blumen_ (flowers, plural of _die Blume_) and _Bäume_ (trees, plural of _der Baum_ — umlaut plural). Both **accusative plural** after _haben_.

---

159. Das klingt wunderschön. Ist das eine tägliche Beschäftigung? 🇬🇧 That sounds wonderful. Is that a daily activity? → _Tägliche Beschäftigung_ — adjective _täglich_ + _-e_ ending (weak declension after indefinite article, feminine nominative predicate). _Die Beschäftigung_ is feminine.

---

160. Nein, nur wöchentlich. Am Samstag verbringe ich Zeit im Garten. 🇬🇧 No, only weekly. On Saturday I spend time in the garden. → _Am Samstag_ in position 1 → **inversion**: _verbringe – ich_. _Zeit_ used without article (general concept of "time") → accusative but unmarked.

---

161. Am Sonntag entspanne ich komplett. Das ist meine Gewohnheit. 🇬🇧 On Sunday I relax completely. That is my habit. → _Komplett_ = adverb "completely," placed after the verb. _Meine Gewohnheit_ — feminine nominative predicate (_die Gewohnheit_).

---

162. Das ist eine sehr gute und regelmäßige Routine, Tobias! 🇬🇧 That is a very good and regular routine, Tobias! → _Eine sehr gute und regelmäßige Routine_ — two adjectives coordinated with _und_, both with _-e_ ending (weak declension after _eine_, feminine nominative predicate).

---

163. Danke, Laura. Regelmäßigkeit ist der Schlüssel zum Glück, glaube ich. 🇬🇧 Thank you, Laura. Regularity is the key to happiness, I believe. → _Zum Glück_ = _zu dem Glück_ — **dative** (_zu_ always dative; _das Glück_ → _dem Glück_, contracted _zum_). _Glaube ich_ appended as a **parenthetical clause** (verb-subject inversion in this tag).

---

164. Hallo, Ben! Machst du gerade Pause? 🇬🇧 Hello, Ben! Are you taking a break right now? → _Pause machen_ — _Pause_ without article (idiomatic). Polar question: verb first → _Machst – du_.

---

165. Hallo, Ben! Ja, ich pausiere kurz. Ich bin sehr müde heute. 🇬🇧 Hello, Ben! Yes, I'm taking a short break. I am very tired today. → _Kurz_ here functions as an **adverb** (briefly), not adjective. _Heute_ at end as a time adverb — standard final position for time adverbs.

---

166. Warum bist du so müde? Hast du schlecht geschlafen? 🇬🇧 Why are you so tired? Did you sleep badly? → _So müde_ — _so_ as an intensifier (so/this) before adjective, similar to _sehr_ but indicates degree with possible cause implied.

---

167. Nein, ich habe gut geschlafen. Aber der Stress heute ist groß. 🇬🇧 No, I slept well. But the stress today is great. → _Der Stress_ is **masculine** (_der Stress_) — loanword gender must be memorised. _Groß_ = uninflected predicate adjective.

---

168. Was hast du erledigt heute? 🇬🇧 What have you taken care of today? → Non-standard word order (time adverb _heute_ moved to end for emphasis). Standard: _Was hast du heute erledigt?_ — both are grammatically possible.

---

169. Ich habe drei Termine organisiert und fünf Berichte vorbereitet. 🇬🇧 I organised three appointments and prepared five reports. → **Perfekt** with two coordinated past participles (_organisiert_, _vorbereitet_) sharing auxiliary _habe_. Both are **regular participles** (_ge-_ + stem + _-t_).

---

170. Das ist sehr viel! Kein Wunder, dass du müde bist. 🇬🇧 That's a lot! No wonder that you're tired. → _Dass_ = **subordinating conjunction** → verb _bist_ goes to the end of the clause. _Kein Wunder_ = "no wonder" — fixed expression, _kein_ negates the noun.

---

171. Ja. Ich brauche eine längere Pause. Vielleicht zehn Minuten. 🇬🇧 Yes. I need a longer break. Maybe ten minutes. → _Längere_ = **comparative of _lang_** + _-e_ ending (weak declension after _eine_, accusative feminine → _eine längere Pause_).

---

172. Zehn Minuten reichen nicht. Du musst mehr entspannen. 🇬🇧 Ten minutes is not enough. You must relax more. → _Reichen_ (to suffice) — plural subject _Minuten_ → plural verb _reichen_. _Mehr_ = comparative adverb "more" (modifying _entspannen_).

---

173. Ich weiß, aber ich habe noch viel zu erledigen. 🇬🇧 I know, but I still have a lot to do. → _Viel zu erledigen_ — _zu_ + infinitive expressing **remaining task**: "a lot to be done." _Noch_ (still) indicates ongoing state.

---

174. Was kommt noch? 🇬🇧 What else is there? → _Noch_ = "else/more/still" in questions. _Kommen_ used idiomatically for "what is still coming/remaining."

---

175. Ich muss noch zwei Meetings vorbereiten. Und die Dokumente beenden. 🇬🇧 I still need to prepare two more meetings. And finish the documents. → _Noch_ before numeral = "still/another" (_noch zwei_ = two more). _Beenden_ = "to finish" — separable? No, _beenden_ is not separable (prefix _be-_ is inseparable).

---

176. Wann beendest du die Dokumente? 🇬🇧 When will you finish the documents? → _Die Dokumente_ = **accusative plural** (direct object of _beenden_). Definite article _die_ (plural, same form for all genders).

---

177. Ich beende sie vor dem Feierabend. Vielleicht um fünf Uhr. 🇬🇧 I'll finish them before the end of work. Maybe at five o'clock. → _Vor dem Feierabend_ — _vor_ + **dative** (location/time before something) → _dem Feierabend_ (masculine dative). _Sie_ = accusative plural pronoun (replacing _die Dokumente_).

---

178. Und wann beginnt das nächste Meeting? 🇬🇧 And when does the next meeting start? → _Das nächste Meeting_ — _Meeting_ is **neuter** (loanword, defaults to neuter in German). Adjective _nächste_ with _-e_ ending (weak, after definite article, neuter nominative).

---

179. Es beginnt um drei Uhr. Ich habe noch eine Stunde. 🇬🇧 It starts at three o'clock. I still have one hour. → _Noch eine Stunde_ — _noch_ + indefinite article = "one more/still one." _Eine Stunde_ = **accusative feminine** (direct object of _haben_).

---

180. Dann nutze die Zeit! Entspann dich jetzt. 🇬🇧 Then use the time! Relax now. → _Nutze_ = du-imperative of _nutzen_ (use). _Entspann dich_ = du-imperative of reflexive _sich entspannen_: _entspann + dich_ (accusative reflexive).

---

181. Du hast recht. Die Eile hilft mir nicht. 🇬🇧 You are right. The hurry doesn't help me. → _Die Eile_ is **feminine** (_die Eile_ = the haste/hurry). _Mir_ = **dative** — _helfen_ always takes dative (_helfen + dative_): "helps to me."

---

182. Genau. Pünktlichkeit ist wichtig, aber Gesundheit auch. 🇬🇧 Exactly. Punctuality is important, but health too. → Both _Pünktlichkeit_ and _Gesundheit_ are **feminine abstract nouns** (_die Pünktlichkeit_, _die Gesundheit_) — the _-keit/-heit_ suffix always produces feminine nouns.

---

183. Stimmt. Ich trinke jetzt einen Kaffee und entspanne fünf Minuten. 🇬🇧 True. I'll now drink a coffee and relax for five minutes. → _Einen Kaffee_ = **accusative masculine** (_haben/trinken_ → accusative; _der Kaffee_ → _einen Kaffee_). _Fünf Minuten_ = accusative time span (duration, no preposition).

---

184. Gut! Kann ich dir helfen? Ich bin gerade frei. 🇬🇧 Good! Can I help you? I'm free right now. → _Helfen_ + **dative**: _dir_ = dative of _du_ (help _to you_). Reminder: _helfen_ never takes accusative.

---

185. Wirklich? Das wäre super! Kannst du die E-Mails erledigen? 🇬🇧 Really? That would be great! Can you take care of the emails? → _Wäre_ = **Konjunktiv II of _sein_** (_würde sein_ → _wäre_) expressing a polite/hypothetical reaction ("that would be great"). Common at A2+ for polite expressions.

---

186. Ja, natürlich. Das erledige ich für dich. 🇬🇧 Yes, of course. I'll take care of that for you. → _Das_ in position 1 (demonstrative pronoun as object) → **inversion**: _erledige – ich_. _Für dich_ — _für_ + accusative → _dich_.

---

187. Danke, Ben! Du bist sehr nett und hilfsbereit. 🇬🇧 Thank you, Ben! You are very nice and helpful. → _Hilfsbereit_ = compound adjective (_Hilfe_ + _bereit_ = "help-ready" = helpful). Predicate adjective → uninflected after _sein_.

---

188. Kein Problem. Kollegen helfen sich gegenseitig! 🇬🇧 No problem. Colleagues help each other! → _Sich gegenseitig_ = **reciprocal expression** "each other." _Helfen_ + dative — but with reciprocal _sich_, the dative is absorbed into _sich_ (dative reflexive, 3rd person plural → _sich_).

---

189. Guten Morgen, David! Hast du deinen Wochenplan schon organisiert? 🇬🇧 Good morning, David! Have you already organised your weekly plan? → _Deinen Wochenplan_ = **accusative masculine** (direct object of _organisiert_; _dein_ + _-en_ accusative masculine ending).

---

190. Guten Morgen, Sophie! Nein, noch nicht. Ich beginne gerade damit. 🇬🇧 Good morning, Sophie! No, not yet. I'm just starting with it. → _Damit_ = _da-_ + _mit_ — **da-compound** replacing _mit ihm/es_ when referring to a thing (the plan). Used because _mit_ + thing → _damit_.

---

191. Ich helfe dir gern. Was hast du diese Woche zu tun? 🇬🇧 I'm happy to help you. What do you have to do this week? → _Gern_ after verb = "gladly/happily." _Was … zu tun_ = _haben + zu + infinitive_ expresses **obligation/necessity** ("have to do").

---

192. Montag und Dienstag habe ich täglich zwei Termine. 🇬🇧 On Monday and Tuesday I have two appointments each day. → _Montag und Dienstag_ in position 1 (two time adverbs) → **inversion**: _habe – ich_. _Zwei Termine_ = accusative plural (direct object of _haben_).

---

193. Mittwoch ist ein Feiertag. Ich bin frei! 🇬🇧 Wednesday is a public holiday. I'm free! → _Mittwoch_ as time adverb in position 1 → inversion: _ist – ein Feiertag_. _Ein Feiertag_ = masculine nominative predicate.

---

194. Oh, schön! Was planst du für den Feiertag? 🇬🇧 Oh, nice! What are you planning for the holiday? → _Für den Feiertag_ — _für_ + **accusative masculine** → _den Feiertag_.

---

195. Ich plane einen ruhigen Tag zu Hause. Ich will entspannen. 🇬🇧 I'm planning a quiet day at home. I want to relax. → _Einen ruhigen Tag_ = **accusative masculine** (direct object; adjective _ruhigen_ with _-en_ ending, weak declension after _einen_, accusative masculine).

---

196. Das klingt gut. Du arbeitest sehr regelmäßig. Du brauchst Erholung. 🇬🇧 That sounds good. You work very regularly. You need recovery/rest. → _Erholung_ used **without article** (general/abstract concept). _Brauchen_ + accusative — but here with zero article for indefinite uncountable noun.

---

197. Ja. Und Donnerstag? Da habe ich ein wichtiges Meeting. 🇬🇧 Yes. And Thursday? Then I have an important meeting. → _Da_ here = "then/in that case" (temporal demonstrative), not "there." _Ein wichtiges Meeting_ = **accusative neuter** (adjective _wichtiges_ with _-es_, strong declension after _ein_, neuter accusative).

---

198. Um wie viel Uhr beginnt das Meeting? 🇬🇧 What time does the meeting start? → _Um wie viel Uhr_ = "at what time?" — fixed interrogative time expression. _Das Meeting_ = neuter nominative subject.

---

199. Es beginnt um neun Uhr. Ich muss alles vorbereiten. 🇬🇧 It starts at nine o'clock. I have to prepare everything. → _Alles_ = **accusative neuter pronoun** ("everything") as direct object of _vorbereiten_. _Vorbereiten_ (separable) stays whole at end after modal.

---

200. Ich muss Daten organisieren und eine Präsentation vorbereiten. 🇬🇧 I have to organise data and prepare a presentation. → _Daten_ = plural of _das Datum_ (irregular plural: _Datum → Daten_). _Eine Präsentation_ = accusative feminine.

---

201. Sie dauert etwa vier Stunden. Es ist viel Arbeit. 🇬🇧 It takes about four hours. It's a lot of work. → _Vier Stunden_ = accusative time span (duration). _Viel Arbeit_ — _Arbeit_ is **feminine** (_die Arbeit_), _viel_ is uninflected before an uncountable singular noun.

---

202. Dann beginne heute Abend damit! Verschiebe es nicht. 🇬🇧 Then start with it tonight! Don't postpone it. → _Beginne … damit_ = **du-imperative** of _beginnen_ + da-compound _damit_. _Verschiebe es nicht_ = negated du-imperative (add _-e_ to _verschib-_ → _verschiebe_); _nicht_ at end.

---

203. Du hast recht. Ich beginne heute um acht Uhr damit. 🇬🇧 You're right. I'll start with it today at eight o'clock. → _Heute um acht Uhr_ — double time expression (general _heute_ + specific _um acht Uhr_); specific time follows general. _Damit_ refers back to the preparation task.

---

204. Gut. Und Freitag? Was erledigst du Freitag? 🇬🇧 Good. And Friday? What are you taking care of on Friday? → _Freitag_ used twice — first as topic marker, then as time adverb in sentence. Both without preposition or article (bare day name as adverb).

---

205. Freitag erledige ich alle kleinen Aufgaben. Dann ist das Wochenende frei! 🇬🇧 On Friday I'll take care of all the small tasks. Then the weekend is free! → _Alle kleinen Aufgaben_ = **accusative plural** (direct object). _Alle_ + adjective: _kleinen_ gets _-en_ ending (weak declension after _alle_, plural accusative).

---

206. Perfekt! Das ist ein guter Zeitplan, David. 🇬🇧 Perfect! That is a good plan, David. → _Ein guter Zeitplan_ — masculine nominative predicate: adjective _guter_ with _-er_ ending (strong declension after _ein_, masculine nominative).

---

207. Danke, Sophie. Du erinnerst mich immer an die wichtigen Dinge. 🇬🇧 Thank you, Sophie. You always remind me of the important things. → _Erinnern + an + accusative_: _an die wichtigen Dinge_ — _an_ + **accusative** (the things being reminded of). Adjective _wichtigen_ = _-en_ ending (weak, after definite article, plural accusative).

---

208. Das mache ich gern. Pünktlichkeit und Organisation sind meine Stärken. 🇬🇧 I'm happy to do that. Punctuality and organisation are my strengths. → _Meine Stärken_ — plural predicate nominative. _Meine_ = plural nominative of possessive (no gender distinction in plural). _Sind_ = plural verb agreeing with plural subject.

---

209. Ja, du bist sehr fleißig und organisiert. Ich lerne von dir! 🇬🇧 Yes, you are very hardworking and organised. I'm learning from you! → _Von dir_ — _von_ + **dative** → _dir_. The phrase _lernen von_ = "to learn from [a person]."

---

210. Das freut mich. Viel Erfolg diese Woche, David! 🇬🇧 That pleases me. Good luck this week, David! → _Das freut mich_ — _freuen_ (to please/delight) takes **accusative** of the person pleased: _mich_. Note: different from _sich freuen_ (to be happy, reflexive).

---

211. Danke dir! Dir auch, Sophie. Auf Wiedersehen! 🇬🇧 Thank you! Same to you, Sophie. Goodbye! → _Danke dir_ — _danken_ takes **dative** (_dir_ = dative of _du_). Contrast: _danke_ alone is fixed expression; _danken_ as verb always + dative.

---

212. Guten Abend, Max! Wann ist dein Feierabend heute? 🇬🇧 Good evening, Max! When is your end of work today? → _Dein Feierabend_ — masculine nominative (_der Feierabend_) → possessive _dein_ (no ending in masculine nominative predicate).

---

213. Guten Abend, Clara! Eigentlich um sechs Uhr. Aber ich bin noch beschäftigt. 🇬🇧 Good evening, Clara! Actually at six o'clock. But I'm still busy. → _Eigentlich_ (actually) is a **modal particle/adverb** indicating a contrast with reality. _Noch_ (still) = ongoing state.

---

214. Was erledigst du noch? 🇬🇧 What else are you still taking care of? → _Noch_ in this context = "still/else." The question asks what remaining tasks exist.

---

215. Ich muss noch einen Bericht beenden. Es dauert nicht lange. 🇬🇧 I still need to finish one more report. It won't take long. → _Einen Bericht_ = **accusative masculine** (_der Bericht_ → _einen Bericht_). _Noch einen_ = "one more." _Beenden_ has inseparable prefix _be-_ → no _ge-_ in participle (_beendet_).

---

216. Wie lange dauert es noch? 🇬🇧 How much longer will it take? → _Noch_ combined with _wie lange_ = "how much longer." _Es_ = neuter placeholder subject referring to the duration/task.

---

217. Vielleicht zwanzig Minuten. Dann bin ich fertig. 🇬🇧 Maybe twenty minutes. Then I'm done. → _Fertig sein_ = "to be done/finished" — **predicate adjective** _fertig_ (uninflected) after _sein_.

---

218. Okay, ich warte auf dich. Wir gehen zusammen nach Hause? 🇬🇧 OK, I'll wait for you. We're going home together? → _Warten auf_ + **accusative**: _auf dich_ (_auf_ + accusative for the person waited for). _Nach Hause_ = directional expression "home(ward)" — contrast with _zu Hause_ (at home, location).

---

219. Ja, gern. Danke, Clara. Bist du pünktlich hier? 🇬🇧 Yes, gladly. Thank you, Clara. Are you here on time? → _Gern_ after verb = "gladly/with pleasure." _Bist du_ = polar question (verb first).

---

220. Ja, ich bin immer pünktlich. Das ist meine Gewohnheit. 🇬🇧 Yes, I'm always punctual. That is my habit. → _Immer_ (always) = **frequency adverb** placed after the verb. _Meine Gewohnheit_ — feminine nominative predicate.

---

221. Das weiß ich. Du bist sehr regelmäßig und zuverlässig. 🇬🇧 I know that. You are very regular and reliable. → _Das weiß ich_ — object _das_ in position 1 → inversion: _weiß – ich_. _Zuverlässig_ = compound adjective (reliable), uninflected predicate.

---

222. Danke. Und du? War der heutige Alltag sehr stressig? 🇬🇧 Thank you. And you? Was today's daily routine very stressful? → _Der heutige Alltag_ — adjective _heutig-_ (of today) + _-e_ ending (weak, definite article, masculine nominative). **Präteritum** _war_ (was) — common for _sein_ and _haben_ even in spoken German.

---

223. Ja, sehr. Ich hatte viele Termine und viel Stress. 🇬🇧 Yes, very much. I had many appointments and a lot of stress. → **Präteritum** of _haben_: _hatte_ (had). Common for _haben_ in speech. _Viele Termine_ = accusative plural; _viel Stress_ = accusative masculine uncountable.

---

224. Das tut mir leid. Was war besonders stressig? 🇬🇧 I'm sorry to hear that. What was particularly stressful? → _Besonders_ (particularly/especially) = **intensifying adverb** modifying the adjective _stressig_. _War_ = Präteritum of _sein_.

---

225. Ein Termin wurde verschoben, und ich musste alles neu organisieren. 🇬🇧 An appointment was postponed and I had to reorganise everything. → **Passive voice (Präteritum)**: _wurde verschoben_ = _wurde_ (past of _werden_) + past participle. _Musste_ = Präteritum of _müssen_ (had to) — common for modals in past.

---

226. Oh nein! Das ist viel Arbeit. Hast du trotzdem Pause gemacht? 🇬🇧 Oh no! That's a lot of work. Did you still take a break? → _Pause gemacht_ — **Perfekt** of _Pause machen_: _gemacht_ is the participle. _Trotzdem_ (nevertheless/still) — concessive adverb.

---

227. Ja, einmal. Um Mittag habe ich kurz pausiert. Fünf Minuten nur. 🇬🇧 Yes, once. I took a brief break at noon. Only five minutes. → _Einmal_ (once) — numeral adverb. _Um Mittag_ in position 1 → inversion. _Nur_ (only) at end for emphasis — can be placed after the element it restricts.

---

228. Fünf Minuten! Das ist zu wenig. Du musst mehr entspannen. 🇬🇧 Five minutes! That's too little. You must relax more. → _Zu wenig_ = "too little" — _zu_ as "too" before adverb/adjective. _Mehr_ = comparative adverb "more" (modifying _entspannen_).

---

229. Ich weiß. Aber die Eile im Büro lässt keine Zeit. 🇬🇧 I know. But the rush at the office leaves no time. → _Lassen_ + **accusative** + bare infinitive: _lässt keine Zeit_ — here _lassen_ means "to allow/leave" (no infinitive following, just object). _Keine Zeit_ = accusative feminine negated (_kein_ + _-e_ for feminine accusative).

---

230. Das ist keine gute Gewohnheit, Max. Deine Gesundheit ist wichtig. 🇬🇧 That's not a good habit, Max. Your health is important. → _Keine gute Gewohnheit_ — **negation with _kein_** in predicate: _kein-_ + _-e_ (feminine nominative after _sein_). Adjective _gute_ = _-e_ (weak, after _keine_, feminine nominative).

---

231. Du hast recht. Ich vergesse manchmal, auf mich zu achten. 🇬🇧 You're right. I sometimes forget to take care of myself. → _Auf mich zu achten_ = **zu-infinitive phrase**: _achten auf_ + accusative → _auf mich_ (on myself). _Zu_ between _auf mich_ and _achten_ — infinitive clause object of _vergessen_.

---

232. Ich erinnere dich morgen an die Pause. Das verspreche ich dir. 🇬🇧 I'll remind you tomorrow about the break. I promise you that. → _Erinnern + an + accusative_: _an die Pause_ — _an_ + accusative. _Versprechen + dative_: _dir_ = dative (promise _to you_). _Das_ in position 1 → inversion: _verspreche – ich_.

---

233. Das ist sehr freundlich von dir. Danke, Clara. 🇬🇧 That is very kind of you. Thank you, Clara. → _Von dir_ — _von_ + **dative** → _dir_. The construction _freundlich von + dative_ = "kind of [person]" (fixed evaluative structure).

---

234. Kein Problem. So, bist du jetzt fertig mit dem Bericht? 🇬🇧 No problem. So, are you now done with the report? → _Fertig mit dem Bericht_ — _mit_ + **dative** → _dem Bericht_ (masculine dative). _Fertig mit_ = "done/finished with" + dative.

---

235. Ja! Ich beende ihn gerade. Noch eine Minute. 🇬🇧 Yes! I'm just finishing it. One more minute. → _Ihn_ = **accusative masculine pronoun** replacing _den Bericht_ (_der Bericht_ → accusative → _ihn_). _Noch eine Minute_ = "one more minute."

---

236. Gut. Dann gehen wir zusammen. Der Abend gehört uns! 🇬🇧 Good. Then let's go together. The evening belongs to us! → _Gehören + dative_: _uns_ = **dative of _wir_** (belongs _to us_). _Gehören_ always governs dative.

---

237. Ja! Feierabend ist die schönste Zeit des Tages. Gehen wir! 🇬🇧 Yes! End of work is the most beautiful time of the day. Let's go! → _Die schönste_ = **superlative** of _schön_ (schön → schönst- + _-e_ weak ending, definite article, feminine nominative). _Des Tages_ = **genitive masculine** (_der Tag_ → _des Tages_).

---

238. Tschüss, Büro! Auf Wiedersehen morgen! 🇬🇧 Bye, office! See you tomorrow! → _Büro_ in direct address = **vocative** (same as nominative in German — no special vocative case). Simply set off by comma.

---

239. Auf Wiedersehen! Und morgen — pünktlich, wie immer! 🇬🇧 Goodbye! And tomorrow — on time, as always! → Elliptical exclamation (verb omitted). _Wie immer_ = "as always" — fixed comparative phrase. _Pünktlich_ stands alone as a predicate adjective with implied _sein_.
