## Dialogue 1: Apartment Viewing

**Mrs. Müller:** Good day! You are Mr. Becker, correct? I am the landlady. **Mr. Becker:** Good day, Mrs. Müller. Yes, exactly. Thank you for making time. **Mrs. Müller:** No problem. The appointment was for 2 PM – you are very punctual! **Mr. Becker:** Punctuality is important to me. I am rarely late. **Mrs. Müller:** Very good. So, the apartment has three rooms – a bedroom, a living room, and a kitchen. **Mr. Becker:** Is the apartment furnished or unfurnished? **Mrs. Müller:** It is unfurnished. But the kitchen has a refrigerator, a stove, and a dishwasher. **Mr. Becker:** Nice. How high is the rent? **Mrs. Müller:** The rent is 850 euros per month. Utility costs are additional – about 150 euros. **Mr. Becker:** That is a bit expensive for me. Is there a discount if I rent longer? **Mrs. Müller:** Unfortunately not. But the price is very reasonable for this location, believe me. **Mr. Becker:** Hmm. How spacious is the bedroom? **Mrs. Müller:** It is 16 square meters – very spacious and bright. The window faces south. **Mr. Becker:** Wonderful. And the bathroom – does it have a bathtub or a shower? **Mrs. Müller:** It has both – a bathtub and a shower. Very practical. **Mr. Becker:** Is there an elevator? I don't like living on the fourth floor without one. **Mrs. Müller:** Yes, there is an elevator. And also a basement for trash and other things. **Mr. Becker:** When can one move in? **Mrs. Müller:** One can move in on the first of March. The rental contract begins automatically then. **Mr. Becker:** And the deposit – how high is it? **Mrs. Müller:** The deposit equals three months' rent. That is 2,550 euros. You can pay by bank transfer. **Mr. Becker:** That is a lot of money at once. Can I pay the deposit in two installments? **Mrs. Müller:** Unfortunately that is not possible. The landlord – meaning my husband – insists on a one-time payment. **Mr. Becker:** I understand. May I view the apartment again with my wife? **Mrs. Müller:** Of course! When does a second appointment suit you? **Mr. Becker:** Perhaps on Saturday? We have free time then and are both available. **Mrs. Müller:** Saturday at 11 o'clock – is that good? **Mr. Becker:** Perfect. I will enter the appointment into my schedule. Thank you very much! **Mrs. Müller:** You're welcome. Goodbye, Mr. Becker! **Mr. Becker:** Goodbye!

## Dialogue 4: Doctor Visit

**Doctor:** Good morning, Mrs. Hoffmann. How are you feeling today? **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Good morning, Doctor. I'm not doing well. I feel very weak and tired. **Doctor:** Since when have you been feeling this way? **Mrs. Hoffmann:** For three days. I also have severe headaches and a bad cough. **Doctor:** Do you have a fever? **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Yes, yesterday evening I had a fever of 38.5 degrees. The pain in my throat is also very uncomfortable. **Doctor:** I understand. I will examine you now. Please breathe in deeply... and out. Once more please. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** _(breathes)_ Is everything alright? **Doctor:** The lungs sound good. But I believe you have a severe cold, possibly a flu. Are you allergic to any medication? **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Yes, I am allergic to penicillin. **Doctor:** That is important. I will prescribe you other tablets – no penicillin-based medications. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** How often do I have to take the tablets? **Doctor:** Three times daily after eating. And drink plenty of water – that helps the body recover. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** How long will I be sick? **Doctor:** A cold normally lasts seven to ten days. With a flu it can last longer. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** That is long. I have important appointments at work next week. **Doctor:** You must recover – stress slows down healing. I will write you a sick note for one week. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Thank you, Doctor. Should I also exercise? I usually go running every day. **Doctor:** No, please don't. Rest and relax. You can exercise again when you are healthy. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Understood. And if I don't get better – should I come back? **Doctor:** Yes, please. If you still have a fever after five days or if the condition – meaning the throat – worsens, come back immediately. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Good. One more question – is the cold contagious? I live together with my family. **Doctor:** Yes, it is contagious. Wash your hands regularly and keep your distance from your family, especially from your mother and young children. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** I'll do that. Thank you for your help, Doctor. **Doctor:** You're welcome. Get well soon, Mrs. Hoffmann! Goodbye. **Mrs. Hoffmann:** Goodbye!
