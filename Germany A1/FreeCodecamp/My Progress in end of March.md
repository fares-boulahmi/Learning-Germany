## The Core Problem You Have

"Read then listen" is comprehension practice — it tells you if you _recognize_ a word, not if you _own_ it. Recognition and recall are completely different. You need a recall trigger built into your workflow, otherwise you'll feel like you know 300 words but freeze when you need them.

---

## When to Move On From a Word

Use the **3-strike recall rule**:

A word is "learned enough" when you can, on 3 separate occasions with time in between, produce it without looking. Not recognize it — produce it. The gap between the 3 occasions matters more than the number.

Practically, when you read a paragraph, cover it and ask yourself: _can I say what "verspätet" means without looking?_ That's strike 1. Next day, same test — strike 2. Two days later — strike 3. If it passes all three, move it out of focus.

If it fails at strike 2 or 3, it resets to zero.

---

## How to Measure With Your Current Paragraphs

After reading and listening, do this one extra step — takes 3 minutes:

Close the paragraph. Look at a list of only the **focus words** (your 50 daily / 300 weekly). For each word, say the meaning out loud or write it. No paragraph context, no help. Count how many you got without hesitation.

That number is your score. Track it. If you score 80% or above on the same word list across 3 sessions, that batch is done.

---

## The Context Window Question

1500 words will not break an AI prompt — that's actually small. Your full weekly 300-word memo plus all 5 paragraphs together is probably under 3000 tokens, well within any model's context. Don't worry about this at all.

---

## The Stacking System You Planned — Make It Concrete

Your instinct to stack daily → weekly is correct. Here's how to make it not collapse under pressure:

**Daily:** 50 focus words, short paragraph, one recall check at the end. Add passing words to a "known" list.

**Weekly:** 300 words = your 50 daily × 6 days (with one day being review only, no new words). The weekly paragraph pulls from the same pool so you see the words again in new context.

**The key rule:** never add new words until you've done the recall check on today's batch. New words are the reward for finishing the check, not the starting point.

**The minimum viable day is just the recall check — no reading, no listening, just 2 minutes of testing yourself on yesterday's words.** Everything else is bonus. This keeps the streak alive on bad days without guilt.

## What "1500 words for B1" Actually Means

It doesn't mean you memorize a list of 1500 words. It means when you encounter a word in a real sentence — reading, listening, speaking — you don't freeze. There's a big difference between:

- **Passive vocabulary** — you understand it when you see/hear it
- **Active vocabulary** — you can produce it when you need it

The Goethe B1 test needs both, but the writing and speaking sections will expose you fast if your active vocabulary is weak. The 1500 number is a rough passive threshold — the active number you actually need is closer to 600-800 words you can genuinely use.

---

## What 900 Words at A1 Probably Means

Honestly? It likely means 900 words _recognized_, not owned. Most people who say this have a large passive bank but a small active one. Don't chase that number — it's misleading and will give you false confidence.

---

## How to Test Yourself Alone — Concretely

There are three levels of self-testing, from easy to real:

**Level 1 — Recognition test (what you're doing now)** Read/listen and check if you understand. This is the floor, not the goal.

**Level 2 — Recall test (what you should add)** Look at a German word → say the meaning without help. Then flip it: look at the meaning → produce the German word. The second direction is much harder and much more valuable.

**Level 3 — Production test (the B1 standard)** Take a topic — your daily routine, your family, your job — and speak or write for 2 minutes using only words you know. Count how many of your focus words appear naturally. If they don't appear without thinking, you don't own them yet.

Level 3 once a week is your most honest progress check.

---

## How to Know You're on the Right Path

Ask yourself these four questions monthly:

1. Can I understand a simple German podcast or YouTube video without stopping every sentence?
2. Can I write 5 sentences about my day without looking anything up?
3. When I see a new word, do I remember it faster than I used to?
4. Do words from 2 weeks ago still feel natural today?

If the answer to all four is slowly moving toward yes, you're on the right path — regardless of what number you've counted.

---

## A Simple Tracking Method for Solo Study

Don't count total words. Instead, keep three lists:

- **Seeing it** — words you recognize when reading
- **Hearing it** — words you catch when listening
- **Using it** — words you've produced in a sentence without help

Only the third list counts toward your real vocabulary bank. When a word moves to that third list, that's a real word earned. Your goal isn't 1500 on a spreadsheet — it's growing that third list steadily every week.

The gap between your first list and your third list will tell you exactly where you are and what needs work.
