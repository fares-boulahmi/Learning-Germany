**Exercise 3.4 (full translation — the real test): 4/10.** This is where the problem becomes clear. You can identify and fill in blanks correctly, but when you must produce the sentence yourself, the rule breaks down:

|Sentence|Your answer|Error|Correct|
|---|---|---|---|
|I go **into** the kitchen|"in **der** Küche"|movement → must be accusative|"in **die** Küche"|
|She **puts** the book on the table|"auf **dem** Tisch"|legen = action = movement → accusative|"auf **den** Tisch"|

|My house is...|"Mein **hause**"|noun not capitalized|"Mein **Haus**"|

**The core pattern:** You know the rule intellectually but in free production, you default to dative even for movement verbs. Verbs like **gehen in**, **legen auf**, **hängen an**, **stellen neben** all describe movement or placement — they need accusative.
