## ⚠️ Your Active Error Targets This Set

Read these before every section. Every exercise contains traps for these.

- **Target 1 — stehen vs. stellen / liegen vs. legen / sitzen vs. setzen:** Position verbs (stehen, liegen) = DATIVE. Action/placement verbs (stellen, legen) = ACCUSATIVE. Furniture STANDS (steht) — it does not sit (sitzt).
- **Target 2 — Accusative plural noun form:** "die Stühle" (correct) NOT "die Stühlen" (wrong). Only dative plural adds -n to the noun: "den Stühlen".
- **Target 3 — Spelling: über / Sommer / Berge:** NOT "uber", NOT "Summer", NOT "Barge". These have been wrong for three sets.
- **Target 4 — Ordinal slippage in free writing:** After "am" → **ten/-sten**. After "Heute/Morgen/Gestern ist/war der" → **te/-ste**. Must be automatic now.
- **Target 5 — Separable verbs:** The prefix detaches and goes to the END of the sentence. "Ich stehe auf." NOT "Ich aufstehe." NOT "Ich stehe aufstehe."
- **Target 6 — Verb-second word order:** When a time phrase starts the sentence: TIME + VERB + SUBJECT. "Am Morgen **gehe** ich..." NOT "Am Morgen ich gehe..."

## Section 5: Verb Pairs — stehen/stellen and liegen/legen

> **THIS WAS YOUR BIGGEST ERROR IN SET 6B. Read carefully.**
> 
> |Verb|Meaning|Case required|Example|
> |---|---|---|---|
> |**stehen**|to be standing (position)|DATIVE|Der Stuhl **steht** neben **dem** Tisch.|
> |**stellen**|to place / stand up (action)|ACCUSATIVE|Ich **stelle** den Stuhl neben **den** Tisch.|
> |**liegen**|to be lying (position)|DATIVE|Das Buch **liegt** auf **dem** Tisch.|
> |**legen**|to lay / put down (action)|ACCUSATIVE|Ich **lege** das Buch auf **den** Tisch.|
> |**hängen** (intrans.)|to be hanging (position)|DATIVE|Das Bild **hängt** an **der** Wand.|
> |**hängen** (trans.)|to hang up (action)|ACCUSATIVE|Ich **hänge** das Bild an **die** Wand.|
> 
> **CRITICAL:** Furniture STANDS (steht) — it does NOT sit (sitzt). "Der Kleiderschrank sitzt" = WRONG. "Der Kleiderschrank **steht**" = CORRECT.

## Section 6: Accusative Plural — Noun Form Drill

> **YOUR ERROR FROM SET 6B:** You wrote "die Stühlen" (wrong). In accusative plural, the noun does NOT change — it stays as "die Stühle". Only in dative plural does the noun add -n: "den Stühlen".
> 
> |Case|Article|Noun|Example|
> |---|---|---|---|
> |Nominative plural|die|Stühle|Die Stühle sind alt.|
> |Accusative plural|die|Stühle (NO change)|Ich stelle die Stühle neben den Tisch.|
> |Dative plural|den|Stühlen (adds -n)|Der Ball liegt zwischen den Stühlen.|
