## ⚠️ Your Six Persistent Error Targets This Set

Every section contains traps for these. Read them before you start.

- **Target 1 — Ordinal endings in date sentences:** After "am" → add **ten/-sten**. After "Heute/Morgen/Gestern ist der" → also **te/-ste**. NEVER "dreißig Juni" or "einunddreißig Mai" — always "dreißigsten" / "einunddreißigsten"
- **Target 2 — Missing verb "bin":** "Ich ziemlich ruhig" is NOT a sentence. **Ich BIN ziemlich ruhig.** Always.
- **Target 3 — Article gender for rooms:** das Wohnzimmer, das Schlafzimmer, das Badezimmer, das Esszimmer, die Küche — "einen Badezimmer" is WRONG. Neuter rooms → **ein** (nominative) / **ein** (accusative) / **dem** (dative)
- **Target 4 — Predicate adjective endings in free writing:** After sein, NEVER add endings. Not "sehr gute", not "Modern" (capitals mid-sentence for adjectives is also wrong)
- **Target 5 — "im Voraus" NOT "in Voraus"** — always **im** (= in dem)
- **Target 6 — Dative case after prepositions:** aus/bei/mit/nach/seit/zu/von ALWAYS trigger dative. No exceptions.

## Section 3: Dual-Case Prepositions — Dative vs Accusative

> **THE KEY RULE:**
> 
> - **Dative** → describes **location / position** (where something IS) — ask "Wo?"
> - **Accusative** → describes **movement into** a location (where something is GOING) — ask "Wohin?"
> 
> Prepositions: zwischen · an · in · unter · neben · auf · über · vor · hinter
