### ![⚠️](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAP///wAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==) Your Active Error Targets This Set

Read these before every section. Every exercise contains traps for these patterns.

Target 1 — er/sie/es conjugation: The third-person singular ending is t (er wacht, sie stellt, es liegt), NOT -e. "ich wache" but "sie wacht". "ich stelle" but "er stellt". This was wrong 4+ times in Set 7.

Target 2 — du verb forms: The du form adds st to the stem: du machst, du stehst, du verlässt, du wachst. NOT "macht du" or "steht du". Irregular stems keep their vowel change: du siehst (not "sehst"), du verlässt (not "verlasst").

Target 3 — stellen vs. legen: Upright objects (chairs, wardrobes, bottles) use stellen. Flat objects (books, papers, sleeping cats) use legen. Position = stehen/liegen (dative). Action = stellen/legen (accusative).

Target 4 — halb + clock hours only: "halb" ONLY works with hours 1–12. "halb dreizehn" and "halb zwanzig" do NOT exist. 12:30 = halb eins (halfway to one). 7:30 PM = halb acht (not halb zwanzig).

Target 5 — Noun capitalization: Am Morgen, Zu Mittag, Am Abend, Am Nachmittag. These were written lowercase 4+ times in Set 7. ALL nouns are capitalized.

Target 6 — Modal verb word order: Modal + infinitive. The modal conjugates and goes to position 2. The infinitive goes to the END. "Ich kann Deutsch sprechen." NOT "Ich kann sprechen Deutsch."

Target 7 — Real-world sense check: Before finishing any sentence in free writing, ask: "Does this make sense in real life?" No more "Ich stelle die Katze über den Tisch" (I place the cat above the table).
