# Shopping and Errands – A Perfectly Normal Day

---

## Paragraph 1 – Planning Begins

Today is Saturday, and I **begin** my day with a long shopping list. I **get up** early, because the errands always **take** a long time. My wife **reminds** me: "Don't forget the bread!" I often **forget** something, so I **plan** everything carefully. The **schedule** for today is full – we **have to** go to the supermarket, the pharmacy, and the bank. I **take** my shopping bag **with me** and **leave** the house at nine o'clock. The street is already **busy**, because many people have the same idea. Shopping on Saturday is **usually** stressful, but today I am **on time**.

---

## Paragraph 2 – On the Way by Bus

I **get on** the bus at the stop. The bus **departs** at half past nine – I am just in time. Next to me sits an old woman with a large, **colorful** shopping basket. She **tells** me that she goes shopping every Tuesday and Friday. "I also **visit** the market," she says kindly. At the next stop, three children **get off** – they **laugh** loudly. I **transfer** at the supermarket and **wait** two minutes for the connecting line. The **delay** is small, but I **hurry** anyway.

---

## Paragraph 3 – In the Supermarket

I **enter** the large supermarket and **take** a shopping cart. The shelves are **full** – there are red apples, yellow bananas, and green vegetables. I **look for** the refrigerated section and **find** the milk quickly. A sales clerk **explains** to me where the bread is – he is very **friendly**. I also **receive** a small flyer with this week's deals. My list **consists** of twelve items, but I also **discover** new products. "May I **try** this?" I ask at a stand. "Yes, of course!" **answers** the saleswoman. In the end I **pay** thirty-five euros – that is **usually** this much.

---

## Paragraph 4 – A Problem While Shopping

Suddenly I **notice**: I have **forgotten** my wallet! I **don't understand** how that **can** happen. I **call** my wife – luckily she is at home. "Can you **bring** me the wallet?" I ask. She **comes by** in twenty minutes – I **wait** patiently. I **use** the time to **compare** prices. A bottle of orange juice **costs** two euros, a large one **costs** three euros fifty. The waiting **doesn't take** long, and I am very **grateful** to her.

---

## Paragraph 5 – At the Checkout

My wife **brings** the wallet **with her** – great! I **place** the products on the belt and **wait** at the checkout. The cashier **scans** everything quickly and **explains** the new loyalty program to me. "You **can collect** points – I **like** that," I say. I **ask**: "May I **have** a bag?" – "Of course, it **costs** thirty cents." I **pay** by card and **receive** a receipt. The cashier is **friendly** and **wishes** me a nice day. "Goodbye!" I say and **leave** the store.

---

## Paragraph 6 – More Errands

After the supermarket, I **go** to the pharmacy next door. I **have to pick up** tablets for my father – he is sick. The pharmacist **recommends** a good medicine for coughs. "How often **does** he **have to take** the tablets?" I ask. "Three times a day," he **explains calmly** and **clearly**. I **understand** the instructions well and **thank** him. The pharmacy is **small**, but very **tidy** and **friendly** organized. I **leave** the pharmacy with the medicine and **continue** on my way.

---

## Paragraph 7 – The Weekly Market

Every Saturday the weekly market **takes place** in the town center. I **visit** it occasionally, because the vegetables there are **fresh**. A farmer **sells** colorful tomatoes – red, yellow, and even purple tomatoes! "How much **does** a kilogram **cost**?" I **ask**. "Two euros fifty," he **answers** and **recommends** the red ones to me. I **shop** and **enjoy** the lively market. A small child accidentally **tears** a bag – oranges **fall** everywhere. The mother **hurries** and **picks** the oranges **up** quickly.

---

## Paragraph 8 – A Short Break

After so many errands, I **need** a break. I **go** into a small café and **sit down** at a **quiet** table. "Good day! What **can** I get you?" **asks** the waiter **kindly**. I **order** a coffee and a piece of cake – I **deserve** it! I **relax** and **enjoy** the **free** time. At the next table, a man **tells** his wife about a **forgotten** appointment. "I **forgot** the doctor's appointment!" he says. His wife **misunderstands** him at first, but then both **laugh**.

---

## Paragraph 9 – Back Home

After the break, I **take** the bus **back**. I **get on** and **find** a free seat – today I am **lucky**. My bags are **full** and **heavy**, but I am **satisfied**. At home I **tidy** everything **up** – the fridge, the kitchen, and the cupboard. The washing machine is already **running** – my wife **turned** it **on**. I **put** the bread on the table and **call out**: "I'm back!" My son **comes** right out of the living room – he is very **hungry**. We **prepare** lunch **together**.

---

## Paragraph 10 – The Weekend Ends Well

In the afternoon we **relax** after the errands. My daughter **helps** me with the **tidying up** – she is very **hardworking**. We **don't forget** to **plan** the shopping list for next week. "We **have to** go to the bank next Monday," says my wife. "I **can take care of** that," I **answer**. I **like to spend** the weekend with my family – that is our **habit**. **Daily life** is sometimes **stressful**, but in the end I **like** our life. We **laugh** together and **look forward** to next Saturday.
