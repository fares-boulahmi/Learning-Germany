# Health & Well-being

---

My name is Anna, and I live in Germany. I am thirty years old and work as a nurse in a large hospital. Every morning I wake up at six o'clock and get up quickly because I want to be on time. I feel fit and strong when I sleep regularly. Sometimes I forget to drink enough water, but I know that it is important. My body needs plenty of water and healthy food every day. I enjoy eating fruits and vegetables because they are good for your health. Without a good routine, I feel tired and weak.

---

This morning I hear that my sister is sick. She has a fever and a severe headache — she sleeps all day. I call her and ask: "How are you?" She says: "I'm not doing well, I have a cold." I visit her in the afternoon and bring her medicine and tablets. She must drink a lot and rest, the doctor says. The doctor has prescribed her a medication that she should take three times a day. I make her a warm soup, because food and warmth help with recovery. My sister is usually very hardworking, but now she must relax.

---

On Wednesday I go to the doctor because I have been coughing and sneezing for a week. The doctor examines me thoroughly and asks: "Are you in pain?" I answer: "Yes, I have a sore throat and feel weak." He treats me and prescribes me a tablet for the flu. I take the medication regularly and drink plenty of tea. After three days I feel better — my body begins to heal. It is important not to forget a doctor's appointment, especially when you have a chronic illness. I always remind myself to take my health seriously.

---

For my free time, I look for activities that are good for the body and the soul. I enjoy reading books about health and learning a lot about healthy habits. Twice a week I ride my bicycle through the park — it is fun and keeps me fit. Exercise without stress is very important for well-being. I find that movement and fresh air can fight chronic fatigue. On weekends I cook healthy meals and sometimes bake bread without sugar. My family loves eating together and trying new recipes. We all know: a healthy everyday life begins with small, regular habits.

---

After work I relax and listen to calm music. I write in my diary what I did today and how I feel. Sometimes I shiver from the cold in winter, so I wear warm clothing and drink hot tea. My grandfather always says: "Without health, you have nothing." I love this saying because it is so true. Before I sleep, I brush my teeth and wash my face — that is part of my daily routine. I need at least eight hours of sleep to feel strong and fit. Good night — tomorrow a new, healthy day begins!
