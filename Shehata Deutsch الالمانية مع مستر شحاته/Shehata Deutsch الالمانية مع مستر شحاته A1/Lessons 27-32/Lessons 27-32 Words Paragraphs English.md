# German Everyday Stories — English Version

---

## Paragraph 1 – The House and the Apartment

I live in a large house in Germany. The house has a beautiful kitchen, a bright living room, and three bedrooms. In the kitchen, an old refrigerator stands next to the stove. Every Monday I buy fruit and milk for the family. Today I'm also buying chocolate and tea at the supermarket. My mother always buys fresh fruit, but she never buys cigarettes. My father has a small office in the house where he works. The office is next to the living room and has a large window. The lamp above the desk is broken — I need to buy a new one. A colorful rug lies on the sofa, and a small table stands next to it. I find the living room very beautiful and peaceful. My sister thinks the apartment is too small, but I find it cozy. We also have a small garden behind the house. Many flowers grow in the garden — red, yellow, and blue ones. My brother works in the garden every Saturday. He works diligently and the garden is always clean. The children play all over the garden and also in the living room. I have a dog, and the dog always sleeps on the sofa. The dog is brown and small, but it is loud. I give the dog food every morning. The bathroom has a shower and a bathtub. The shower is broken, but the faucet works well. I shower every morning at seven o'clock. After the shower, I eat breakfast in the kitchen. I drink tea and eat bread for breakfast. My mother drinks coffee, but I prefer tea. The kitchen is bright and cheerful. Many pictures hang on the walls in the kitchen. Next to the sink stand the washing machine and the dishwasher. I am happy with my house.

## Paragraph 2 – The Morning and Daily Life

I wake up every morning at six o'clock. Then I get up and go to the bathroom. I brush my teeth and shower quickly. Afterwards I go to the kitchen and have breakfast. Today I'm having bread with jam and drinking milk. My father always eats more than me for breakfast — he eats bread, cheese, and drinks two cups of coffee. My daughter is young and still sleeping when I have breakfast. She always sleeps late in the morning. I leave the house at seven o'clock. Today I'm leaving five minutes earlier because I have an important appointment. My brother leaves the house at the same time as me. We drive together by car to work. He drives fast, but I prefer to drive slowly. I work in an office in the city. I work every day from eight o'clock until five o'clock. Sometimes I also work on Saturdays. My mother works in a hospital as a nurse. She works diligently and the patients like her very much. My uncle is a teacher and he works in a school. The school is large and has many rooms. At twelve o'clock I have lunch at the restaurant next to the office. I often eat salad or soup for lunch. Sometimes I drink tea after the meal. I go for a short walk after lunch because walking is good for your health. At five o'clock I come home. Then I watch a little television or read a book. I always read in the evenings — I enjoy reading novels and newspapers. My son also likes to read, but he prefers books about animals. In the evenings we eat together as a family. We eat at seven o'clock. After dinner we talk about the day.

## Paragraph 3 – The Family

My family is large and friendly. There are eight people in the family. My father's name is Klaus and he is fifty years old. He is tall and slim, with brown eyes and gray hair. My mother's name is Anna and she is forty-eight years old. She is kind and hardworking. I have two brothers and a sister. My older brother is married and has two children. His children are young and funny. My sister is single and studies at the university. She is very intelligent and diligent. My grandmother also lives with us. She is old but very active. She works in the garden and cooks for the family. My grandfather is also with us, but he is ill and sleeps a lot. We have a dog, and the children always play with it. The dog's name is Max and he is black and large. My father bought the dog two years ago. He bought the dog as a gift for the children. The children were very happy about the dog. My aunt sometimes comes to visit. She lives in another city but comes every month. My uncle works as a computer scientist and has a large car. He always drives the car fast. We celebrate birthdays and other holidays together. On my birthday, all family members come. My mother cooks a lot — she makes soup, meat, and vegetables. My sister always brings chocolate and sweets. The children eat many sweets and become very loud. But that's okay, because family is the most important thing. I am happy in my family.

## Paragraph 4 – Work

I am a computer scientist and work at a large company. The company is in the city center. Every morning I take the train to work. The train comes at seven thirty. Sometimes the train is late and I wait at the station. I don't like waiting, but it's part of everyday life. At the office I have many colleagues. My boss's name is Mr. Müller and he is friendly, but sometimes nervous. He works a lot and doesn't sleep enough. My colleague Lena is also a computer scientist. She is smart and creative. We often work together on projects. I write many emails and read many documents on the computer. Sometimes I write reports for the boss. The boss reads the reports and gives me feedback. During the lunch break I eat at the café next to the office. The café has good food and friendly waiters. I always drink tea on my break. Sometimes I drink coffee when I'm tired. At three o'clock we often have a meeting. In the meeting we talk about the projects and the work. I speak German and English in the office. Some colleagues also speak French. I understand French a little, but I don't speak it well. After work I sometimes go to the supermarket. I buy food for dinner. Today I'm buying vegetables, bread, and milk. I also buy fruit for the children. The supermarket is large and has everything. I always find what I'm looking for. After shopping I drive home.

## Paragraph 5 – Shopping

Today is Saturday and I'm going shopping. I always go into the city on Saturdays. I buy clothes, groceries, and sometimes gifts. First I go to the supermarket. At the supermarket I buy milk, bread, fruit, and chocolate. I also buy tea and coffee. My mother gave me a list and I buy everything on it. The list has twenty items. I find everything in the supermarket without any problems. At the checkout I pay by card. Then I go to a clothing store. I'm looking for new trousers and a coat. I find a stylish pair of blue trousers and a black coat. I buy the trousers, but I don't buy the coat because it is too expensive. I'm waiting for a sale. Afterwards I go to a bakery. The bakery sells fresh bread and rolls. I buy six rolls for tomorrow's breakfast. The baker is friendly and gives me one roll for free. I eat the roll right away — it's delicious! My daughter is waiting for me at home. She's waiting for the chocolate I'm buying. I always buy her chocolate when I go into the city. My son is waiting for new pens for school. I buy him blue and black pens. At a bookstore I also buy a book for my father. My father loves to read — he always reads books about history. I find an interesting book about the history of Germany. It costs twelve euros. Then I go home with many bags. The bags are heavy, but I'm satisfied.

## Paragraph 6 – Hobbies and Free Time

In my free time I have many hobbies. I play guitar and enjoy singing. I have been playing guitar for five years. Sometimes I play the guitar for my family. The family enjoys listening when I play. My brother also plays guitar, but he doesn't play as well as I do. We sometimes play together and sing old songs. That's a lot of fun! I also enjoy going to the cinema. At the cinema I enjoy watching adventure films and comedies. Tonight I'm going to the cinema with my girlfriend. We're watching a new film. My girlfriend prefers romance films, but today we're watching an adventure film together. I also love reading. I read books about nature, history, and travel. My favorite book is a novel about adventures in Africa. I have read the book three times. My father also reads a lot — he reads newspapers and books about history. Sometimes we dance at home. My sister dances very well. She has been dancing for ten years and goes to a dance class every week. I don't dance as well, but I still enjoy dancing. On weekends I sometimes go hiking. I hike in the mountains or in the forest. Nature is beautiful and the air is fresh. My dog always comes along on the hike. The dog runs everywhere and is always happy in nature. I take many photos with my camera. The camera is new and takes very good pictures. I photograph flowers, animals, and landscapes. I find photography very creative.

## Paragraph 7 – The Weather and the Seasons

In Germany there are four seasons: spring, summer, autumn, and winter. In winter it is cold and sometimes it snows. I don't like winter very much because it is so cold. My children love winter because they can play in the snow. In winter I wear a warm coat and boots. My coat is black and very warm. I buy the coat in autumn before winter comes. In spring it gets warmer and the flowers bloom. Spring is my favorite season. In spring I often go to the park and sit on a bench. The sun shines and the birds sing. In summer it is hot. In summer we often go on vacation. We sometimes go to the sea or to the mountains. The weather is nice and we swim a lot. The children are very happy in summer because they have no school. In autumn the leaves fall from the trees. Autumn is colorful — the leaves are red, yellow, and orange. I take many photos in autumn because nature is so beautiful. Today the weather is not good. It is raining and the wind is strong. I leave the house with an umbrella. My umbrella is blue and large. I find my umbrella very practical. Tomorrow the weather is supposed to be better — the sun should shine. I'm waiting for the good weather because I want to go into the garden. My father listens to the weather forecast on the radio every morning. He plans the day according to the weather.

## Paragraph 8 – Traveling

I love to travel. I have already been to many countries. I have been to Turkey, Austria, Switzerland, and Tunisia. Tunisia is very beautiful — the weather is warm and the people are friendly. In Tunisia I ate a lot of fruit and drank a lot of tea. I also took many photos. My camera was always with me. I enjoy traveling by train because I can look out the window. The train is slower than the plane, but I find it more comfortable. Sometimes I also travel by car when the distance is not too far. When I travel, I always pack a large suitcase. In the suitcase I have clothes, shoes, my camera, and a book. I never forget my book because I always read on the train or on the plane. In the hotel you sleep well, but sometimes the hotel is run-down or old. I always look for a friendly hotel with a good breakfast. At the hotel breakfast I drink tea and eat rolls. When I'm in a new city, I first go to the museum. I find museums very interesting. I learn a lot about the history and culture of the city. Then I walk through the old town and look at the old buildings. I sometimes buy souvenirs for my family. I always buy my mother something nice. For my father I often buy a book about the country. For the children I buy sweets and small toys. Traveling makes me happy and I always learn a lot.

## Paragraph 9 – School and Learning

My children go to school. My daughter is ten years old and is in the fourth grade. My son is thirteen years old and is in the seventh grade. School starts at eight o'clock. The children get up early and have breakfast quickly. Then they leave the house at seven forty-five. They take the bus to school. At school they learn German, mathematics, English, and other subjects. My daughter really likes mathematics. She finds it interesting and easy. My son prefers English. He already speaks English well and reads English books. I sometimes help the children with their homework. I understand mathematics well, but my wife helps more with English. The teachers at school are hardworking and friendly. My daughter's teacher's name is Mr. Wagner. He is young and very kind. My son's teacher's name is Mrs. Klein. She is strict, but fair. After school the children come home at one o'clock. They eat lunch and then do their homework. The homework sometimes takes two hours. After the homework they are allowed to play or watch television. On Saturdays the children have no school. They sleep in and play all day. My son plays football with his friends. My daughter plays with her dolls or reads books. I find education very important and I always buy the children good books. We also sometimes go to the museum together so the children can learn more.

---

## Paragraph 10 – The Evening and the Night

Evening is my favorite time. After work and dinner the family is together. We sit in the living room and talk about the day. Sometimes we watch television. We watch the news or a film. The children like to watch cartoons, but I prefer documentaries about nature and animals. My wife always reads in the evenings — she enjoys reading novels. I sometimes play guitar after dinner. The family listens and sometimes we all sing together. That makes us all happy. My father sometimes comes to visit in the evening. He drinks tea with us and tells stories. He enjoys telling stories from his youth. The children always listen attentively. At eight o'clock the children go to bed. I say "Good night" to them and they fall asleep quickly. The dog also sleeps — he lies on the rug in the living room. At nine or ten o'clock I also go to bed. Before that I brush my teeth and sometimes shower again. In bed I read for another ten or twenty minutes. Then I fall asleep. I sleep well because I was active all day. Tomorrow is a new day with new tasks. Maybe tomorrow I'll ride my bicycle to work if the weather is good. Or I'll take the train. I don't know yet. Life is beautiful when you work hard, love your family, and enjoy the little things. I am grateful for my life and my family. Good night!
